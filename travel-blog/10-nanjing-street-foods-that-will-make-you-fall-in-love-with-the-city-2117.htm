10 Nanjing Street Foods That Will Make You Fall in Love with the City

  • Home
  • Travel Blog
  • Blog Details

Nanjing, the ancient capital of China, is a city where history and modernity blend seamlessly. But beyond its stunning landmarks like the Confucius Temple and Sun Yat-sen Mausoleum, there’s another reason to fall in love with this city—its incredible street food. From savory snacks to sweet treats, Nanjing’s culinary scene is a feast for the senses. Here are 10 must-try street foods that will leave you craving more.

1. Duck Blood Vermicelli Soup (鸭血粉丝汤, Yā Xuě Fěn Sī Tāng)

A Nanjing Classic

No visit to Nanjing is complete without trying Duck Blood Vermicelli Soup. This iconic dish features tender duck blood cubes, chewy vermicelli noodles, and a rich, savory broth infused with duck bones and spices. Topped with cilantro and chili oil, it’s a comforting bowl of warmth that locals swear by.

Where to Try It

Head to Laomendong (Old Gate East), where small stalls serve steaming bowls of this delicacy. The best spots often have long lines, but trust us—it’s worth the wait.

2. Salted Duck (盐水鸭, Yán Shuǐ Yā)

Nanjing’s Signature Dish

Salted Duck is Nanjing’s most famous export, with a history dating back over 1,500 years. The duck is brined in a mixture of salt, Sichuan peppercorns, and spices, then slow-cooked to perfection. The result? Tender, flavorful meat with a subtle saltiness that keeps you coming back for more.

Best Places to Taste It

Look for vendors near Confucius Temple or visit Zhujiang Road, where specialty shops sell vacuum-packed ducks to take home.

3. Pan-Fried Dumplings (锅贴, Guō Tiē)

Crispy, Juicy, and Addictive

These golden-brown dumplings are pan-fried to crispy perfection while retaining a juicy filling—usually pork or beef. The contrast between the crunchy bottom and soft top makes them irresistible.

Where to Find Them

Liu Chang Xing Dumpling House near Xinjiekou is a local favorite. Pair them with a bowl of hot soy milk for the ultimate breakfast.

4. Sweet Osmanthus Cake (桂花糕, Guì Huā Gāo)

A Fragrant Delight

Made with glutinous rice flour and infused with sweet osmanthus flowers, this soft, chewy cake is a beloved Nanjing dessert. The floral aroma and delicate sweetness make it a perfect snack any time of day.

Best Spots to Try

Street vendors around Xuanwu Lake often sell freshly steamed versions. Grab one while strolling along the scenic lakeside.

5. Stinky Tofu (臭豆腐, Chòu Dòu Fu)

Love It or Hate It

Yes, it smells strong, but Nanjing’s version of stinky tofu is milder than others in China. Fermented tofu is deep-fried until crispy and served with a spicy or sweet sauce. The bold flavor is surprisingly addictive.

Where to Brave It

The night market near Fuzimiao (Confucius Temple) is the best place to try this polarizing snack.

6. Sesame Pancakes (烧饼, Shāo Bǐng)

Crunchy, Flaky Goodness

These baked or fried pancakes are stuffed with scallions, sesame seeds, and sometimes minced meat. Crispy on the outside and soft inside, they’re a popular grab-and-go snack.

Top Picks

Look for vendors near Nanjing University, where students flock for an affordable and filling bite.

7. Tangbao (Soup Dumplings, 汤包, Tāng Bāo)

Bursting with Flavor

Unlike Shanghai’s xiaolongbao, Nanjing’s tangbao are larger and filled with a rich, gelatinized broth that melts into liquid when steamed. Bite carefully to avoid spills!

Best Eateries

Jinling Dumpling House is legendary for its juicy, perfectly wrapped tangbao.

8. Fried Glutinous Rice Balls (糖油粑粑, Táng Yóu Bā Ba)

Sweet and Chewy

These deep-fried glutinous rice balls are coated in caramelized sugar, creating a crispy shell with a soft, sticky center. A must-try for dessert lovers.

Where to Indulge

Night markets like Shiziqiao serve them fresh and piping hot.

9. Beef Potstickers (牛肉锅贴, Niú Ròu Guō Tiē)

A Meaty Twist

Similar to pan-fried dumplings but stuffed with spiced beef, these potstickers are crispy, juicy, and packed with umami flavor.

Local Hotspots

Jiang’s Beef Potstickers near Laomendong is a cult favorite.

10. Lotus Root Stuffed with Sticky Rice (糯米藕, Nuò Mǐ Ǒu)

A Sweet Surprise

Lotus root is stuffed with sweet glutinous rice, steamed, and drizzled with osmanthus syrup. The combination of textures—crunchy lotus and chewy rice—is divine.

Best to Enjoy

Teahouses around Qinhuai River often serve this as a dessert.

Nanjing’s street food is more than just sustenance—it’s a cultural experience. Whether you’re a foodie or a casual traveler, these flavors will make you fall in love with the city. So grab a bite, explore the streets, and let your taste buds lead the way!
