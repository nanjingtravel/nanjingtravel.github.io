15 Instagrammable Places in Nanjing for Autumn Colors

  • Home
  • Travel Blog
  • Blog Details

There's a certain magic that descends upon Nanjing when autumn arrives. The city, steeped in centuries of history as a former capital, undergoes a breathtaking transformation. The oppressive summer humidity gives way to crisp, cool air, and the sprawling canopy of ginkgo and maple trees ignites in a spectacular display of gold, crimson, and amber. For photographers, influencers, and anyone with a penchant for beauty, autumn in Nanjing is not just a season; it's a photoshoot waiting to happen. This is your ultimate guide to capturing the most stunning, Instagram-worthy autumn scenes the city has to offer.

The Golden Canopy: Ginkgo Biloba Paradises

When you think of autumn in Nanjing, the first image that often comes to mind is the brilliant yellow of the ginkgo biloba tree. The city is famous for them, and when their fan-shaped leaves turn and fall, they create landscapes that feel almost otherworldly.

1. Dr. Sun Yat-sen's Mausoleum

The approach to the final resting place of the founding father of modern China is an architectural marvel in itself, but in autumn, it becomes a golden pathway. The long, wide stairway leading up to the memorial hall is flanked by towering ginkgo trees. As you ascend the 392 steps, you're walking through a tunnel of shimmering gold. The contrast of the vibrant yellow leaves against the iconic blue-glazed tile of the mausoleum's roof is a photographer's dream. For the best shot, go on a weekday morning to avoid the crowds and capture the sunlight filtering through the golden leaves.

2. Nanjing University (Gulou Campus)

Step into the hallowed grounds of one of China's oldest and most prestigious universities, and you'll find an atmosphere of scholarly tranquility amplified by autumn's touch. The old campus is dotted with magnificent ginkgo trees, some of which are hundreds of years old. The most famous spot is near the North Garden, where a carpet of golden ginkgo leaves covers the lawn and the pathways. The combination of the red-brick, ivy-clad academic buildings from the Republican era and the bright yellow leaves creates a profoundly nostalgic and picturesque scene. It’s a location that whispers history and screams aesthetic perfection.

3. Ming Xiaoling Mausoleum

The Sacred Way of the Ming Xiaoling Mausoleum, a UNESCO World Heritage site, is arguably the most iconic autumn spot in all of Nanjing. The stone statues of elephants, camels, and lions silently guard a path that becomes a breathtaking golden corridor in late November. The ancient ginkgo and maple trees lining the path create a dense, colorful canopy overhead. Walking this path feels like traveling back in time. Capture the long, winding perspective of the road blanketed in leaves, using the majestic stone creatures as powerful foreground elements in your composition.

4. Qixia Mountain

While Qixia Mountain is famous for its thousand-year-old temple and rock carvings, it's also a premier destination for fall foliage. The entire mountain erupts in color, with a stunning mix of ginkgo and maple trees. The Qixia Temple itself becomes a focal point, with its traditional Buddhist architecture framed by fiery red and warm yellow leaves. Don't miss the ancient ginkgo tree within the temple complex; it's a majestic giant that turns a spectacular shade of gold. The view from the mountain looking down over the temple roofs peeking through the colorful forest is a panoramic shot worth the hike.

Crimson Dreams: Maple Leaves & Scarlet Canopies

Beyond the gold of the ginkgo, Nanjing offers deep, passionate hues of red that set the autumn landscape ablaze.

5. Xuanwu Lake Park

Nanjing's largest inner-city lake is a beauty in every season, but autumn provides a unique charm. The willows may be turning yellow, but it's the maple trees scattered around the five islets that truly captivate. The best views are from the lakeshore paths, where you can frame the iconic Zifeng Tower and the Nanjing skyline with foregrounds of rich crimson and orange foliage. Rent a paddle boat and get a shot from the water, with the colorful trees of the islands reflecting perfectly on the lake's calm surface. The combination of modern architecture and natural beauty is quintessential Nanjing.

6. The City Wall (near Zhonghua Gate)

For a truly unique perspective on autumn, walk a section of the 600-year-old Nanjing City Wall. The area near the massive Zhonghua Gate fortress is particularly scenic. Here, ancient maple trees grow alongside the formidable wall, their red leaves creating a stunning juxtaposition against the grey, weathered bricks. You can capture a shot of the winding wall disappearing into a tunnel of fiery red, a powerful image that blends nature's delicate beauty with mankind's enduring strength.

7. Mochou Lake Park

Steeped in the legend of the sorrowful maiden Mochou, this park is a classic Chinese garden that becomes exceptionally romantic in the fall. The maple trees around the lake, pavilions, and winding corridors are carefully curated to provide picture-perfect views at every turn. The reflection of the red leaves and the white-walled, black-tiled buildings in the still lake waters is a classic shot. It’s a more intimate and serene autumn experience compared to the grander locales.

Urban Oases: Parks & Boulevards

You don't always have to venture far to find stunning autumn colors in Nanjing. The city itself is a vast park, with colors spilling onto its streets and into its everyday life.

8. Beijing Dong Road

This is perhaps the most famous "ginkgo road" in the city. For a few weeks in autumn, the entire street is transformed into a golden wonderland. The tall ginkgo trees on both sides of the road form a continuous golden arch. While it's a busy thoroughfare, the local government often restricts traffic during peak foliage season, allowing pedestrians to flood the street and take photos. The energy here is infectious, with everyone from professional photographers to couples taking wedding photos vying for the perfect spot. It’s urban autumn at its most vibrant.

9. Presidential Palace

The Presidential Palace is a living museum of modern Chinese history, and its beautifully landscaped gardens are a hidden gem for autumn colors. Stroll through the West Garden where pavilions, ponds, and rockeries are adorned with colorful maple and ginkgo trees. The historical significance of the location adds a layer of depth to your photos. Imagine the figures of the past who walked these same paths under these same brilliant trees.

10. Nanjing Normal University (Xianlin Campus)

While the Gulou campus of Nanjing University gets most of the attention, the newer Xianlin Campus of Nanjing Normal University is a modern architectural marvel that also boasts incredible autumn foliage. The wide, open spaces and contemporary buildings are framed by meticulously planted trees that turn brilliant shades of red and yellow. The reflection of the fall colors in the campus's large ponds and lakes, with sleek university buildings in the background, offers a more modern and minimalist aesthetic for your feed.

Serene Escapes: Temples & Hidden Gems

For those looking to escape the crowds and find a moment of peace amidst the autumn splendor, these spots offer tranquility and beauty in equal measure.

11. Jiming Temple

Located right next to Xuanwu Lake, Jiming Temple is one of the oldest and most revered Buddhist temples in Nanjing. In autumn, the temple's pagoda rises majestically above a sea of golden ginkgo leaves. The incense smoke from the temple mingling with the falling leaves creates an ethereal, almost spiritual atmosphere. The view from the temple walls overlooking the lake and the city, framed by autumn colors, is absolutely unparalleled.

12. Linggu Temple Scenic Area

Adjacent to the Sun Yat-sen Mausoleum, this area is a vast forest park that is often less crowded. The centerpiece is the Beamless Hall within Linggu Temple, a unique Ming Dynasty structure made entirely of brick and stone without a single beam. In front of it stands a towering ginkgo tree that turns a magnificent gold. The combination of the ancient, architectural wonder and the vibrant natural beauty is a powerful composition. The surrounding paths through the pine and maple forests are also wonderfully colorful.

13. Jiangxinzhou

Take a short ferry ride to this large island in the Yangtze River for a more rustic and expansive autumn experience. Jiangxinzhou is an agricultural area known for its vast reed beds and poplar forests. In autumn, the reeds turn a beautiful silver-white, swaying in the wind, while the poplars turn a bright, clear yellow. It’s a different palette from the urban ginkgo and maple scenes, offering a minimalist, almost pastoral beauty that is incredibly photogenic, especially during the golden hour.

Beyond the Leaves: Experiencing Nanjing's Autumn Vibe

Autumn in Nanjing isn't just about the visuals; it's a full-sensory experience. To truly capture the essence of the season in your content, immerse yourself in its associated delights.

14. Savor the Seasonal Crab at a Traditional Restaurant

Autumn is the season for the famous Yangcheng Lake hairy crab, and Nanjing's food scene comes alive with it. A photo of these delicately steamed crabs, with their vibrant orange roe, paired with a cup of warm Shaoxing wine, is a quintessential autumn foodie shot. Visit a respected restaurant like Nanjing Da Pao Dong to enjoy this seasonal ritual. The warm, cozy interior of a traditional restaurant provides the perfect backdrop for a culinary story.

15. Enjoy a Warm Cup of Tea at a Wutaishan Cafe

After a long day of chasing fall colors, there's nothing better than warming up with a hot drink. The Wutaishan area has a thriving cafe culture. Find a cafe with a rooftop terrace or large windows overlooking the street, order a pumpkin spice latte or a traditional Chinese tea, and capture the cozy "autumn aesthetic." The simple pleasure of a warm drink, a good book, and a view of the changing leaves is the perfect way to frame the relaxed, introspective mood of the season.
