15 Rooftop Views in Nanjing Perfect for Instagram

  • Home
  • Travel Blog
  • Blog Details

The ancient capital of Nanjing, where history whispers from every brick and modernity scrapes the sky, holds a secret perspective waiting to be discovered—from above. While the city is famed for its Sun Yat-sen Mausoleum and Confucius Temple, a new trend is captivating travelers and locals alike: rooftop exploration. These elevated sanctuaries offer a breathtaking fusion of the old and the new, providing the ultimate backdrop for your Instagram feed. Forget the crowded ground-level spots; it's time to ascend and capture Nanjing from a dizzying, picture-perfect angle. We've scaled the city to bring you the 15 most Instagram-worthy rooftop views that will make your followers do a double-tap.

The Historical Heart: Rooftops Overlooking Ancient Landmarks

This category is for those who want their photos to echo with the stories of a bygone era. These rooftops provide a direct visual dialogue with Nanjing's imperial and republican past.

1. The Porcelain Tower Rooftop Terrace at Bao'en Temple

While the reconstructed Porcelain Tower itself is a marvel, the secret is the rooftop terrace of the adjacent museum. From this vantage point, you get a stunning, head-on view of the brilliant white tower with its intricate blue porcelain tiles spiraling towards the heavens. The composition is symmetrical perfection. Wait for a clear day when the white structure contrasts sharply against the deep blue sky, or capture it during the golden hour when the setting sun sets the porcelain ablaze with a warm, golden glow. It’s a shot that speaks of historical grandeur and architectural genius.

2. Zifeng Tower Observation Deck

You can't talk about Nanjing rooftops without mentioning the king of them all. Zifeng Tower, also known as the Greenland Puli Center, is one of the tallest skyscrapers in the city. Its 89th-floor observation deck offers a 360-degree, unobstructed panorama of Nanjing. To the north, you can capture the serpentine curve of the Yangtze River. To the east, the purple ridges of Zhongshan Mountain provide a dramatic backdrop. The key Instagram shot here is a wide-angle panorama that captures the vastness of the city, or a creative shot of your reflection in the glass with the urban sprawl laid out behind you.

3. Ming City Wall near Jiming Temple

This isn't a rooftop in the traditional sense, but the broad, elevated walkway of the Ming City Wall functions as one. The section that passes by the Jiming Temple offers an unparalleled view. You can frame your shot with the ancient, weathered bricks of the wall in the foreground, leading the eye towards the vibrant, golden roofs of the 1,700-year-old temple and the shimmering Xuanwu Lake beyond. It’s a powerful image that layers history upon history. Visit during sunset for a truly magical silhouette of the temple against a colorful sky.

The Modern Pulse: Sky-High Bars and Urban Oases

For a vibe that's more chic and contemporary, these rooftop spots blend craft cocktails with killer views, creating the perfect setting for a sophisticated Instagram post.

4. Flair Rooftop Bar at The Ritz-Carlton, Nanjing

Perched on the 62nd floor, Flair is the epitome of luxury. This rooftop bar offers an intimate and stylish setting with stunning views of the Xinjiekou central business district. The twinkling city lights at night create a dazzling carpet below. The best Instagram moments here involve their artisanal cocktails—often served with dry ice for a smoky effect—placed strategically in the foreground with the glittering skyline out of focus in the background. It’s a shot that screams urban elegance.

5. Vantage Bar at the InterContinental Nanjing

Another heavyweight in the high-altitude social scene, Vantage Bar provides a spectacular, nearly 360-degree view. Its floor-to-ceiling windows are perfect for capturing clean, dramatic lines of the city. The bar itself is beautifully lit, making for great portrait shots. Capture the transition from day to night, when the deep blue twilight sky provides a perfect canvas for the emerging city lights. It’s a location that works for both stylish solo portraits and group shots with friends.

6. Sky Bar at The Grand Mansion

This bar offers a different perspective, looking out over the bustling Gulou district. The view is less about towering skyscrapers and more about the lively, neon-lit energy of a central university and commercial area. It’s a great place to capture the "city that never sleeps" vibe. A time-lapse video of the flowing traffic from this rooftop is a guaranteed winner for your Instagram Stories.

Hidden Gems and Secret Spots

The most rewarding views are often the ones you have to seek out. These spots are less about commercial glamour and more about authentic discovery.

7. The Rooftop of a Laomendong Teahouse

Wander through the historic Laomendong (Old Gate East) district and keep your eyes peeled for traditional teahouses with upper floors. Many have small, secluded rooftops or balconies that look out over the neighborhood's characteristic grey-tiled roofs and winding alleys. The photo opportunity here is pure, unadulterated old Nanjing. Capture a cup of steaming tea with the rustic rooftops stretching out behind it. It’s a quiet, contemplative shot that tells a rich story.

8. Rooftop Cafe near Nanjing University (NJU)

In the backstreets surrounding the prestigious Nanjing University, you'll find several low-rise buildings housing independent cafes that have opened up their rooftops. These spots are popular with students and artists. The view is often a charming jumble of old campus buildings and lush, green trees. It’s the perfect setting for a more casual, academic-inspired photoshoot. Think open book, a cup of coffee, and a thoughtful gaze into the distance.

9. Top Floor of the Nanjing Library Annex

While the main reading rooms require silence, some of the annex buildings or public areas on higher floors offer surprising views of the city. You can get a unique perspective looking towards the Presidential Palace or the busy streets of Daxinggong. The key here is to capture a "scholar's view," framing the modern world through the window of a place of knowledge. A shot of the cityscape reflected in a windowpane, with books subtly in the frame, creates a compelling narrative.

Scenic and Panoramic Overlooks

These locations offer vast, sweeping views that are more about natural and urban landscapes than a specific bar or building.

10. Sun Yat-sen Mausoleum Ascend

The climb up the hundreds of steps to the Sun Yat-sen Mausoleum is a pilgrimage every visitor makes. While not a rooftop, the platform at the top provides one of the most iconic views in all of Nanjing. Looking back down the steps, you see the symmetrical layout of white marble, the blue-tiled archway, and the sprawling, forested purples of Zhongshan Mountain beyond. It’s a powerful, awe-inspiring shot that captures both human ambition and natural beauty.

11. Xuanwu Lake View from the City Wall

Another brilliant use of the Ming City Wall, this section runs along the southern edge of Xuanwu Lake. From up here, you get an elevated, panoramic view of the entire lake, its five islets, and the modern skyline of the city rising in the distance. It’s especially stunning in the spring when the lakeside is dotted with cherry blossoms, or in the autumn when the leaves change color. A wide-angle shot capturing the harmony between the tranquil lake and the bustling city is a must.

12. Purple Mountain Observatory Peak

For the ultimate panoramic view, hike or take a taxi to the peak of Purple Mountain where the historic observatory is located. From this highest point in the area, you can see all of Nanjing laid out like a map. On a hazy day, the city has a mysterious, ethereal quality. On a clear day, the visibility is endless. This is the place for that epic, "I conquered Nanjing" landscape photo that will stop your followers mid-scroll.

Cultural and Artistic Vantage Points

These rooftops are integrated into spaces dedicated to culture, offering a view that is as intellectually stimulating as it is visually appealing.

13. Rooftop of the Nanjing Museum of Art

The museum's rooftop terrace is a relatively quiet spot that offers a unique perspective. You're surrounded by the artistic essence of the building itself, with views overlooking the greenery of the surrounding area. It’s a place to capture a more refined, artistic shot. Perhaps a photo that contrasts the clean, modern lines of the museum's architecture with the organic forms of the trees and city in the distance.

14. The 1865 Creative Industry Park Roofts

This park, set in a repurposed historic munitions factory, is a hub of creativity. Many of the renovated industrial buildings have accessible rooftops. The view from here is a fascinating mix of red-brick historical architecture and stark, modern metal and glass. It’s a dream location for urban photographers and those with an industrial-chic aesthetic. Capture the textures, the lines, and the fusion of eras.

15. Rooftop of a Xinjiekou Shopping Mall

In the heart of the shopping district, some of the larger malls have public rooftop parking or garden areas. While the view might not be of ancient temples, it is a pulsating snapshot of modern Chinese urban life. You can capture the dizzying flow of traffic, the dense concentration of neon signs, and the sheer verticality of the city's architecture. It’s a gritty, energetic, and authentically metropolitan shot that showcases Nanjing's powerful economic engine.

Each of these rooftops tells a different story of Nanjing. From the solemn whispers of history to the vibrant shouts of modernity, these elevated platforms provide the perfect stage for your next Instagram masterpiece. So charge your phone, clear your memory card, and get ready to see—and share—Nanjing like never before.
