20 Instagrammable Places in Nanjing for a Unique Experience

  • Home
  • Travel Blog
  • Blog Details

Nanjing, a city where ancient history and modern vibrancy coalesce, offers a treasure trove of visually stunning locations perfect for capturing that unforgettable Instagram shot. From serene lakes and majestic mountains to bustling markets and architectural marvels, every corner tells a story. Whether you're a history buff, a nature lover, or a foodie, Nanjing's diverse scenery provides the perfect backdrop for a unique and photogenic adventure. Let's explore twenty of the most Instagrammable spots that promise not only great photos but also an immersive experience into the heart of this incredible city.

The Historical & Cultural Gems

The Sun Yat-sen Mausoleum

Perched on the southern slope of Purple Mountain, the Sun Yat-sen Mausoleum is an architectural masterpiece and a place of profound historical significance. The grand blue-roofed marble structure, with its 392 steps leading to the memorial hall, offers a powerful symmetrical composition for your photos. The sense of scale and the surrounding lush greenery create a majestic atmosphere. Early morning visits provide soft lighting and fewer crowds, allowing you to capture the solemn beauty of this national monument. The view from the top, overlooking the sprawling forests, is absolutely breathtaking and worth the climb.

The Nanjing City Wall (Ming City Wall)

For a unique perspective on the city, head to the Nanjing City Wall, one of the longest city walls ever built. The section near the Jiming Temple or the Zhonghua Gate offers incredible photo opportunities. The ancient, weathered bricks contrast beautifully with the modern skyline in the distance, telling a story of past and present. Walking along the top of the wall as the sun sets, casting a golden glow over the stones, makes for a dramatic and evocative shot. It’s a place where you can literally touch history while framing a perfect picture.

The Presidential Palace

A complex that witnessed the transformation of modern China, the Presidential Palace is a blend of Chinese and Western architectural styles. Its gardens, corridors, and historical buildings are incredibly photogenic. The East Garden, with its traditional pavilions, ponds, and rockeries, is a classic Chinese landscape perfect for a serene, elegant photo. The historical offices and meeting rooms, preserved in their original state, offer a glimpse into a bygone era. The play of light and shadow through the wooden lattices creates a captivating effect for photography enthusiasts.

The Confucius Temple (Fuzimiao) Qinhuai Scenery Area

This is the heart of Nanjing's old town, where the past comes alive. The Confucius Temple itself, with its vibrant red and gold colors, is a stunning subject, especially when illuminated at night. The real magic, however, lies along the Qinhuai River. Take a traditional painted boat ride and capture the reflections of the ancient buildings and red lanterns on the water. The bustling streets filled with snack stalls, souvenir shops, and crowds in traditional Hanfu clothing create a lively, dynamic scene full of color and energy. It’s a sensory overload in the best way possible.

The Linggu Temple Scenic Area

Nestled deep in the Purple Mountain, the Linggu Temple area is a haven of tranquility. While the temple itself is beautiful, the most iconic spot is the Wuliang Hall, also known as the Beamless Hall. This unique brick-arch structure, built without a single beam, is an architectural wonder. Its simple yet powerful form against a backdrop of dense forest makes for a minimalist and impactful photo. The nearby Linggu Pagoda offers a high vantage point for sweeping views of the entire scenic area, especially stunning during the autumn when the leaves change color.

The Natural & Scenic Escapes

Xuanwu Lake

A vast urban lake right in the city center, Xuanwu Lake is a favorite among locals and tourists alike. Its five islets connected by causeways offer endless picturesque views. The lake is particularly famous for its cherry blossoms in spring and lotus flowers in summer. Rent a paddleboat or simply stroll along the willow-lined paths. The skyline of Nanjing reflected in the lake's surface, especially during golden hour, creates a perfect blend of nature and city life. The numerous pavilions and bridges provide classic frames for your photos.

Purple Mountain (Zijin Shan)

As a major lung of the city, Purple Mountain is a sprawling scenic area brimming with photo ops. Beyond the mausoleum and temple, the mountain itself offers lush forests, quiet trails, and the Nanjing Astronautics Museum. The cable car ride to the top provides a unique aerial view of the canopy below. The Ming Xiaoling Mausoleum's Sacred Way, lined with giant stone statues of animals and generals, is a remarkable sight. Walking this path feels like stepping back in time, and the scale of the statues against the natural backdrop is truly impressive.

Mochou Lake Park

Steeped in legend, Mochou Lake Park is a classical Chinese garden known for its elegance and peace. The park's namesake, the statue of Lady Mochou, the lotus-filled lake, and the intricate pavilions are all beautiful subjects. It’s a place that encourages slower, more contemplative photography. The reflections of the traditional architecture in the calm waters, the curved bridges, and the weeping willows create a composition straight out of a traditional painting. It’s especially romantic in the early morning mist.

Laoshan National Forest Park

For those willing to venture a bit outside the city center, Laoshan offers a wilder, more rugged natural experience. It's one of the largest national forest parks in China, known for its hot springs and dense forests. The hiking trails lead to stunning viewpoints, and the biodiversity is incredible. Capture the dense greenery, the unique rock formations, and if you're lucky, some wildlife. It’s a fantastic location for landscape photography and for showcasing a different, more untamed side of Nanjing.

Yangtze River Bridge

An iconic symbol of Chinese engineering and self-reliance, the Nanjing Yangtze River Bridge is a monumental structure. The view from the bridge walkway, with the mighty Yangtze River flowing beneath and the cityscape on either side, is powerful. At night, the bridge is lit up, creating a dazzling display of lights reflected on the water. For a different perspective, go to the bridge park on the south bank to capture the full scale of this double-decker bridge. It represents the strength and progress of the city.

The Modern & Quirky Spots

Nanjing Eye Pedestrian Bridge

A stunning piece of modern architecture, the Nanjing Eye is a cable-stayed pedestrian bridge that has quickly become a city landmark. Its sleek, white structure, especially when lit with changing colors at night, is a fantastic subject for contemporary and abstract photography. Walking across the bridge offers panoramic views of the Jiangxinzhou and the river. The best time to shoot is during blue hour, when the sky has a deep blue hue and the bridge lights are on, creating a magical contrast.

Deji Plaza & Xinjiekou Area

For a taste of Nanjing's modern, pulsating heart, the Xinjiekou commercial area is the place to be. Deji Plaza, with its high-end shopping and the renowned "Lighthouse" art installation, is a hub of contemporary style. The surrounding neon-lit streets, towering skyscrapers, and bustling crowds capture the city's energetic vibe. It’s perfect for street photography, urban landscapes, and showcasing the fashionable side of Nanjing life. The mix of light, movement, and architecture here is endless.

The 1912街区 (1912 Block)

Adjacent to the Presidential Palace, the 1912 Block is a trendy entertainment district housed in restored Republican-era buildings. The blend of historical architecture with modern bars, restaurants, and cafes is incredibly chic. The red-brick walls, cobblestone pathways, and stylish decor make every corner Instagrammable. By day, it's a quiet, elegant area for coffee shots; by night, it transforms into a vibrant social scene with beautiful lighting. It’s the perfect spot to show Nanjing's sophisticated nightlife and cultural fusion.

Nanjing International Youth Cultural Centre

Designed by the renowned architecture firm Zaha Hadid, this centre is a masterpiece of futuristic design. The twin towers, with their fluid, sculptural forms, seem to defy gravity. The public spaces and the interior are just as impressive, with sweeping curves and dynamic spaces. It’s a paradise for architectural photographers and anyone interested in bold, contemporary design. The play of light on its unique facade changes throughout the day, offering a variety of shooting opportunities.

Hexi CBD Skyline

To capture the full force of Nanjing's modernity, head to the Hexi Central Business District. The forest of gleaming skyscrapers, including the Zifeng Tower and the Jiangsu Greenland Financial Centre, creates a powerful and impressive skyline. The best views can be captured from across the river or from high vantage points within the CBD itself. At night, the light show from the buildings is a spectacular sight. This area embodies the city's economic dynamism and its vision for the future.

The Unique & Offbeat Finds

Niushou Mountain (Niushou Shan)

This Buddhist cultural park is as visually stunning as it is spiritually significant. The most photogenic feature is the Usnisa Palace, an incredibly ornate structure built into the mountainside. The interior is a breathtaking spectacle of intricate carvings, grand halls, and a stunning dome. The exterior, with its grand staircase and traditional-elements-meet-modern-design architecture, is equally impressive. The surrounding park is beautifully landscaped, offering peaceful scenes and panoramic views of the area.

Nanjing Yangshan Quarry (The Giant Stele)

For a truly unique and slightly mysterious location, visit the Yangshan Quarry. Here lies an unfinished imperial stele from the Ming Dynasty—a colossal stone block that was abandoned. The sheer scale of the stone and the story behind it are awe-inspiring. It’s a place that feels forgotten by time, offering a raw and powerful photographic subject. The quarry itself and the surrounding landscape provide a dramatic, almost surreal backdrop. It's a hidden gem for those looking for something different.

Nanjing University (Gulou Campus)

Experience the nostalgic and scholarly atmosphere of one of China's oldest and most prestigious universities. The Gulou campus is beautiful, with its historic buildings, tree-lined avenues, and a sense of quiet intellectualism. The old library and the North Building are particularly photogenic. It’s a great place for capturing a more tranquil, academic side of the city. The vibrant energy of the students adds life to the scenes, making it feel authentic and vibrant.

Porcelain Tower of Nanjing (Replica at Dabao'en Temple)

While the original wonder of the world is long gone, a stunning modern replica stands at the Dabao'en Temple Park. This glass and steel structure is a contemporary interpretation of the ancient tower, and it glows with a mesmerizing light show after dark. The contrast between the ancient temple grounds and this futuristic tower is fascinating. The reflections in the surrounding pools and the intricate details of the tower itself make for captivating photos, especially at night when it becomes a kaleidoscope of color.

Nanjing's Hidden Hutongs & Alleys

Beyond the main tourist trails, Nanjing is filled with charming, narrow alleys known as Hutongs. Wandering through areas like Laomendong or the alleys near the Confucius Temple reveals the everyday life of the city. You'll find traditional architecture, small local eateries, vintage shops, and washing hanging out to dry. These scenes are full of character and color, offering a glimpse into the authentic, unpolished soul of Nanjing. It’s perfect for candid street photography and capturing the simple, beautiful moments of local life.
