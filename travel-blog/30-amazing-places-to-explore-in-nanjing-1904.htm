30 Amazing Places to Explore in Nanjing

  • Home
  • Travel Blog
  • Blog Details

Nanjing, the ancient capital of China, is a city where history and modernity blend seamlessly. From imperial palaces to serene lakes, bustling markets to tranquil temples, Nanjing offers a treasure trove of experiences for every traveler. Whether you're a history buff, a nature lover, or a foodie, this city has something unforgettable for you. Here are 30 amazing places to explore in Nanjing.

Historical & Cultural Gems

1. The Sun Yat-sen Mausoleum

Perched on the slopes of Purple Mountain, this grand mausoleum honors Dr. Sun Yat-sen, the founding father of modern China. The 392-step staircase leads to a breathtaking view of the surrounding landscape.

2. Ming Xiaoling Mausoleum

A UNESCO World Heritage Site, this is the tomb of the Hongwu Emperor, founder of the Ming Dynasty. The Sacred Way, lined with stone statues of animals and officials, is a highlight.

3. Presidential Palace

Once the seat of China’s provisional government, this complex showcases a mix of Chinese and Western architectural styles. It’s a must-visit for history enthusiasts.

4. Nanjing Museum

One of China’s largest museums, it houses over 400,000 artifacts, including ancient ceramics, jade, and calligraphy. The underground exhibition hall is particularly impressive.

5. Confucius Temple (Fuzimiao)

A lively area along the Qinhuai River, this temple is surrounded by shops, teahouses, and street food stalls. Don’t miss the evening boat ride for a magical view of the lantern-lit river.

6. Nanjing City Wall

Built during the Ming Dynasty, this is one of the best-preserved ancient city walls in the world. Rent a bike and ride along its 35-kilometer stretch for a unique perspective of the city.

7. Zhonghua Gate (Gate of China)

The largest castle-style gate in China, this fortress-like structure offers a glimpse into Nanjing’s military history. Climb to the top for panoramic views.

8. Jiming Temple

One of Nanjing’s oldest Buddhist temples, Jiming Temple is especially beautiful during cherry blossom season. The peaceful atmosphere makes it a perfect escape from the city’s hustle.

9. Linggu Temple & Pagoda

Located near the Sun Yat-sen Mausoleum, this temple complex features a stunning nine-story pagoda with sweeping views of Purple Mountain.

10. Nanjing Massacre Memorial Hall

A sobering but essential visit, this museum commemorates the victims of the 1937 Nanjing Massacre. The exhibits are powerful and deeply moving.

Natural Escapes

11. Xuanwu Lake

This vast lake in the city center is perfect for a leisurely stroll or boat ride. The surrounding park is especially picturesque in spring when cherry blossoms bloom.

12. Purple Mountain (Zijin Shan)

A haven for hikers and nature lovers, Purple Mountain is home to several historical sites, including the Ming Xiaoling Mausoleum and the Linggu Temple.

13. Mochou Lake

Named after a legendary sorrowful beauty, this lake is surrounded by lush gardens and pavilions. It’s a serene spot for a morning walk.

14. Qixia Mountain

Famous for its autumn foliage and the Qixia Temple, this mountain is a popular weekend getaway. The thousand-year-old Buddhist statues carved into the cliffs are a must-see.

15. Niushou Mountain

A newer attraction, this mountain features a stunning Buddhist palace with a jaw-dropping golden dome. The underground palace is equally mesmerizing.

16. Tangshan Hot Springs

Just outside Nanjing, these natural hot springs are perfect for relaxation. Many resorts offer private pools with mountain views.

17. Laoshan National Forest Park

A great spot for hiking and camping, this park is known for its dense forests and fresh air. The cable car ride to the summit offers spectacular scenery.

18. Jiangxinzhou

An island in the Yangtze River, Jiangxinzhou is a peaceful retreat with orchards, flower fields, and cycling trails.

Modern Attractions

19. Nanjing Eye

This pedestrian bridge over the Yangtze River is illuminated at night, creating a futuristic glow. Walk across for stunning city skyline views.

20. Deji Plaza

One of Nanjing’s premier shopping destinations, Deji Plaza is home to luxury brands, gourmet restaurants, and an impressive art gallery.

21. Nanjing Greenland Financial Center

Standing at 450 meters, this skyscraper is one of the tallest in China. The observation deck offers a bird’s-eye view of the city.

22. Hexi New Town

Nanjing’s modern business district boasts sleek architecture, high-end malls, and a vibrant nightlife scene.

23. Nanjing International Youth Cultural Centre

Designed by Zaha Hadid, this architectural marvel hosts exhibitions, concerts, and cultural events.

Food & Markets

24. Confucius Temple Snack Street

A food lover’s paradise, this street offers local delicacies like salted duck, tangbao (soup dumplings), and duck blood vermicelli soup.

25. Shiziqiao Night Market

Open after sunset, this market is packed with street food stalls, souvenir shops, and live performances.

26. Nanjing Impressions (Nanjing Da Pai Dang)

A popular restaurant chain serving authentic Nanjing cuisine in a nostalgic, old-world setting.

27. Lao Mendong

A historic neighborhood with charming alleyways, teahouses, and hidden eateries. Perfect for an off-the-beaten-path culinary adventure.

Unique Experiences

28. Qinhuai River Cruise

Glide along the river at night to see the illuminated bridges and traditional architecture. It’s one of the most romantic things to do in Nanjing.

29. Nanjing Underwater World

A fun family attraction featuring marine life exhibits, a tunnel aquarium, and interactive shows.

30. Hongshan Forest Zoo

Home to over 4,000 animals, including rare species like the South China tiger, this zoo is a great day trip for animal lovers.

Nanjing is a city that captivates with its rich history, stunning landscapes, and vibrant culture. Whether you're exploring ancient ruins, savoring local flavors, or soaking in natural beauty, every corner of this city has a story to tell.
