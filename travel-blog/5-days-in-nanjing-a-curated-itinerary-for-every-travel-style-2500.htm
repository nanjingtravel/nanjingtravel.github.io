5 Days in Nanjing: A Curated Itinerary for Every Travel Style

  • Home
  • Travel Blog
  • Blog Details

Nanjing, the ancient capital of China, is a city where history and modernity collide. Whether you're a history buff, a foodie, an outdoor enthusiast, or a luxury traveler, this five-day itinerary has something tailored just for you. From the majestic Sun Yat-sen Mausoleum to the bustling Confucius Temple, Nanjing offers a rich tapestry of experiences. Let’s dive into the perfect five-day plan for every type of traveler.

Day 1: The History Lover’s Dream

Morning: Sun Yat-sen Mausoleum

Start your journey at the Sun Yat-sen Mausoleum, a tribute to the founding father of modern China. The 392-step climb rewards you with panoramic views of the Purple Mountain and a serene atmosphere. The blue-tiled roof and white marble structures are architectural marvels.

Afternoon: Ming Xiaoling Mausoleum

A short walk away lies the Ming Xiaoling Mausoleum, the resting place of the first Ming emperor, Zhu Yuanzhang. The Sacred Way, lined with stone statues of mythical creatures, is a photographer’s paradise.

Evening: Nanjing Museum

Wrap up your day at the Nanjing Museum, one of China’s largest museums. The "Jiangnan" (southern China) exhibits and the replica of a 1930s street are must-sees.

Day 2: Foodie’s Paradise

Morning: Zhonghua Gate & Qinhuai Snacks

Begin at Zhonghua Gate, the largest ancient city gate in China. Nearby, the Qinhuai River area is packed with street food stalls. Try tangbao (soup dumplings) and duck blood vermicelli soup—local favorites.

Afternoon: Lao Men Dong Food Street

Head to Lao Men Dong, a culinary hotspot. Don’t miss saltwater duck, Nanjing’s signature dish, or stinky tofu for the adventurous.

Evening: Night Market at Confucius Temple

As the sun sets, the Confucius Temple area transforms into a vibrant night market. Sip on sweet osmanthus rice wine while browsing souvenirs.

Day 3: Nature & Outdoor Adventure

Morning: Purple Mountain Hiking

Hike or bike through Purple Mountain’s lush trails. The Zijin Observatory offers stunning city views.

Afternoon: Xuanwu Lake

Rent a paddleboat on Xuanwu Lake, a peaceful retreat in the city center. The lakeside paths are perfect for a leisurely stroll.

Evening: Yangtze River Cruise

Cap off the day with a sunset cruise on the Yangtze River. The illuminated Nanjing Yangtze River Bridge is a sight to behold.

Day 4: Arts & Culture Immersion

Morning: Presidential Palace

Explore the Presidential Palace, a blend of Chinese and Western architecture that witnessed pivotal moments in China’s modern history.

Afternoon: 1865 Creative Industry Park

This former arms factory is now an artsy hub with galleries, cafes, and indie boutiques. Perfect for Instagram-worthy shots.

Evening: Nanjing Folk Custom Museum

Dive into local traditions at this museum, where you can watch puppet shows and try your hand at paper-cutting.

Day 5: Luxury & Relaxation

Morning: Spa Day at The Ritz-Carlton

Pamper yourself with a traditional Chinese massage or a jade-infused facial at The Ritz-Carlton’s spa.

Afternoon: Shopping at Deji Plaza

Splurge at Deji Plaza, home to high-end brands and a stunning rooftop garden.

Evening: Fine Dining at Jinling Hotel

End your trip with a gourmet meal at Jinling Hotel, where the Nanjing salted duck is elevated to an art form.

No matter your travel style, Nanjing promises an unforgettable journey. Pack your bags and get ready to fall in love with this dynamic city!
