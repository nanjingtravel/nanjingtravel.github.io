A Guide to Nanjing’s Best Bubble Tea and Drink Spots

  • Home
  • Travel Blog
  • Blog Details

Nanjing, a city steeped in history and culture, is also a hidden gem for bubble tea enthusiasts. Whether you're exploring the ancient city walls or strolling through the vibrant Xinjiekou district, there’s always a refreshing drink nearby to keep you energized. From classic milk teas to innovative fruit infusions, Nanjing’s drink scene offers something for every palate. Here’s your ultimate guide to the best bubble tea and drink spots in Nanjing.

The Classics: Must-Try Bubble Tea Chains

1. Heytea (喜茶)

Heytea is a household name in China, and Nanjing is no exception. Known for its premium ingredients and creative flavors, this chain is a must-visit. Their signature Cheese Tea—a creamy, savory-sweet foam atop a fragrant tea base—is a game-changer. For something fruity, try the Strawberry Cheezo Tea or the Mango Pomelo Sago, a tropical delight.

2. Nayuki (奈雪の茶)

Nayuki combines bubble tea with artisanal bakery treats, making it a perfect pit stop. Their Strawberry Shortcake Milk Tea is a fan favorite, blending fresh strawberries with fluffy cake bits. If you’re feeling adventurous, opt for the Dirty Boba Latte, a rich, coffee-infused twist on traditional boba.

3. Coco (都可)

A global bubble tea giant, Coco is reliable for quick, affordable drinks. The Pearl Milk Tea is a timeless choice, but don’t miss their Passion Fruit Bubble Tea for a tangy kick. Pro tip: Ask for less sugar if you prefer a subtler sweetness.

Hidden Gems: Local Favorites

1. Lelecha (乐乐茶)

Tucked away in the bustling Deji Plaza, Lelecha is a haven for cheese tea lovers. Their Grape Cheezo Tea is a standout, with juicy grapes and a velvety cheese cap. Pair it with one of their fluffy Dirty Bread pastries for the ultimate snack combo.

2. Yi Fang (一芳)

Specializing in fruit teas, Yi Fang uses fresh, seasonal ingredients. The Brown Sugar Pearl Latte is a must-try, with its caramelized pearls and creamy milk. For a lighter option, the Lychee Fruit Tea is refreshingly sweet with a hint of floral notes.

3. Xingfutea (幸福堂)

This Taiwanese import is famous for its Brown Sugar Boba Milk, made with hand-stirred pearls and organic milk. The theatrical preparation—flame-seared sugar crust—adds to the experience.

Trendy & Instagram-Worthy Spots

1. Modern Tea Lab

Located near Confucius Temple, this sleek spot experiments with molecular mixology. Try their Bubble Tea Cocktail, a boozy twist on the classic, or the Matcha Cloud, a frothy, photogenic drink topped with edible flowers.

2. Bubble Bee

A quirky café in the 1912 District, Bubble Bee serves drinks in lightbulb-shaped cups. Their Honeycomb Milk Tea is a sweet, floral concoction, while the Lavender Lemonade is a soothing, aromatic pick-me-up.

3. Machi Machi

Made famous by Jay Chou, Machi Machi’s Jasmine Green Tea with Cheese Foam is a must. The minimalist décor and signature bottle packaging make it a hotspot for Instagram snaps.

Seasonal Specials & Regional Flavors

Nanjing’s drink scene shines with seasonal offerings. In summer, look out for Watermelon Slush stands near Xuanwu Lake. Winter brings warm Ginger Milk Tea at traditional teahouses like Laomendong Tea House.

For a taste of local flair, try Osmanthus Oolong Tea, a fragrant nod to Nanjing’s city flower. Many shops near Qinhuai River infuse their drinks with osmanthus syrup for a subtly sweet, floral finish.

Pro Tips for Bubble Tea Tourists

  • Customize Your Sweetness: Most shops offer sugar level adjustments (0%, 30%, 50%, 70%, 100%).
  • Toppings Galore: Beyond pearls, explore options like aloe vera, grass jelly, or salted cream.
  • Late-Night Cravings: Spots near Nanjing University stay open past midnight, perfect for post-sightseeing refreshments.

Whether you’re a boba newbie or a seasoned sipper, Nanjing’s drink spots promise a delicious adventure. So grab a cup, explore the city, and let the flavors guide your journey.
