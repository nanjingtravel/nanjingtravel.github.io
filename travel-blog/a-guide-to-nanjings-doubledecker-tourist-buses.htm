A Guide to Nanjing’s Double-Decker Tourist Buses

  • Home
  • Travel Blog
  • Blog Details

Nanjing, a city steeped in history and modernity, offers travelers a unique way to explore its treasures: the double-decker tourist buses. These iconic red buses not only provide convenience but also a panoramic view of the city’s stunning landscapes, from ancient landmarks to bustling urban centers. Whether you’re a first-time visitor or a seasoned traveler, hopping on one of these buses is an unforgettable way to experience Nanjing.

Why Choose Nanjing’s Double-Decker Buses?

Unbeatable Views from the Top Deck

The upper deck of these buses is the prime spot for sightseeing. With open-air seating (weather permitting), you’ll get unobstructed views of Nanjing’s skyline, the Qinhuai River, and landmarks like the Confucius Temple and Zhongshan Mountain. It’s like a moving observation deck!

Hassle-Free Exploration

Navigating a new city can be daunting, especially with language barriers. The tourist buses eliminate the stress by stopping at major attractions, so you won’t miss a thing. Plus, multilingual audio guides (available in English, Japanese, and Korean) provide fascinating insights into Nanjing’s history.

Budget-Friendly and Flexible

For a flat fee, you can hop on and off at any stop throughout the day. This flexibility lets you explore at your own pace—linger at the Nanjing Museum, stroll along Xuanwu Lake, or shop in Xinjiekou without worrying about transportation.

Key Routes and Must-See Stops

Route 1: The Classic City Tour

This route covers Nanjing’s most iconic sites:
- Zhongshan Scenic Area: Home to Sun Yat-sen’s Mausoleum and the Ming Xiaoling Mausoleum.
- Presidential Palace: A blend of Chinese and Western architectural styles.
- Confucius Temple (Fuzimiao): The heart of Nanjing’s cultural and culinary scene.

Route 2: The Modern Nanjing Loop

Perfect for those who love urban vibes:
- Xinjiekou: Nanjing’s shopping mecca.
- Nanjing Eye Pedestrian Bridge: A futuristic landmark over the Yangtze River.
- Jiangning织造 Museum: A hidden gem for textile and history enthusiasts.

Tips for Riding the Double-Decker Buses

Best Time to Ride

  • Early Morning: Beat the crowds and enjoy cooler temperatures.
  • Evening: Catch the city lights and the Qinhuai River’s illuminated boats.

Ticket Options

  • 24-Hour Pass: Ideal for a full day of exploration.
  • 48-Hour Pass: Great for weekend travelers.
    Pro tip: Buy tickets online in advance during peak seasons (spring and autumn).

Don’t Forget

  • Sunscreen and hats for the open-top deck.
  • A camera—every turn offers a photo-worthy moment.

Beyond the Bus: Nearby Food and Souvenirs

After hopping off, indulge in Nanjing’s famous duck blood vermicelli soup (鸭血粉丝汤, yā xiě fěn sī tāng) or grab a sweet osmanthus cake (桂花糕, guì huā gāo) from a street vendor. For souvenirs, look for Yunjin brocade, a luxurious silk fabric unique to Nanjing.

Whether you’re a history buff, a foodie, or just love a good view, Nanjing’s double-decker buses are your ticket to an unforgettable adventure. So grab a seat, feel the breeze, and let the city unfold before your eyes!
