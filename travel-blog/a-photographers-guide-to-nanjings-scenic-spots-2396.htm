A Photographer’s Guide to Nanjing’s Scenic Spots

  • Home
  • Travel Blog
  • Blog Details

Nanjing, the ancient capital of China, is a treasure trove for photographers. From its rich historical landmarks to its serene natural landscapes, the city offers endless opportunities to capture stunning visuals. Whether you’re a professional photographer or an enthusiastic traveler with a camera, this guide will help you discover the best spots to photograph in Nanjing.

The Best Time to Photograph Nanjing

Golden Hours: Sunrise and Sunset

The soft, warm light during sunrise and sunset enhances the beauty of Nanjing’s landscapes. The best locations for golden hour photography include:
- Xuanwu Lake: The reflection of the sun on the water creates a magical glow.
- Purple Mountain (Zijin Shan): The misty peaks and ancient temples look ethereal in the early morning light.

Seasonal Highlights

  • Spring: The plum blossoms at Plum Blossom Hill (Meihua Shan) are a must-capture.
  • Autumn: The ginkgo trees lining the streets of Nanjing turn golden, offering a breathtaking backdrop.

Top Photography Spots in Nanjing

Historical Landmarks

The Sun Yat-sen Mausoleum

This grand mausoleum, dedicated to Dr. Sun Yat-sen, is an architectural marvel. The symmetrical design and the long staircase make for powerful compositions. For a unique perspective, try shooting from a low angle to emphasize the grandeur.

The Ming Xiaoling Mausoleum

A UNESCO World Heritage Site, this ancient tomb is surrounded by lush greenery and stone statues. The Sacred Way, lined with mythical creatures, is perfect for moody, atmospheric shots.

The Presidential Palace

A blend of Chinese and Western architecture, this site offers diverse photographic opportunities. The gardens and historical buildings are ideal for both wide-angle and detail shots.

Natural Landscapes

Xuanwu Lake

One of Nanjing’s most picturesque spots, Xuanwu Lake is perfect for capturing reflections, especially during sunrise. The lake’s pagodas and bridges add a traditional touch to your photos.

Purple Mountain (Zijin Shan)

This mountain is a paradise for nature photographers. The dense forests, hiking trails, and panoramic views of the city are worth exploring. Don’t miss the Linggu Temple area, where the autumn foliage is spectacular.

Qinhuai River

The river comes alive at night with illuminated boats and traditional architecture. Long-exposure shots of the water and lights create a dreamy effect.

Urban and Modern Nanjing

Nanjing Eye Pedestrian Bridge

This modern architectural wonder is a great spot for nighttime photography. The LED-lit bridge against the city skyline makes for a striking image.

Confucius Temple (Fuzimiao) Area

While the temple itself is historic, the surrounding area is bustling with street vendors, lanterns, and lively crowds. It’s a great place for street photography.

Photography Tips for Nanjing

Gear Recommendations

  • Wide-angle lens: Essential for capturing landscapes and architecture.
  • Telephoto lens: Useful for compressing distant scenes, like the views from Purple Mountain.
  • Tripod: Necessary for long-exposure shots, especially at Qinhuai River.

Composition Techniques

  • Leading lines: Use the staircases at Sun Yat-sen Mausoleum to guide the viewer’s eye.
  • Symmetry: The Presidential Palace’s architecture is perfect for symmetrical compositions.
  • Foreground interest: Incorporate elements like fallen leaves or water reflections to add depth.

Cultural Sensitivity

Always be respectful when photographing locals or religious sites. Ask for permission if you’re taking close-up portraits of people.

Hidden Gems for Photographers

Qixia Mountain

Less crowded than Purple Mountain, Qixia offers stunning temples and autumn foliage. The Qixia Temple is particularly photogenic.

Lao Men Dong

This revitalized historical district combines old and new Nanjing. The narrow alleys and street art provide unique backdrops.

Nanjing Yangtze River Bridge

An iconic symbol of Nanjing, this bridge is best photographed at dusk when the city lights begin to glow.

Final Thoughts on Capturing Nanjing

Nanjing’s blend of history, nature, and modernity makes it a photographer’s dream. Whether you’re drawn to ancient temples, vibrant streets, or tranquil lakes, the city has something to offer every lens. Plan your shoots around the light and seasons, and don’t forget to explore beyond the well-known spots—sometimes the best photos come from unexpected places.
