Best Apps for Navigating Nanjing Public Transport

  • Home
  • Travel Blog
  • Blog Details

Nanjing, the ancient capital of China, is a city where history and modernity blend seamlessly. From the majestic Sun Yat-sen Mausoleum to the bustling Confucius Temple, there’s no shortage of attractions to explore. But to truly experience the city like a local, mastering its public transport system is essential. Fortunately, several apps make navigating Nanjing’s buses, metros, and taxis a breeze. Here’s a rundown of the best apps to help you get around efficiently.

Why Use Public Transport Apps in Nanjing?

Nanjing’s public transport network is extensive, with over 10 metro lines, hundreds of bus routes, and plenty of bike-sharing options. While the system is well-organized, it can be overwhelming for first-time visitors. Public transport apps provide real-time updates, route planning, and even ticket purchasing, saving you time and stress.

Benefits of Using These Apps

  • Real-time tracking: Avoid long waits by checking bus and metro schedules.
  • Route optimization: Find the fastest or cheapest way to your destination.
  • Multilingual support: Some apps offer English interfaces for international travelers.
  • Integrated payments: Purchase tickets or scan QR codes directly from your phone.

Top Apps for Nanjing Public Transport

1. Metro Man (地铁通)

If you’re relying heavily on Nanjing’s metro system, Metro Man is a must-have. This app provides detailed metro maps, station exits, and transfer guides.

Key Features

  • Interactive metro map: Zoom in to see station layouts and nearby attractions.
  • Offline functionality: No internet? No problem—download maps in advance.
  • First/last train times: Never miss the last train back to your hotel.

Pro Tip: Use Metro Man to locate the best exits for popular spots like Xinjiekou (Nanjing’s shopping hub) or the Nanjing Museum.

2. Baidu Maps (百度地图)

While Google Maps struggles in China, Baidu Maps is the go-to navigation app. It covers all transport modes, including walking and cycling routes.

Why It’s Great for Tourists

  • English-friendly: One of the few Chinese apps with decent English support.
  • Live traffic updates: Avoid crowded buses or delayed metros.
  • Points of interest (POI) search: Find nearby restaurants, ATMs, or tourist sites.

Example: Searching "Qinhuai River" will show you the best bus or metro routes to reach the scenic area.

3. Alipay (支付宝)

More than just a payment app, Alipay has a "Transport" section that lets you scan QR codes for buses and metros.

How to Use It

  1. Open Alipay and search for "Nanjing Public Transport."
  2. Apply for an electronic transit card (no deposit required).
  3. Scan the QR code at metro gates or bus card readers.

Bonus: Alipay also works for bike-sharing (Hello Bike, Meituan Bike) and taxis.

4. Didi Chuxing (滴滴出行)

China’s answer to Uber, Didi is perfect for when you need a ride. It offers taxis, private cars, and even shuttle buses.

Traveler-Friendly Features

  • English interface: Easily book rides without language barriers.
  • Price estimates: Avoid overpaying with upfront fare calculations.
  • Safety tools: Share your ride details with friends or family.

Ideal for: Late-night returns after exploring Fuzimiao (Confucius Temple) night markets.

5. Nanjing Public Transport (南京公交)

This official app specializes in bus routes, providing live arrival times and crowdedness indicators.

Standout Tools

  • Real-time bus tracking: See exactly when your bus will arrive.
  • Route alerts: Get notified about detours or delays.
  • Historical data: Check peak hours to avoid rush times.

Best for: Reaching off-the-beaten-path sites like the Jiangning织造 Museum.

Honorable Mentions

6. Meituan (美团)

Primarily a food delivery app, Meituan also offers transport options, including bike rentals and ride-hailing.

7. Hello Bike (哈啰出行)

For short distances, Hello Bike provides affordable bike-sharing with dedicated parking spots across Nanjing.

Tips for Using Transport Apps in Nanjing

  • Download before arrival: Some apps require a Chinese phone number for verification.
  • Use VPNs cautiously: Google-based apps may not work without a VPN, but local apps don’t need one.
  • Carry backup cash: While apps are convenient, some buses or taxis may only accept cash.

Final Thoughts

Exploring Nanjing’s rich culture and stunning landscapes is much easier with these apps in your pocket. Whether you’re hopping on a metro to Zhongshan Ling or taking a bus to the Yangtze River, these tools ensure smooth and stress-free travels.
