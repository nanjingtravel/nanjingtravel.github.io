Best Parks and Gardens in Nanjing

  • Home
  • Travel Blog
  • Blog Details

Nanjing, a city steeped in history and culture, is also home to some of the most breathtaking parks and gardens in China. Whether you're a nature lover, a history buff, or just looking for a peaceful retreat, Nanjing's green spaces offer something for everyone. From imperial gardens to modern urban oases, here’s your guide to the best parks and gardens in Nanjing.

Xuanwu Lake Park: The Crown Jewel of Nanjing

A Lakeside Paradise

Xuanwu Lake Park is arguably the most famous park in Nanjing, and for good reason. Spanning over 500 hectares, this expansive park centers around the stunning Xuanwu Lake, offering picturesque views, lush greenery, and a serene atmosphere. The lake itself is divided into five islands, each with its own unique charm.

What to Do Here

  • Boat Rides: Rent a paddleboat or take a guided tour to explore the lake’s tranquil waters.
  • Cherry Blossom Avenue: Visit in spring to witness the breathtaking cherry blossoms in full bloom.
  • Ming City Wall: Walk along the ancient city wall that borders the park for a mix of history and nature.

Purple Mountain (Zijin Shan): Where Nature Meets History

A Scenic and Historic Haven

Purple Mountain is not just a park—it’s a sprawling natural and cultural landmark. Home to several historical sites, including the Sun Yat-sen Mausoleum and the Ming Xiaoling Mausoleum, this area is perfect for those who want to combine outdoor exploration with a dose of history.

Must-Visit Spots

  • Sun Yat-sen Mausoleum: Pay respects to the founding father of modern China while enjoying the mountain’s lush surroundings.
  • Linggu Temple: A peaceful Buddhist temple nestled in the forest.
  • Botanical Garden: Part of the mountain’s vast greenery, this garden showcases a diverse collection of plants and flowers.

Mochou Lake Park: A Poetic Retreat

A Park with a Story

Named after the legendary figure Mochou ("No Sorrow"), this park is as poetic as its name suggests. With its elegant pavilions, lotus-filled ponds, and winding pathways, Mochou Lake Park is a favorite among locals and tourists alike.

Highlights

  • Mochou Pavilion: The centerpiece of the park, offering stunning views of the lake.
  • Lotus Festival: If you visit in summer, don’t miss the vibrant lotus blossoms covering the lake.
  • Traditional Tea Houses: Sit back, relax, and enjoy a cup of Nanjing’s famous tea while soaking in the scenery.

Qinhuai River Scenic Area: A Blend of Nature and Culture

The Soul of Nanjing

The Qinhuai River is the lifeblood of Nanjing, and its scenic area is a delightful mix of waterways, gardens, and historic sites. Strolling along the riverbanks, you’ll encounter traditional architecture, lively markets, and beautifully landscaped gardens.

Top Experiences

  • Confucius Temple (Fuzimiao): Explore this historic temple and its surrounding bustling streets.
  • Night Cruise: The river is especially magical at night when illuminated by lanterns.
  • Zhanyuan Garden: A hidden gem within the area, this classical Chinese garden is perfect for quiet reflection.

Nanjing Botanical Garden: A Floral Wonderland

A Paradise for Plant Lovers

As one of the oldest botanical gardens in China, Nanjing Botanical Garden is a must-visit for nature enthusiasts. Divided into two sections—the North Garden and the South Garden—it boasts an impressive variety of flora, including rare and exotic species.

What Not to Miss

  • Tropical Greenhouse: Step into a world of vibrant tropical plants.
  • Plum Blossom Hill: Visit in late winter to see the garden’s famous plum blossoms.
  • Bamboo Forest: A peaceful spot to unwind and enjoy the rustling leaves.

Bailuzhou Park: A Modern Riverside Escape

A Contemporary Green Space

Located along the Yangtze River, Bailuzhou Park is a modern addition to Nanjing’s park scene. With its wide-open spaces, cycling paths, and riverfront views, it’s a great spot for outdoor activities and family picnics.

Activities to Enjoy

  • Cycling and Jogging: The park’s well-maintained paths are perfect for fitness enthusiasts.
  • Riverside Walks: Take a leisurely stroll and enjoy the refreshing breeze from the Yangtze.
  • Sculpture Garden: Admire the artistic installations scattered throughout the park.

Tips for Visiting Nanjing’s Parks

  • Best Time to Visit: Spring (March to May) and autumn (September to November) offer the most pleasant weather.
  • Transportation: Most parks are accessible via Nanjing’s efficient metro system.
  • Entry Fees: While some parks are free, others like Purple Mountain’s attractions may charge a small fee.

Nanjing’s parks and gardens are more than just green spaces—they’re windows into the city’s soul, blending nature, history, and culture in the most enchanting ways. Whether you're wandering through ancient gardens or relaxing by a modern lakeside, these spots promise unforgettable experiences.
