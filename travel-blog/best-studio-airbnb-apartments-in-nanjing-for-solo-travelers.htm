Best Studio Airbnb Apartments in Nanjing for Solo Travelers

  • Home
  • Travel Blog
  • Blog Details

Traveling solo can be one of the most liberating experiences—exploring a new city at your own pace, indulging in spontaneous adventures, and immersing yourself in local culture. Nanjing, a city rich in history and modern charm, offers an incredible blend of ancient landmarks, vibrant nightlife, and serene natural escapes. For solo travelers, finding the perfect accommodation is key—somewhere cozy, safe, and centrally located.

That’s where studio Airbnb apartments come in. Compact yet fully equipped, these spaces provide privacy, convenience, and a homey vibe. Whether you’re a digital nomad, a history buff, or just looking for a weekend getaway, Nanjing has some fantastic options. Here’s a curated list of the best studio Airbnb apartments in Nanjing for solo travelers.

1. Modern Minimalist Studio Near Xinjiekou

Why It’s Great for Solo Travelers

Located in the heart of Nanjing’s shopping and dining district, this studio is perfect for those who want to be steps away from the action. Xinjiekou is Nanjing’s equivalent of Times Square—bustling, bright, and full of energy.

Key Features

  • Prime Location: Walking distance to metro lines 1 and 2, making it easy to explore the city.
  • Stylish Design: Sleek, minimalist decor with a comfortable queen-sized bed and a small kitchenette.
  • Safety First: Secure building with 24/7 surveillance, ideal for solo female travelers.
  • Workspace-Friendly: A compact desk with high-speed Wi-Fi, great for remote workers.

Nearby Attractions

  • Confucius Temple (Fuzimiao): A 10-minute metro ride away.
  • Nanjing Museum: Just two stops on the metro.
  • 1912 Bar Street: Perfect for evening drinks and socializing.

2. Cozy Riverside Studio with a View

Why It’s Great for Solo Travelers

If you prefer a quieter stay with scenic views, this studio along the Qinhuai River is a dream. It’s tucked away from the city’s hustle but still well-connected.

Key Features

  • Panoramic Windows: Wake up to stunning river views every morning.
  • Fully Equipped Kitchen: Ideal for those who enjoy cooking their own meals.
  • Laundry Facilities: A rare find in small studios—great for long-term stays.
  • Bike Rental Access: Explore the riverside trails at your leisure.

Nearby Attractions

  • Zhonghua Gate: A historic fortress just a short walk away.
  • Laomendong: A charming old district with traditional snacks and teahouses.
  • Qinhuai River Night Cruise: A must-do for first-time visitors.

3. Chic Industrial Loft in Gulou District

Why It’s Great for Solo Travelers

Gulou (Drum Tower) is one of Nanjing’s trendiest neighborhoods, packed with cafes, indie boutiques, and live music spots. This loft-style studio is perfect for creative souls and night owls.

Key Features

  • Open-Concept Layout: High ceilings and exposed brick walls give it an artsy vibe.
  • Smart Home Tech: Voice-controlled lights, AC, and entertainment system.
  • Rooftop Access: A shared terrace with city views—great for sunset drinks.
  • Pet-Friendly: If you’re traveling with a furry friend, this one’s a winner.

Nearby Attractions

  • Nanjing University: A short stroll through one of the city’s most picturesque campuses.
  • Red Brick Art Museum: A hidden gem for contemporary art lovers.
  • Craft Beer Bars: The area is known for its microbreweries.

4. Zen-Inspired Studio Near Purple Mountain

Why It’s Great for Solo Travelers

For nature lovers, this tranquil studio near Purple Mountain (Zijin Shan) offers a peaceful retreat while still being close to major attractions.

Key Features

  • Eco-Friendly Design: Bamboo flooring, energy-efficient lighting, and organic toiletries.
  • Private Balcony: Overlooking lush greenery—perfect for morning yoga.
  • Free Bicycle: Explore the mountain trails right from your doorstep.
  • On-Site Café: A small café downstairs serves healthy breakfast options.

Nearby Attractions

  • Sun Yat-sen Mausoleum: A must-visit historical site.
  • Xiaoling Tomb: Part of the Ming Dynasty heritage.
  • Botanical Garden: A serene escape with seasonal blooms.

5. Ultra-Modern Pod-Style Studio in Hexi

Why It’s Great for Solo Travelers

If you love futuristic design and efficiency, this pod-style studio in Hexi (Nanjing’s business district) is a unique pick.

Key Features

  • Space-Saving Layout: Compact but intelligently designed with foldable furniture.
  • High-Tech Amenities: Mood lighting, touchless faucets, and a built-in projector.
  • 24/7 Convenience Store: Right downstairs for late-night snacks.
  • Soundproof Walls: Ideal for light sleepers.

Nearby Attractions

  • Jiangsu Art Museum: A short taxi ride away.
  • Olympic Sports Center: Great for fitness enthusiasts.
  • International Dining: Hexi has some of the best global cuisine in Nanjing.

Tips for Booking the Perfect Studio in Nanjing

Check the Neighborhood

Nanjing is vast, so pick a location based on your interests. Xinjiekou for nightlife, Gulou for culture, or Purple Mountain for nature.

Read Reviews Carefully

Look for mentions of Wi-Fi reliability, noise levels, and host responsiveness—especially important for solo travelers.

Consider Public Transport Access

Nanjing’s metro is efficient, but some areas require transfers. Studios near Line 1 or 2 are the most convenient.

Pack Light

Most studios are compact, so avoid overpacking. Many provide laundry facilities anyway.

Nanjing is a city that effortlessly blends tradition and modernity, making it an exciting destination for solo travelers. Whether you’re here for history, food, or just a change of scenery, these studio Airbnb apartments offer comfort, style, and the perfect base for your adventures. Happy travels!
