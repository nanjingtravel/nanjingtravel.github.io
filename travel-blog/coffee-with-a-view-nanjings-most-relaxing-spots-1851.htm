Coffee with a View: Nanjing’s Most Relaxing Spots

  • Home
  • Travel Blog
  • Blog Details

Nanjing, a city where ancient history meets modern charm, offers some of the most breathtaking spots to enjoy a cup of coffee while soaking in stunning views. Whether you’re a local looking for a quiet escape or a traveler seeking a serene moment, these cafés and scenic spots will leave you refreshed and inspired.

The Perfect Blend: Scenic Cafés in Nanjing

1. Xuanwu Lake Teahouse & Café

Nestled along the shores of Xuanwu Lake, this spot combines traditional Chinese teahouse aesthetics with modern café culture. The floor-to-ceiling windows offer panoramic views of the lake, especially during sunset when the water glows golden. Sip on a locally sourced Yunnan pour-over or a creamy matcha latte while watching boats glide across the water.

Why it’s special:
- Lakeside seating with unobstructed views
- A mix of traditional and contemporary drinks
- Ideal for quiet reflection or casual meetings

2. Zijin Mountain Rooftop Café

Perched near the iconic Zǐjīn Shān (Purple Mountain), this rooftop café is a hidden gem for those who love elevation. The open-air terrace overlooks the sprawling cityscape and the mountain’s lush greenery. Their signature cold brew, infused with osmanthus, is a must-try—refreshing and subtly floral.

Pro tip: Visit in the early morning to avoid crowds and catch the mist rolling over the mountain.

Urban Oases: Cafés with City Views

3. The Confucius Temple Sky Lounge

Located in the heart of the bustling Confucius Temple area, this sky lounge offers a peaceful retreat above the chaos. The modern-industrial décor contrasts beautifully with the historic surroundings. Order their cinnamon-spiced cappuccino and enjoy the bird’s-eye view of the Qinhuai River, especially enchanting at night when the lanterns light up.

Best time to visit: Evening, when the temple area is illuminated.

4. Nanjing Eye Café

Adjacent to the famous Nanjing Eye pedestrian bridge, this glass-walled café provides uninterrupted views of the Yangtze River. The minimalist design lets the scenery take center stage. Try their river breeze iced coffee—a citrusy, sparkling cold brew that’s perfect for warm days.

Bonus: Walk the bridge after your coffee for an unforgettable sunset stroll.

Hidden Gems: Off-the-Beaten-Path Spots

5. Lao Men Dong’s Secret Garden Café

Tucked away in the historic Lao Men Dong district, this café is a tranquil escape with a courtyard filled with blooming wisteria. The rustic charm and slow-paced vibe make it ideal for unwinding. Their honey lavender latte pairs perfectly with the serene atmosphere.

Don’t miss: The homemade rose petal scones.

6. Qixia Mountain Forest Café

For nature lovers, this café deep in Qixia Mountain’s forest is a dream. The wooden deck overlooks a canopy of maple trees, which turn fiery red in autumn. Their smoked cherry mocha is a seasonal favorite, offering warmth and depth with every sip.

Ideal for: A post-hike recharge.

Trendy & Instagram-Worthy: Cafés with a Vibe

7. 1912 District’s Artisan Coffee Lab

In Nanjing’s trendy 1912 area, this café doubles as a coffee workshop. The industrial-chic space features a rotating menu of single-origin brews. The highlight? A deconstructed latte served with a view of the district’s vibrant nightlife.

For the gram: The coffee flight presentation is a visual delight.

8. Heytea’s Scenic Pop-Up

While not a traditional café, Heytea’s seasonal pop-ups near Ming Xiaoling Mausoleum offer limited-edition drinks with a view. Their grapefruit tea, served in eco-friendly packaging, tastes even better against the backdrop of ancient stone statues and towering trees.

Why it’s unique: Combines modern bubble tea culture with historic scenery.

Seasonal Specials: Coffee with a View Year-Round

Spring: Cherry Blossom Café at Mochou Lake

During spring, Mochou Lake’s café sets up outdoor seating under blooming cherry trees. The sakura latte, sprinkled with edible petals, is as picturesque as the surroundings.

Summer: Riverfront Iced Coffee at Jiangxinzhou

This island café serves tropical-inspired iced coffees with a view of the Yangtze’s gentle waves. Perfect for beating the heat.

Autumn: Sunflower Field Café in Gulin

A short trip from downtown, this countryside café overlooks vast sunflower fields. Their pumpkin spice latte pairs beautifully with the golden scenery.

Winter: Fireside Coffee at Tangshan Hot Springs

After a dip in the hot springs, warm up with a spiked mocha at this cozy mountainside café. The steam rising from your cup mirrors the misty springs below.

Whether you’re chasing sunsets, city lights, or forested horizons, Nanjing’s café scene has a view for every mood. So grab your favorite brew, settle into a scenic spot, and let the city’s beauty unfold before you.
