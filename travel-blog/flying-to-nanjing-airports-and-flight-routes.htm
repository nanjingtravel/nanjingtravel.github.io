Flying to Nanjing: Airports and Flight Routes

  • Home
  • Travel Blog
  • Blog Details

Nanjing, the capital of Jiangsu Province, is a city where ancient history and modernity collide. Whether you're drawn to its rich cultural heritage, bustling urban life, or scenic landscapes, getting there is easier than ever thanks to its well-connected aviation network. In this guide, we’ll explore the airports serving Nanjing, major flight routes, and tips for making your journey seamless.

Nanjing’s Primary Airport: Nanjing Lukou International Airport

Overview of Lukou Airport

Nanjing Lukou International Airport (NKG) is the main gateway to the city and one of the busiest airports in Eastern China. Located about 35 kilometers south of downtown Nanjing, it serves as a hub for both domestic and international travelers. The airport boasts modern facilities, including lounges, duty-free shopping, and efficient transportation links.

Terminals and Airlines

Lukou Airport has two terminals:
- Terminal 1: Primarily handles domestic flights.
- Terminal 2: Serves international routes and select domestic carriers.

Major airlines operating here include China Eastern, Air China, and international carriers like Emirates and Lufthansa. Whether you're flying from Beijing, Shanghai, or overseas, chances are you’ll land at Lukou.

Getting to the City Center

Transport options from the airport include:
- Metro: Line S1 connects the airport to Nanjing South Railway Station, with transfers to other metro lines.
- Taxi: A 40-minute ride to downtown, costing around 120-150 RMB.
- Airport Shuttle Buses: Affordable and convenient, with routes covering major districts.

Alternative Airports Near Nanjing

While Lukou is the primary choice, nearby airports can be useful depending on your travel plans.

Hefei Xinqiao International Airport (HFE)

Located about 150 kilometers west of Nanjing, Hefei Xinqiao is a viable alternative if you’re exploring Anhui Province as well. Flight options here might be cheaper, and high-speed trains connect Hefei to Nanjing in under an hour.

Shanghai Pudong International Airport (PVG)

If you’re flying internationally, Shanghai Pudong offers more long-haul options. From there, you can take a 2.5-hour high-speed train to Nanjing.

Popular Flight Routes to Nanjing

Domestic Routes

Nanjing is well-connected to major Chinese cities:
- Beijing to Nanjing: A 2-hour flight, with multiple daily departures.
- Shanghai to Nanjing: Just a 1-hour hop, though many prefer the high-speed train for this route.
- Guangzhou to Nanjing: Around 2.5 hours, with frequent flights by China Southern.

International Routes

While not as extensive as Shanghai or Beijing, Nanjing has growing international connections:
- Tokyo to Nanjing: A 3-hour flight, operated by airlines like Spring Airlines.
- Seoul to Nanjing: Just 2 hours, making it a popular weekend getaway.
- Bangkok to Nanjing: Direct flights take about 4 hours, ideal for Southeast Asian travelers.

Tips for Booking Flights to Nanjing

Best Time to Book

To snag the best deals, book at least 2-3 months in advance, especially for peak seasons like spring (for cherry blossoms) or autumn (for pleasant weather).

Layover Strategies

If you’re flying internationally, consider layovers in major hubs like Shanghai or Beijing. Sometimes, splitting your journey can save money.

Visa Considerations

Most international travelers will need a Chinese visa, but transit visas (24-144 hours) are available if you’re passing through to another destination.

Exploring Nanjing Beyond the Airport

Once you’ve landed, the adventure begins. Here are some must-visit spots:

The Confucius Temple (Fuzimiao)

A historic area along the Qinhuai River, perfect for sampling local snacks and soaking in Nanjing’s cultural vibe.

Sun Yat-sen Mausoleum

A tribute to the founding father of modern China, set against the lush Zijin Mountain.

Xuanwu Lake

A serene escape in the city center, ideal for a leisurely stroll or boat ride.

Final Thoughts on Flying to Nanjing

With its efficient airport, growing flight network, and endless attractions, Nanjing is a destination worth exploring. Whether you’re a history buff, foodie, or business traveler, the city welcomes you with open arms—and a smooth landing at Lukou Airport.
