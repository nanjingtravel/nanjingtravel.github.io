Hidden Gem Rooftop Bars in Nanjing for a Quiet Evening

  • Home
  • Travel Blog
  • Blog Details

Nanjing, a city steeped in history and modern charm, offers more than just ancient temples and bustling markets. For those seeking a serene escape with a view, the city’s hidden rooftop bars provide the perfect backdrop for a quiet evening. These lesser-known spots combine stunning panoramas, crafted cocktails, and an ambiance that feels like a well-kept secret. Whether you’re a local or a traveler looking to unwind, here are some of Nanjing’s best-kept rooftop bar secrets.

The Skyline Whisper: Rooftop Bars Off the Beaten Path

While Nanjing’s popular bars draw crowds, the hidden gems offer intimacy and exclusivity. These spots are tucked away in unassuming buildings, often requiring a bit of exploration to find. The reward? Breathtaking views of the Qinhuai River, the glittering cityscape, or the distant Purple Mountain—all without the noise of tourist-heavy venues.

1. The Cloud Nine Lounge

Location: Near Confucius Temple

Perched atop a boutique hotel, The Cloud Nine Lounge is a sanctuary for those who appreciate understated luxury. The bar’s minimalist design lets the view take center stage, with floor-to-ceiling windows framing the Qinhuai River. By day, it’s a quiet café; by night, it transforms into a mood-lit haven with signature cocktails like the "Purple Mountain Breeze" (a gin-based drink infused with local herbs).

Why it’s special:
- Limited seating ensures privacy.
- Live acoustic music on weeknights.
- A curated menu of tapas inspired by Jiangsu cuisine.

2. The Hidden Lantern

Location: Gulou District

True to its name, The Hidden Lantern is easy to miss—accessed via a discreet elevator in a retrofitted 1920s building. The bar’s lantern-lit terrace overlooks the Gulou area, blending old-world Nanjing with modern mixology. Their "Nanjing Mule" (a twist on the Moscow Mule with ginger-infused baijiu) is a must-try.

Why it’s special:
- Vintage décor with a speakeasy vibe.
- Reservations recommended; capacity is under 20.
- A "secret" cocktail menu for regulars.

Rooftop Bars with a View of History

Nanjing’s skyline tells a story, and these rooftop bars offer front-row seats to the city’s past and present.

3. The Ming Pavilion

Location: Near Zhonghua Gate

Situated on the top floor of a restored Ming-era building, The Ming Pavilion is a love letter to Nanjing’s imperial history. The bar’s terrace faces the ancient city wall, illuminated at night. Sip on a "Dynasty Old Fashioned" (made with aged rice wine) while soaking in the ambiance.

Why it’s special:
- Historical setting with modern comforts.
- Tea-infused cocktails honoring local traditions.
- Private nooks for couples or small groups.

4. The Starry Pagoda

Location: Xuanwu Lake Area

This bar’s name is no exaggeration—its open-air deck offers unobstructed views of Xuanwu Lake and the starry sky. The Starry Pagoda is ideal for stargazers, with telescopes provided for guests. Their "Lakeside Martini" (vodka, chrysanthemum syrup, and a hint of lime) is a refreshing choice.

Why it’s special:
- Astronomy-themed cocktails and décor.
- Cozy blankets provided on cooler evenings.
- A small library of books about Nanjing’s history.

Under-the-Radar Rooftop Bars for Cocktail Connoisseurs

For those who prioritize mixology over crowds, these bars are worth the hunt.

5. The Apothecary

Location: Jianye District

Hidden behind an unmarked door in a high-rise, The Apothecary feels like a VIP club for cocktail enthusiasts. The bartenders, or "liquid chefs," craft drinks using house-made bitters, infused syrups, and rare spirits. Try the "Jianye Sour" (a smoky whiskey sour with a black tea foam).

Why it’s special:
- Seasonal menus based on local ingredients.
- Interactive cocktail workshops by reservation.
- A strict no-photos policy to preserve the vibe.

6. The Bamboo Grove

Location: Near Nanjing University

Inspired by the scholarly gardens of ancient China, The Bamboo Grove is a rooftop oasis with bamboo-lined walkways and quiet corners. Their "Scholar’s Cup" (a gin cocktail with bamboo leaf infusion) pairs perfectly with the bar’s book-lined shelves.

Why it’s special:
- A "silent hour" policy (no loud groups).
- A small menu of vegan bites.
- Monthly poetry readings.

Tips for Enjoying Nanjing’s Hidden Rooftop Bars

  • Timing is key: Arrive just before sunset to secure the best seats.
  • Dress code: While most spots are casual, some upscale bars prefer smart-casual attire.
  • Reservations: Many hidden bars have limited space—book ahead if possible.
  • Explore nearby: Pair your evening with a visit to a quiet teahouse or a night stroll along the Qinhuai River.

Nanjing’s rooftop bar scene is a treasure trove for those willing to look beyond the obvious. Whether you’re drawn to history, mixology, or simply a peaceful evening under the stars, these hidden gems promise an unforgettable experience.
