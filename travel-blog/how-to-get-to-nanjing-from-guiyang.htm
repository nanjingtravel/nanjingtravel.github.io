How to Get to Nanjing from Guiyang

  • Home
  • Travel Blog
  • Blog Details

Nanjing, the historic capital of China, is a must-visit destination for travelers seeking a blend of ancient culture and modern vibrancy. If you're starting your journey in Guiyang, the lush capital of Guizhou Province, you’re in for an exciting adventure. This guide covers everything you need to know about traveling from Guiyang to Nanjing, including transportation options, scenic stops, and insider tips to make your trip unforgettable.

Transportation Options from Guiyang to Nanjing

By Air: The Fastest Route

Flying is the quickest way to get from Guiyang to Nanjing. Guiyang Longdongbao International Airport (KWE) offers direct flights to Nanjing Lukou International Airport (NKG), with a flight time of approximately 2.5 hours. Airlines like China Eastern, Air China, and Juneyao Air operate this route frequently.

Pro Tip: Book your tickets in advance, especially during peak travel seasons like Chinese New Year or the National Day holiday, to secure the best deals.

By High-Speed Train: Scenic and Comfortable

For those who prefer land travel, high-speed trains are an excellent option. The journey takes around 8–10 hours, depending on the train type. Departing from Guiyang North Railway Station, trains arrive at Nanjing South Railway Station, a major hub with easy access to the city center.

Why Choose the Train?
- Enjoy breathtaking views of the countryside, including the Yangtze River as you approach Nanjing.
- More legroom and comfort compared to flights.
- Eco-friendly travel with a lower carbon footprint.

By Bus or Car: For the Adventurous

While less common, long-distance buses or self-driving are options for travelers who love road trips. The distance is roughly 1,500 kilometers, and the drive takes about 18–20 hours. This route is ideal for those who want to explore smaller towns along the way, such as Changsha or Wuhan.

Caution: Be prepared for tolls, rest stops, and varying road conditions. Renting a car with a GPS is highly recommended.

Must-Visit Attractions in Nanjing

The Ming Xiaoling Mausoleum

A UNESCO World Heritage Site, this majestic tomb complex is the final resting place of the Hongwu Emperor, founder of the Ming Dynasty. The Sacred Way, lined with stone statues of animals and officials, is a highlight.

Confucius Temple (Fuzimiao)

Located in the Qinhuai Scenic Area, this temple is surrounded by lively markets, street food stalls, and traditional architecture. Don’t miss the Qinhuai River night cruise for a magical view of illuminated bridges and pagodas.

Sun Yat-sen Mausoleum

Dedicated to the founding father of modern China, this mausoleum sits atop Purple Mountain. The 392-step climb rewards visitors with panoramic views of Nanjing.

Nanjing Massacre Memorial Hall

A sobering but essential visit, this museum commemorates the victims of the 1937 Nanjing Massacre. It’s a powerful reminder of history’s impact.

Foodie Hotspots in Nanjing

Nanjing Salted Duck

A local delicacy, this dish is known for its tender meat and subtle salty flavor. Try it at Laomendong or any reputable restaurant near Confucius Temple.

Duck Blood and Vermicelli Soup

A hearty street food favorite, this soup combines duck blood, tofu, and vermicelli in a rich broth. Perfect for chilly days!

Tangbao (Soup Dumplings)

Unlike Shanghai’s xiaolongbao, Nanjing’s tangbao are larger and filled with a savory broth. Pair them with vinegar and ginger for the full experience.

Seasonal Travel Tips

Spring (March–May)

  • Cherry blossoms at Jiming Temple and Xuanwu Lake are a must-see.
  • Light jackets are recommended as temperatures can fluctuate.

Summer (June–August)

  • Expect heat and humidity. Stay hydrated and visit indoor attractions during midday.
  • The Lotus Festival at Mochou Lake is a summer highlight.

Autumn (September–November)

  • Purple Mountain’s foliage is stunning. Ideal for hiking and photography.
  • The Mid-Autumn Festival brings lantern displays to Confucius Temple.

Winter (December–February)

  • Snow-covered cityscapes are rare but magical.
  • Warm up with hotpot or a bowl of duck soup.

Budget-Friendly Travel Hacks

  1. Use Public Transport: Nanjing’s metro system is efficient and cheap. A day pass costs around ¥15.
  2. Free Attractions: Many parks, like Xuanwu Lake Park, are free to enter.
  3. Student Discounts: Bring your ISIC card for discounts at museums and historical sites.

Final Thoughts

Whether you choose to fly, take the train, or embark on a road trip, the journey from Guiyang to Nanjing is filled with opportunities for discovery. From imperial tombs to bustling food streets, Nanjing offers a travel experience that’s both enriching and exhilarating. Pack your bags, grab your camera, and get ready to explore one of China’s most captivating cities!
