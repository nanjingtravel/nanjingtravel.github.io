How to Get to Nanjing Olympic Sports Center by Bus

  • Home
  • Travel Blog
  • Blog Details

Nanjing Olympic Sports Center is one of the city’s most iconic landmarks, hosting major sports events, concerts, and cultural activities. Whether you're a sports enthusiast, a music lover, or just exploring Nanjing’s vibrant scene, knowing how to get there by bus is essential for a smooth journey. Here’s everything you need to know about navigating Nanjing’s public transport to reach this hotspot.

Why Visit Nanjing Olympic Sports Center?

Before diving into the bus routes, let’s talk about why this destination is worth the trip. The center was built for the 2005 National Games of China and has since become a hub for entertainment and fitness. Its striking architecture, including the lotus-shaped stadium, makes it a photographer’s dream. Nearby, you’ll find shopping malls, cafes, and even a scenic lakeside park—perfect for a full day of exploration.

Understanding Nanjing’s Bus System

Nanjing’s bus network is extensive and affordable, making it a great option for travelers. Buses run frequently, and many routes connect directly to the Olympic Sports Center. Here’s what you need to know:

Payment Methods

  • Yikatong Card: The city’s rechargeable transit card (also works for the metro).
  • Mobile Payments: Apps like Alipay or WeChat Pay can be used on most buses.
  • Cash: Exact change is required (typically ¥2 per ride).

Bus Etiquette

  • Priority seats are reserved for seniors and pregnant women.
  • Avoid rush hours (7:30–9:00 AM and 5:00–7:00 PM) for a more comfortable ride.

Top Bus Routes to Nanjing Olympic Sports Center

From Nanjing Railway Station

If you’re arriving by train, hop on Bus 7 or Bus 166. Both stop near the stadium’s east gate. The ride takes about 30 minutes, depending on traffic.

Pro Tip: If you’re carrying luggage, opt for Bus 166—it’s less crowded than Bus 7 during peak times.

From Xinjiekou (Downtown Nanjing)

Xinjiekou is Nanjing’s bustling commercial heart. To reach the Olympic Sports Center:
- Take Bus 109 from Zhujiang Road Station.
- Alternatively, Bus 129 from Gulou offers a scenic route past Xuanwu Lake.

Both options take roughly 25–40 minutes.

From Nanjing South Railway Station

For high-speed rail arrivals, Bus 129 is your best bet. It’s a direct route with no transfers, taking about 50 minutes. If you’re in a hurry, consider taking the metro (Line 1 to Line 10) and switching to a bus for the last mile.

Insider Tips for a Smooth Ride

Avoid Traffic Jams

Nanjing’s traffic can be unpredictable. Use apps like Gaode Maps or Baidu Maps for real-time updates. Midday (10:00 AM–4:00 PM) is usually the least congested time to travel.

Explore Nearby Attractions

Since you’re already in the area, why not check out these spots?
- Yuantong Lake: A peaceful retreat with walking trails.
- Heyuan Shopping Mall: Great for souvenirs and local snacks.

Night Bus Options

If you’re attending an evening event, don’t worry—buses like Night 7 and Night 166 run until midnight. Just double-check the schedule in advance.

Final Thoughts

Getting to Nanjing Olympic Sports Center by bus is convenient, budget-friendly, and a great way to see the city. With multiple routes and easy payment options, you’ll have no trouble joining the crowds at the next big game or concert. Pack your Yikatong card, plan your route, and enjoy the ride!
