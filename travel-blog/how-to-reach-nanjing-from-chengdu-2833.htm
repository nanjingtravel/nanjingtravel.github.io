How to Reach Nanjing from Chengdu

  • Home
  • Travel Blog
  • Blog Details

Nanjing, the ancient capital of China, is a city brimming with history, culture, and modern charm. If you're starting your journey from Chengdu, the land of pandas and spicy hotpot, you’re in for an exciting adventure. Whether you're a history buff, a foodie, or just looking to explore, this guide will walk you through the best ways to travel from Chengdu to Nanjing, along with must-see attractions and insider tips.

By Air: The Fastest Option

Flying is the quickest and most convenient way to get from Chengdu to Nanjing. Several airlines operate daily flights between Chengdu Shuangliu International Airport (CTU) and Nanjing Lukou International Airport (NKG).

Flight Details

  • Duration: Approximately 2.5 hours
  • Airlines: Air China, Sichuan Airlines, China Eastern, and more
  • Frequency: Multiple flights per day
  • Cost: Prices range from $80 to $200, depending on the season and booking time

Tips for Flying

  • Book in advance to secure the best deals.
  • Check for layovers if you’re on a budget—some flights may stop in cities like Chongqing or Wuhan.
  • Arrive at the airport early, especially during peak travel seasons like Chinese New Year or Golden Week.

By High-Speed Train: Scenic and Comfortable

If you prefer to enjoy the countryside views, taking a high-speed train is an excellent choice. The journey offers a glimpse of China’s diverse landscapes, from Sichuan’s lush mountains to the flat plains of Jiangsu.

Train Details

  • Duration: Around 10–12 hours
  • Departure: Chengdu East Railway Station
  • Arrival: Nanjing South Railway Station
  • Cost: $60–$120 for second-class seats; $180–$250 for first-class

Why Take the Train?

  • More legroom and comfort compared to flights.
  • No baggage weight restrictions.
  • Stops at major cities like Chongqing and Wuhan, giving you a chance to stretch your legs.

By Regular Train: Budget-Friendly but Longer

For those who aren’t in a hurry, an overnight sleeper train can be a unique experience. It’s cheaper but takes significantly longer.

Train Details

  • Duration: 20–25 hours
  • Cost: $30–$80 for hard sleeper; $60–$120 for soft sleeper
  • Experience: A great way to meet locals and enjoy slow travel

Exploring Nanjing: Top Attractions

Once you arrive in Nanjing, here are some must-visit spots:

The Sun Yat-sen Mausoleum

A tribute to Dr. Sun Yat-sen, the founding father of modern China, this mausoleum sits atop the Purple Mountain and offers breathtaking views.

Confucius Temple (Fuzimiao)

A lively area filled with street food, traditional architecture, and boat rides along the Qinhuai River.

Nanjing Massacre Memorial Hall

A somber but important historical site that commemorates the victims of the Nanjing Massacre.

Xuanwu Lake

A serene escape in the heart of the city, perfect for a leisurely stroll or a boat ride.

Food You Must Try in Nanjing

Nanjing’s cuisine is a delightful mix of sweet and savory flavors. Don’t miss:

  • Nanjing Salted Duck: A local specialty with tender, flavorful meat.
  • Duck Blood and Vermicelli Soup: A unique dish that’s surprisingly delicious.
  • Tangbao (Soup Dumplings): Juicy dumplings filled with rich broth.

Best Time to Visit Nanjing

  • Spring (March–May): Pleasant weather and blooming cherry blossoms.
  • Autumn (September–November): Cool temperatures and golden foliage.
  • Avoid summer if you dislike humidity, as Nanjing can get quite hot.

Final Travel Tips

  • Language: While Mandarin is widely spoken, having a translation app can help.
  • Transport: Nanjing’s metro system is efficient and easy to navigate.
  • Currency: Have some cash (RMB) on hand, though digital payments like WeChat Pay are widely accepted.

Now that you know how to get from Chengdu to Nanjing, all that’s left is to pack your bags and embark on your journey!
