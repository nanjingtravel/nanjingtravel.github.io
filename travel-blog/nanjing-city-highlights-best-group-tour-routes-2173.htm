Nanjing City Highlights – Best Group Tour Routes

  • Home
  • Travel Blog
  • Blog Details

Nanjing, the ancient capital of China, is a city where history and modernity blend seamlessly. With its rich cultural heritage, stunning natural landscapes, and vibrant urban life, it’s a perfect destination for group tours. Whether you're traveling with family, friends, or colleagues, Nanjing offers a variety of routes that cater to different interests. Below, we’ve curated the best group tour routes to help you explore the city’s top attractions efficiently and enjoyably.

Historical & Cultural Exploration Route

For groups fascinated by history and culture, this route covers Nanjing’s most iconic landmarks.

Sun Yat-sen Mausoleum

Start your journey at the Sun Yat-sen Mausoleum, a tribute to Dr. Sun Yat-sen, the founding father of modern China. The grand architecture and serene surroundings make it a must-visit. The 392-step climb to the top offers a panoramic view of the Purple Mountain area.

Ming Xiaoling Mausoleum

Next, head to the Ming Xiaoling Mausoleum, the resting place of the Hongwu Emperor, founder of the Ming Dynasty. The Sacred Way, lined with stone statues of animals and officials, is a highlight. The tranquil atmosphere here is perfect for reflection.

Presidential Palace

Wrap up your historical tour at the Presidential Palace, a site that witnessed pivotal moments in China’s modern history. The blend of Chinese and Western architectural styles tells the story of Nanjing’s political evolution.

Scenic & Nature Lovers’ Route

If your group prefers natural beauty, this route showcases Nanjing’s picturesque landscapes.

Xuanwu Lake

Begin at Xuanwu Lake, a sprawling urban lake surrounded by lush greenery. Rent a paddleboat or take a leisurely stroll along the lakeside paths. The lake is especially stunning during cherry blossom season in spring.

Purple Mountain (Zijin Shan)

Next, explore Purple Mountain, a verdant retreat dotted with historical sites. Take the cable car for breathtaking views, or hike to the peak for a more immersive experience. Don’t miss the Linggu Temple and its iconic Beamless Hall.

Qinhuai River

End your day with a boat ride along the Qinhuai River, especially enchanting at night when the lanterns illuminate the water. The surrounding Confucius Temple area offers lively street food and souvenir stalls.

Modern & Urban Experience Route

For groups who enjoy contemporary attractions, this route highlights Nanjing’s modern side.

Nanjing Eye Pedestrian Bridge

Start at the Nanjing Eye, a futuristic pedestrian bridge spanning the Yangtze River. The LED-lit structure is a marvel at night, offering stunning skyline views.

Deji Plaza & Xinjiekou

Next, head to Xinjiekou, Nanjing’s bustling commercial hub. Deji Plaza is a shopper’s paradise, featuring luxury brands and gourmet dining. The area’s vibrant energy is contagious.

1912 Art District

End your urban adventure at the 1912 Art District, a trendy area filled with bars, cafes, and galleries. The blend of historic architecture and modern nightlife makes it a great spot for group photos and relaxation.

Foodie & Market Exploration Route

No group tour is complete without indulging in local flavors. This route is all about Nanjing’s culinary delights.

Confucius Temple Snack Street

Kick off your food journey at Confucius Temple Snack Street. Try Nanjing’s famous salted duck (Yanshui Ya), duck blood vermicelli soup, and sesame pancakes. The lively atmosphere is perfect for group bonding.

Lao Men Dong

Next, visit Lao Men Dong, a historic neighborhood turned foodie hotspot. Sample traditional snacks like tangbao (soup dumplings) and glutinous rice cakes while soaking in the old-world charm.

Night Market at Fuzimiao

End your culinary tour at the Fuzimiao Night Market, where street vendors serve everything from grilled skewers to sweet desserts. It’s a fantastic place to pick up souvenirs and enjoy Nanjing’s nightlife.

Family-Friendly Group Route

Traveling with kids? This route ensures fun for all ages.

Hongshan Forest Zoo

Start at Hongshan Forest Zoo, home to pandas, giraffes, and interactive animal shows. The zoo’s spacious layout and playgrounds make it ideal for families.

Nanjing Underwater World

Next, dive into Nanjing Underwater World, where kids can marvel at colorful fish, jellyfish, and even shark tunnels. The touch pools are a hit with younger visitors.

Nanjing Happy Valley

End your day at Nanjing Happy Valley, an amusement park with thrilling rides and live performances. The themed zones cater to both adrenaline seekers and younger children.

Tips for Group Tours in Nanjing

  • Transportation: Opt for a chartered bus or metro for convenience.
  • Guides: Hiring a local guide enhances the experience with insider knowledge.
  • Timing: Visit popular sites early to avoid crowds.
  • Weather: Spring and autumn offer the most pleasant climate for touring.

Nanjing’s diverse attractions ensure that every group finds something to love. Whether you’re history buffs, nature enthusiasts, or food lovers, these routes will make your trip unforgettable.
