Nanjing High-Speed Train Food & Dining Options

  • Home
  • Travel Blog
  • Blog Details

Nanjing, a city steeped in history and modernity, is a major hub for China’s high-speed rail network. Whether you’re passing through or starting your journey here, the dining options aboard Nanjing’s high-speed trains and at its stations are worth exploring. From quick snacks to full meals, let’s dive into the flavors you can enjoy while traveling at 300 km/h.

The High-Speed Rail Dining Experience

Onboard Meal Services

Most high-speed trains in China, including those departing from Nanjing, offer onboard dining services. The options vary depending on the train class and duration of the trip.

  1. Dining Car Meals – Many trains feature a dedicated dining car where passengers can enjoy hot meals. The menu typically includes Chinese staples like braised pork rice (红烧肉饭), kung pao chicken (宫保鸡丁), and vegetable stir-fries. Some trains even offer regional specialties, so keep an eye out for Nanjing-inspired dishes.

  2. Pre-Ordered Meals – On select routes, passengers can pre-order meals when booking tickets. These meals are often fresher and come with more variety, including vegetarian and halal options.

  3. Snack Carts – If you’re not in the mood for a full meal, attendants frequently push snack carts through the cabins. You’ll find instant noodles, chips, bottled drinks, and even small desserts.

Premium Class Dining

First-class and business-class passengers often receive complimentary meals or upgraded dining options. These may include:
- A multi-course meal with appetizers, main dishes, and dessert.
- Higher-quality ingredients, such as seafood or premium cuts of meat.
- A selection of teas, juices, and sometimes even small bottles of wine.

Nanjing South Station: A Food Lover’s Pit Stop

Nanjing South Railway Station is one of the busiest high-speed rail hubs in China, and its food options reflect that. Whether you have 10 minutes or an hour before your train, here’s where to grab a bite.

Fast Food & Chain Restaurants

  1. KFC & McDonald’s – Familiar Western fast food is available for those craving a quick burger or fried chicken.
  2. Dicos (德克士) – A popular Chinese fast-food chain offering fried chicken, rice bowls, and burgers.
  3. Zhen Gongfu (真功夫) – Specializing in steamed dishes and healthier fast-food options.

Local Nanjing Flavors

If you want a taste of Nanjing before boarding, try these spots:
- Nanjing Salted Duck (盐水鸭) – A must-try local delicacy, available at several station vendors.
- Xiaolongbao (小笼包) – Steamed soup dumplings, perfect for a quick yet satisfying meal.
- Duck Blood Vermicelli Soup (鸭血粉丝汤) – A hearty, flavorful dish that’s a Nanjing staple.

Convenience Stores & Snacks

For those in a rush, convenience stores like FamilyMart and 7-Eleven offer:
- Pre-packaged sandwiches and sushi.
- Hot tea eggs and steamed buns.
- A variety of chips, chocolates, and drinks for the journey.

Tips for Dining on Nanjing High-Speed Trains

  1. Bring Cash or Mobile Payment – While most vendors accept WeChat Pay and Alipay, having some cash ensures you won’t go hungry if there’s a technical issue.
  2. Check Meal Times – Dining cars may close between peak hours, so plan accordingly.
  3. Try Local Specialties – If you see a Nanjing dish on the menu, give it a try—it might be your only chance to taste it on the go!
  4. Pack Light Snacks – Trains are fast, but delays happen. Having a backup snack is always smart.

Final Thoughts

Traveling by high-speed rail in Nanjing is not just about speed—it’s also an opportunity to savor delicious food along the way. Whether you’re indulging in a full meal onboard or grabbing a quick bite at the station, the culinary options make the journey even more enjoyable. Next time you’re at Nanjing South Station or aboard a high-speed train, take a moment to explore the flavors that make this experience uniquely satisfying.
