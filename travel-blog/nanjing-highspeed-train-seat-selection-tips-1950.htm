Nanjing High-Speed Train Seat Selection Tips

  • Home
  • Travel Blog
  • Blog Details

Traveling by high-speed train in Nanjing is one of the most efficient and scenic ways to explore China’s bustling cities and picturesque countryside. Whether you’re heading to Shanghai, Beijing, or other destinations, choosing the right seat can make a huge difference in your journey. Here’s everything you need to know to pick the perfect seat on a Nanjing high-speed train.

Understanding Nanjing High-Speed Train Classes

Before selecting a seat, it’s essential to understand the different classes available on Chinese high-speed trains. Each class offers varying levels of comfort, space, and amenities.

1. Business Class (Shangwu Zuo)

Business Class is the most luxurious option, featuring spacious, reclining seats arranged in a 2-2 configuration. Passengers enjoy premium services like complimentary snacks, drinks, and sometimes even access to exclusive lounges at major stations like Nanjing South Railway Station.

2. First Class (Yideng Zuo)

First Class provides a comfortable ride with wider seats (2-2 configuration) and more legroom than Second Class. It’s a great choice for travelers who want extra comfort without the high price of Business Class.

3. Second Class (Erdeng Zuo)

The most common and budget-friendly option, Second Class seats are arranged in a 3-2 configuration. While slightly more crowded, they still offer decent legroom and amenities like power outlets.

4. Premium Soft Sleeper (Gaotie Ruanwo)

Available on some overnight high-speed routes, these cabins offer private compartments with beds, perfect for long-distance travel.

Best Seat Selection Strategies

Picking the right seat depends on your travel needs—whether you prioritize views, legroom, or convenience.

Window Seats vs. Aisle Seats

  • Window Seats (A or F) – Ideal for sightseeing, especially on scenic routes like Nanjing to Hangzhou.
  • Aisle Seats (C or D) – Better for those who prefer easy access to restrooms or frequent movement.

Front vs. Back of the Train

  • Front Rows – Quieter with less foot traffic, great for business travelers.
  • Back Rows – Often near restrooms, which can be convenient but noisier.

Seats Near Power Outlets

If you need to charge devices, look for seats near power outlets, typically found:
- In First Class and Business Class.
- Near the ends of Second Class carriages.

How to Book the Best Seat

1. Use Official Booking Platforms

Websites like 12306.cn or apps like Trip.com allow seat selection during booking. Look for visual seat maps when choosing tickets.

2. Opt for Early Booking

Popular routes (e.g., Nanjing to Shanghai) sell out fast, especially during holidays like Golden Week. Booking early ensures better seat options.

3. Consider Seat Preferences When Traveling in Groups

  • Families: Choose a block of seats (e.g., D-F in Second Class).
  • Solo travelers: Window seats for privacy.

Pro Tips for a Smooth Ride

  • Arrive Early at Nanjing South Station – High-speed trains depart precisely on time, and security lines can be long.
  • Pack Light – Overhead storage is limited, especially in Second Class.
  • Bring Snacks – While some trains offer food, options may be limited.

Hidden Perks of Nanjing High-Speed Trains

  • Free Wi-Fi (on select trains).
  • Quiet Carriages – Some trains have designated quiet zones.
  • Scenic Routes – The Nanjing to Huangshan route offers breathtaking mountain views.

By following these tips, you’ll ensure a comfortable and enjoyable high-speed train journey from Nanjing. Whether you’re a frequent traveler or a first-time rider, the right seat can turn your trip into a seamless experience.
