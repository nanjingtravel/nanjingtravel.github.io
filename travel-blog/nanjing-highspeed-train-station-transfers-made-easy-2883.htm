Nanjing High-Speed Train Station Transfers Made Easy

  • Home
  • Travel Blog
  • Blog Details

Nanjing, the capital of Jiangsu Province, is a bustling metropolis rich in history and modern conveniences. As a major transportation hub in eastern China, Nanjing’s high-speed train stations—Nanjing South Station (南京南站) and Nanjing Station (南京站)—serve millions of travelers annually. Navigating these stations efficiently can make or break your travel experience. Whether you're a first-time visitor or a frequent traveler, this guide will help you master transfers between trains, metro lines, taxis, and buses with ease.

Why Nanjing’s High-Speed Rail Network Matters

Nanjing is a critical node on China’s high-speed rail network, connecting major cities like Shanghai, Beijing, Hangzhou, and Wuhan. The city’s two primary stations cater to different routes:

  • Nanjing South Station (南京南站): The larger and more modern of the two, serving high-speed trains to southern and eastern destinations.
  • Nanjing Station (南京站): Located closer to the city center, handling both high-speed and conventional trains, with easy access to scenic spots like Xuanwu Lake.

Understanding the differences between these stations and how to transfer between them (or to other transport options) is key to a smooth journey.

Seamless Transfers at Nanjing South Station

Metro Connections

Nanjing South Station is seamlessly integrated with Metro Lines 1, 3, and S1, making it incredibly convenient for travelers heading into the city or to nearby attractions like Confucius Temple (夫子庙) or Zhonghua Gate (中华门).

  • Line 1 (Blue Line): Runs north-south, connecting to Xinjiekou (Nanjing’s shopping district) and Nanjing Station.
  • Line 3 (Orange Line): Covers eastern and western routes, useful for reaching areas like Nanjing University.
  • S1 Line (Airport Line): Direct access to Nanjing Lukou International Airport (NKG).

Pro Tip: If you’re transferring from a high-speed train to the metro, follow the clearly marked signs—the stations are directly connected, so no need to exit and re-enter.

Taxi and Ride-Hailing Services

Taxis are readily available outside the station’s south and north exits. Apps like Didi (China’s Uber equivalent) are also widely used.

  • Avoid Scams: Always insist on using the meter or book through an app to avoid overcharging.
  • Peak Hours: Expect longer waits during rush hours (7–9 AM and 5–7 PM).

Bus Transfers

Nanjing South Station has a dedicated bus hub with routes covering major districts:

  • Y1 Tourist Bus: Stops at Sun Yat-sen Mausoleum and Ming Xiaoling Mausoleum.
  • Route 190: Connects to Nanjing Railway Station for onward travel.

Navigating Nanjing Station Transfers

Metro Links

Nanjing Station is served by Metro Lines 1 and 3, providing quick access to downtown and tourist hotspots.

  • Line 1: Takes you to Xinjiekou in under 10 minutes.
  • Line 3: Useful for reaching Nanjing Museum or the Presidential Palace.

Walking to Xuanwu Lake

One of the best perks of Nanjing Station is its proximity to Xuanwu Lake, a scenic spot perfect for stretching your legs after a long train ride. The lake is just a 5-minute walk from the station’s east exit.

Taxi and Bus Options

  • Taxis: Available at the east and west squares; queues can get long, so consider ride-hailing apps.
  • Buses: Multiple routes (e.g., Bus 13, 33) connect to key areas like Gulou (Drum Tower) and Nanjing University.

Transferring Between Nanjing South and Nanjing Station

If you need to switch stations, here are your best options:

By Metro

Take Line 1 directly between Nanjing South Station and Nanjing Station (about 25 minutes).

By Taxi

A 30–40 minute ride depending on traffic; costs around ¥50–70.

By High-Speed Train

Yes, you can take a high-speed train between the two stations! Some Shanghai-bound trains stop at both, with a travel time of just 8–10 minutes.

Insider Tips for Stress-Free Transfers

  1. Download Offline Maps: Apps like Amap (高德地图) or Baidu Maps (百度地图) work even without Wi-Fi.
  2. Use Station Lockers: Both stations offer luggage storage (¥5–20 per bag), great for short layovers.
  3. Avoid Rush Hour: Metro lines get packed—travel mid-morning or early afternoon if possible.
  4. Check Train Schedules in Advance: Use 12306.cn or apps like Trip.com to plan connections.

Exploring Nanjing During a Layover

If you have a few hours to spare, consider these quick trips:

From Nanjing South Station

  • Confucius Temple (夫子庙): 15 minutes by Metro Line 3.
  • Zhonghua Gate (中华门): A stunning Ming Dynasty gate just 10 minutes away.

From Nanjing Station

  • Xuanwu Lake: Perfect for a lakeside stroll or coffee break.
  • Jiming Temple: A historic Buddhist temple 10 minutes by taxi.

Final Thoughts

Mastering transfers at Nanjing’s high-speed train stations opens up endless travel possibilities. With efficient metro links, reliable taxis, and well-planned bus routes, getting around is easier than ever. Whether you're rushing to catch a connecting train or taking time to explore Nanjing’s rich culture, these tips will ensure a hassle-free journey.

Now, go forth and travel like a pro—Nanjing awaits!
