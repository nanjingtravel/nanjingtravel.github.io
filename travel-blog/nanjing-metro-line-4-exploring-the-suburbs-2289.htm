Nanjing Metro Line 4: Exploring the Suburbs

  • Home
  • Travel Blog
  • Blog Details

Nanjing Metro Line 4 is more than just a transit route—it’s a gateway to the city’s lesser-known gems. Stretching from Longjiang to Xianlinhu, this line connects urban buzz with suburban tranquility, offering travelers a chance to explore hidden parks, cultural landmarks, and local flavors. Whether you're a history buff, a nature lover, or a foodie, Line 4 has something for everyone.

Why Ride Nanjing Metro Line 4?

Unlike the crowded downtown lines, Metro Line 4 provides a relaxed journey through Nanjing’s evolving suburbs. The trains are spacious, the stations are modern, and the views outside the windows shift from skyscrapers to tree-lined streets. It’s perfect for a weekend escape or a slow-paced adventure.

Key Stops to Explore

1. Longjiang Station: Where Culture Meets Convenience

Located near the bustling Hexi district, Longjiang Station is a great starting point. Within walking distance, you’ll find:
- Nanjing Art Museum – A hub for contemporary Chinese art.
- Qingliangshan Park – A peaceful retreat with lush greenery and historic pagodas.
- Local Cafés – Perfect for a quick coffee before hopping back on the train.

2. Gulou Station: The Heart of Old Nanjing

Though technically on Line 1, Gulou is a transfer point for Line 4 and worth a detour. Highlights include:
- Drum Tower (Gulou) – A Ming Dynasty landmark with panoramic city views.
- Nanjing University – Stroll through its historic campus for a scholarly vibe.
- Laomendong – A food street where you can try Nanjing’s famous salted duck.

3. Xianlin Station: University Town Vibes

Home to several major universities, Xianlin has a youthful energy. Don’t miss:
- Xianlin Bookstore – A cozy spot for book lovers.
- Student Night Markets – Affordable street food and trendy trinkets.
- Biking Trails – Rent a bike and explore the scenic campus roads.

4. Xianlinhu Station: Lakeside Serenity

The final stop on Line 4 is a hidden paradise for nature lovers:
- Xianlin Lake – Ideal for picnics, kayaking, or sunset walks.
- Forest Trails – Hiking paths with minimal crowds.
- Weekend Farmers’ Markets – Fresh produce and handmade crafts.

Hidden Gems Along the Route

Jiangwangmiao Station: A Foodie’s Dream

This lesser-known stop is a goldmine for authentic Nanjing cuisine. Try:
- Duck Blood Vermicelli Soup – A local specialty.
- Pan-Fried Dumplings (Shengjianbao) – Crispy on the outside, juicy inside.
- Tea Houses – Sip on Biluochun, a famous Nanjing green tea.

Zhujianglu Station: Shopping & History

A short walk from the station leads to:
- 1912 District – A trendy area with bars, boutiques, and live music.
- Presidential Palace – A must-visit for history enthusiasts.

Tips for Riding Metro Line 4

  • Avoid Rush Hour – Trains are quieter mid-morning or early afternoon.
  • Use a Transit Card – Saves time and offers small discounts.
  • Download a Metro Map App – Helps with transfers and station exits.

Final Thoughts

Nanjing Metro Line 4 isn’t just about getting from point A to B—it’s an invitation to slow down and discover the city’s quieter, more authentic side. Whether you're exploring lakeside trails, digging into street food, or wandering through historic sites, this line proves that sometimes the best adventures are just a subway ride away.
