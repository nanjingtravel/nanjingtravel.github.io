Nanjing Subway Map: How to Use the Metro Card

  • Home
  • Travel Blog
  • Blog Details

Nanjing’s metro system is a traveler’s best friend—efficient, affordable, and incredibly user-friendly. Whether you’re visiting the majestic Sun Yat-sen Mausoleum, strolling along the Qinhuai River, or shopping in Xinjiekou, the subway is the fastest way to navigate the city. But to truly master it, you’ll need to understand how to use the Nanjing Metro Card. Here’s everything you need to know to ride like a local.

Why the Nanjing Metro Card is a Must-Have

Convenience at Your Fingertips

Forget fumbling for change or waiting in long ticket lines. The Nanjing Metro Card (or "Jinlingtong" card) lets you tap and go at every station. It’s rechargeable, reusable, and works on buses, taxis, and even some convenience stores.

Cost-Effective Travel

Single-journey tickets add up quickly, especially if you’re hopping between attractions like Confucius Temple and Nanjing Museum. The metro card offers discounted fares, and transfers between lines are often cheaper than buying separate tickets.

Tourist Perks

Some cards come with special designs featuring Nanjing’s landmarks, making them a fun souvenir. Plus, certain tourist passes include unlimited rides for a set number of days—perfect for explorers.

How to Get Your Nanjing Metro Card

Where to Buy

  • Metro Stations: Look for ticket counters or automated kiosks (English options available).
  • Convenience Stores: Select FamilyMart or Suguo locations sell them.
  • Online: Platforms like Taobao offer themed cards, but you’ll need to recharge them in person.

Types of Cards

  1. Standard Card: Pay a ¥20 deposit (refundable) and load it with credit.
  2. Tourist Pass: Unlimited rides for 1/3/7 days (ideal for short stays).
  3. Mobile Options: Alipay or WeChat Pay’s "Metro QR Code" works, but physical cards are more reliable offline.

How to Use the Card Step-by-Step

Entering the Station

  1. Tap In: Hold your card over the sensor at the turnstile (look for the NFC symbol).
  2. Green Light = Go: The gate opens—don’t hesitate, or it might close on you!

Transferring Between Lines

Nanjing’s metro map is color-coded. For example:
- Line 1 (Blue): Connects Nanjing Station to Xuanwumen.
- Line 3 (Green): Runs near the Presidential Palace.
Transfers are free within 30 minutes—just follow signs to your next line.

Exiting the Station

  1. Tap Out: Use the same card at your destination’s turnstile.
  2. Balance Check: The screen shows your remaining credit. Low balance? Top up before your next ride.

Recharging Your Card

At the Station

  • Kiosks: Insert cash (¥10–¥100 notes) or use WeChat/Alipay.
  • Counters: Staff can assist in English for larger top-ups.

Digital Options

Link your card to apps like "Jinlingtong" (available on iOS/Android) to check balances and recharge remotely.

Insider Tips for Tourists

Avoid Rush Hour

Lines 1 and 2 get packed during 7:30–9:30 AM and 5–7 PM. Visit quieter attractions like Jiming Temple early, then metro back post-peak.

Hidden Gems Near Metro Stops

  • Zhujiang Lu Station (Line 1): Foodies—this is your stop for Nanjing salted duck.
  • Yunjin Lu Station (Line 2): Walk to the Yunjin Brocade Museum.

Lost Card? No Panic!

Report it immediately at any station. If registered, you might recover unused credit.

Beyond the Metro: Card Uses

Buses & Ferries

Swipe your card on city buses (50% discount!) or the Qinhuai River ferry for scenic night views.

Small Purchases

Some card versions work at vending machines or chain stores like KFC—handy when you’re craving a quick snack.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Forgetting to Tap Out

You’ll be charged the maximum fare (up to ¥10). Always double-check!

Using Damaged Cards

Bent or wet cards may not scan. Keep yours in a protective sleeve.

Ignoring Metro Etiquette

  • Offer seats to elders.
  • No eating/drinking (fines apply).
  • Stand clear of closing doors.

Now that you’re a metro-card pro, Nanjing’s treasures are just a tap away. Happy exploring!
