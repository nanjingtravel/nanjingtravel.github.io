Nanjing Summer Travel: Safety Tips and Advice

  • Home
  • Travel Blog
  • Blog Details

Nanjing, a city steeped in history and culture, transforms into a vibrant summer destination with its lush greenery, scenic waterways, and dynamic urban life. However, traveling during the scorching summer months requires careful planning to ensure both enjoyment and safety. Whether you're exploring the ancient city walls, indulging in local delicacies, or cruising along the Qinhuai River, these tips will help you make the most of your Nanjing summer adventure.

Beating the Heat: Staying Cool in Nanjing’s Summer

Nanjing’s summer temperatures often soar above 35°C (95°F), with high humidity amplifying the discomfort. Here’s how to stay cool while sightseeing:

Dress for the Weather

Lightweight, breathable fabrics like linen or moisture-wicking athletic wear are ideal. A wide-brimmed hat, sunglasses, and UV-protective clothing can shield you from the intense sun. Don’t forget to apply high-SPF sunscreen every few hours, especially if you’re visiting open-air attractions like the Sun Yat-sen Mausoleum or Xuanwu Lake.

Hydration is Key

Carry a reusable water bottle and refill it frequently. Many tourist spots, such as Confucius Temple (Fuzimiao), have water stations. Local favorites like chilled sweet osmanthus soup (guihua tang) or sour plum juice (suanmeitang) are delicious ways to rehydrate.

Plan Around the Heat

Start your day early to visit major landmarks before noon, then retreat to air-conditioned museums like the Nanjing Museum or Presidential Palace during peak afternoon heat. Evening strolls along the Qinhuai River offer cooler temperatures and dazzling night views.

Navigating Crowds and Tourist Hotspots

Summer is peak travel season, so popular sites like the Nanjing Massacre Memorial Hall or Zhonghua Gate can get crowded. Here’s how to avoid frustration:

Book Tickets in Advance

Many attractions now require online reservations. Platforms like WeChat mini-programs or Ctrip (Trip.com) allow you to secure slots ahead of time, skipping long queues.

Visit Lesser-Known Gems

Escape the crowds by exploring hidden spots like the quiet alleys of Lao Men Dong (Old Gate East) or the serene Jiangxinzhou Island. The Purple Mountain Observatory offers panoramic views with fewer tourists compared to the nearby Ming Xiaoling Mausoleum.

Food Safety and Local Delicacies

Nanjing’s culinary scene is a summer highlight, but street food and heat demand caution:

Street Food Smarts

Stick to busy vendors with high turnover—like those near Confucius Temple—to ensure freshness. Must-tries include salted duck (yan shui ya), duck blood vermicelli soup, and pan-fried beef buns. Avoid raw or undercooked dishes in humid weather.

Handling Spice Levels

Nanjing cuisine isn’t as fiery as Sichuan food, but dishes like "hot and sour" noodles can surprise beginners. Ask for "wei la" (mild spice) if you’re sensitive.

Transportation Tips for Smooth Travel

Metro Over Taxis

Nanjing’s efficient metro system (Lines 1-10) is air-conditioned and avoids traffic jams. Key stations like Xinjiekou connect major shopping and dining hubs. Use apps like Amap or Google Maps for real-time navigation.

Bike-Sharing with Caution

Shared bikes (HelloBike, Meituan) are great for short distances, but summer heat can make cycling exhausting. Opt for shaded routes like the tree-lined Beijing East Road.

Health and Emergency Preparedness

Heatstroke Prevention

Recognize symptoms (dizziness, nausea) and seek shade immediately. Pharmacies (like Huashen) stock rehydration salts. Hospitals such as Drum Tower Hospital have English-speaking staff.

Mosquito Protection

Wet areas like Xuanwu Lake attract mosquitoes. Pack DEET-based repellent and wear long sleeves at dusk.

Cultural Etiquette and Local Customs

Respect at Historical Sites

Dress modestly at temples (e.g., Jiming Temple) and avoid loud behavior at solemn memorials.

Tipping Isn’t Expected

Unlike Western countries, tipping isn’t customary in Nanjing. A simple "xiexie" (thank you) suffices.

Sustainable Travel Practices

Reduce Plastic Waste

Carry a foldable tote for souvenirs instead of accepting plastic bags. Refill stations are increasingly common in malls like Deji Plaza.

Support Local Artisans

Buy handmade fans or Nanjing cloud-pattern brocade from craft markets to support traditional artisans.

Nanjing’s summer charm lies in its ability to blend ancient heritage with modern energy. By staying mindful of the heat, crowds, and cultural nuances, you’ll unlock an unforgettable journey through this storied city. Pack your curiosity—and plenty of sunscreen—for a trip that’s as safe as it is exhilarating.
