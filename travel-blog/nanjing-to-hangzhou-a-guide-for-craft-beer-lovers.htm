Nanjing to Hangzhou: A Guide for Craft Beer Lovers

  • Home
  • Travel Blog
  • Blog Details

For those who love craft beer and adventure, the journey from Nanjing to Hangzhou offers an exciting blend of urban exploration, scenic landscapes, and—most importantly—outstanding breweries. Whether you're a hophead, a sour enthusiast, or just someone who enjoys a well-crafted pint, this route is packed with hidden gems and must-visit spots. Here’s your ultimate guide to navigating the craft beer scene between these two vibrant cities.

The Craft Beer Scene in Nanjing

Nanjing, a city rich in history and culture, is also home to a growing craft beer movement. While it may not be as internationally recognized as Shanghai or Beijing, the local breweries here are making waves with innovative flavors and cozy taprooms.

Best Breweries in Nanjing

1. Master Gao Brewing
One of Nanjing’s pioneers in craft beer, Master Gao offers a mix of classic and experimental brews. Their Nanjing Pale Ale is a local favorite, balancing citrusy hops with a smooth malt backbone. The taproom has a laid-back vibe, perfect for chatting with fellow beer lovers.

2. 8Pints Brewing
Known for pushing boundaries, 8Pints frequently rotates its taps with bold IPAs, barrel-aged stouts, and even some wild-fermented sours. Their Jiangnan Double IPA is a must-try for those who love a punchy, resinous hop profile.

3. The Brew
A smaller but passionate operation, The Brew focuses on Belgian-style ales and German lagers. Their Wheat King hefeweizen is a refreshing choice, especially after a day of exploring Nanjing’s historic sites.

Where to Pair Beer with Local Food

Nanjing’s street food scene is legendary, and nothing complements a crisp lager or a malty amber ale like some xiao long bao (soup dumplings) or duck blood vermicelli soup. Head to Fuzimiao (Confucius Temple) area for a mix of traditional snacks and modern beer-friendly bites.

The Journey from Nanjing to Hangzhou

The high-speed train between Nanjing and Hangzhou takes just under two hours, making it an easy day trip or weekend getaway. Along the way, you’ll pass through lush countryside and small towns—perfect for snapping some travel pics.

Pitstop: Suzhou’s Emerging Beer Culture

If you’re not in a rush, consider stopping in Suzhou, where a handful of breweries are gaining traction. Suzhou Craft Beer Co. offers a solid lineup, including a Jasmine Green Tea Lager that pays homage to the region’s tea culture.

Hangzhou: Where Tradition Meets Craft Beer Innovation

Hangzhou, famous for its West Lake and Longjing tea, might seem like an unlikely craft beer destination—but think again. The city’s expat community and young locals have fueled a thriving microbrewery scene.

Top Breweries in Hangzhou

1. Taste Room
A trailblazer in Hangzhou’s craft beer scene, Taste Room combines Chinese ingredients with global brewing techniques. Try their West Lake White, a witbier infused with local herbs, or their Qiandao Lake Pilsner, a crisp, clean lager.

2. X Beer
With a sleek industrial-chic taproom, X Beer focuses on bold flavors. Their Hangzhou Hazy IPA is juicy and aromatic, while their Black Dragon Stout incorporates hints of oolong tea for a unique twist.

3. Bubble Lab
For those who enjoy experimental brews, Bubble Lab is a playground of flavors. From lychee gose to sichuan peppercorn saison, their small-batch creations are always surprising.

Best Bars for Beer Lovers

If you’re looking for a wider selection, check out Malt Malt Beer Bar, which features both local and international craft beers. Their knowledgeable staff can guide you through flights or recommend pairings with their gourmet burgers.

Beyond Beer: Hangzhou’s Must-See Sights

While beer is the focus, don’t miss Hangzhou’s iconic attractions:

  • West Lake: Rent a bike or take a leisurely boat ride while sipping a canned craft brew from a local brewery.
  • Lingyin Temple: A serene escape with ancient Buddhist carvings—pair the visit with a light, refreshing ale afterward.
  • Qinghefang Ancient Street: Perfect for picking up souvenirs and trying dragon well tea-infused beers from nearby taprooms.

Tips for the Ultimate Craft Beer Trip

  • Travel Light: Many breweries sell cans or bottles, so bring a small cooler bag if you want to take some home.
  • Use Ride-Sharing Apps: While public transport is efficient, ride-sharing makes brewery-hopping easier.
  • Check for Events: Both Nanjing and Hangzhou host beer festivals, especially in autumn.

From historic Nanjing to picturesque Hangzhou, this route is a dream for craft beer lovers who also crave culture, scenery, and unforgettable flavors. Cheers to the adventure—ganbei!
