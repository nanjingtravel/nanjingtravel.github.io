Nanjing Travel: First-Time Visitor’s Dos and Don’ts

  • Home
  • Travel Blog
  • Blog Details

Nanjing, the ancient capital of China, is a city where history and modernity collide. From its imperial past to its vibrant present, this destination offers a unique blend of culture, cuisine, and scenic beauty. If you’re planning your first trip, here’s a comprehensive guide to help you navigate the city like a pro—what to do, what to avoid, and how to make the most of your adventure.

Must-Do Experiences in Nanjing

1. Explore the Rich History

Nanjing’s historical significance is unmatched. Don’t miss these iconic sites:

  • The Sun Yat-sen Mausoleum: A tribute to the founding father of modern China, this grand structure sits atop Purple Mountain. The climb is worth it for the panoramic views.
  • The Ming Xiaoling Mausoleum: The resting place of the first Ming emperor, this UNESCO World Heritage Site is surrounded by a serene forest.
  • The Nanjing Massacre Memorial Hall: A somber but essential visit to understand the city’s wartime history.

2. Walk Along the City Wall

Nanjing boasts one of the best-preserved ancient city walls in China. Rent a bike or take a leisurely stroll along sections like Zhonghua Gate for breathtaking views of the city.

3. Stroll Through Confucius Temple (Fuzimiao)

This lively area blends history with modern entertainment. By day, explore the temple and Qinhuai River; by night, enjoy street food, lantern displays, and boat rides.

4. Indulge in Nanjing’s Famous Cuisine

Food is a highlight here. Must-try dishes include:
- Nanjing Salted Duck (Yanshuiya): A local delicacy with tender, flavorful meat.
- Duck Blood and Vermicelli Soup (Yaxue Fensi Tang): A hearty street food staple.
- Tangbao (Soup Dumplings): Juicy, broth-filled dumplings best enjoyed at a traditional teahouse.

5. Visit Xuanwu Lake

This massive lake is perfect for a relaxing afternoon. Rent a paddleboat, cycle around the shore, or simply enjoy the cherry blossoms in spring.

What to Avoid in Nanjing

1. Don’t Skip the Weather Prep

Nanjing’s summers are scorching (often above 35°C/95°F), while winters can be chilly. Pack accordingly—light clothing for summer, layers for winter, and always carry an umbrella (rain is frequent).

2. Avoid Rush Hour in the Metro

The subway is efficient but gets extremely crowded during peak hours (7:30–9:00 AM and 5:00–7:00 PM). If possible, plan your travel outside these times.

3. Don’t Overlook Local Etiquette

  • Tipping isn’t customary: Unlike in Western countries, tipping at restaurants or for taxis isn’t expected.
  • Queue politely: Cutting in line is frowned upon, especially at tourist spots.
  • Respect sacred sites: Dress modestly when visiting temples or memorials.

4. Don’t Assume Everyone Speaks English

While younger locals and hotel staff may understand basic English, learning a few Mandarin phrases (or using a translation app) will go a long way.

5. Avoid Tourist Scams

  • Overpriced souvenirs: Shop at reputable stores rather than street vendors near major attractions.
  • "Free" tours: Some touts offer "free" guided tours but pressure you into expensive purchases later. Stick to official tour operators.

Hidden Gems & Off-the-Beaten-Path Spots

1. Qixia Mountain

Escape the crowds and hike this scenic mountain, especially stunning in autumn when the maple leaves turn red. The Qixia Temple at the base is equally enchanting.

2. Lao Men Dong (Old Gate East)

A revitalized historic district with boutique shops, art galleries, and trendy cafés. Perfect for an afternoon of exploration.

3. Jiangning织造府博物馆 (Jiangning Weaving Museum)

A lesser-known museum showcasing Nanjing’s silk-weaving heritage. Fascinating for history and textile enthusiasts.

Transportation Tips

1. Use the Metro

Nanjing’s subway system is clean, fast, and covers most tourist areas. Purchase a rechargeable Jinlingtong Card for convenience.

2. Taxis & Ride-Hailing

Taxis are affordable, but ensure the meter is running. Apps like Didi (China’s Uber) are reliable alternatives.

3. Biking the City

Shared bikes (Mobike, HelloBike) are everywhere. They’re a great way to explore, but watch out for erratic traffic.

Best Times to Visit

  • Spring (March–May): Mild weather and blooming flowers.
  • Autumn (September–November): Cool temperatures and golden foliage.
    Avoid July–August unless you can handle extreme heat and humidity.

Where to Stay

  • Xinjiekou Area: Central, with easy access to shopping and dining.
  • Confucius Temple Area: Ideal for first-timers who want to stay near historic sites.
  • Gulou District: A mix of modern and traditional, great for nightlife.

Nanjing is a city that rewards curiosity. Whether you’re a history buff, a foodie, or just looking to soak in the atmosphere, following these dos and don’ts will ensure a smooth and unforgettable trip. Happy travels!
