Nanjing Travel Hacks for First-Time Visitors

  • Home
  • Travel Blog
  • Blog Details

Nanjing, the ancient capital of China, is a city where history and modernity collide. From its imperial past to its vibrant present, this destination offers a mix of cultural landmarks, scenic beauty, and mouthwatering cuisine. If you're visiting for the first time, these travel hacks will help you navigate the city like a pro.

Getting Around Nanjing

Public Transportation Tips

Nanjing’s metro system is efficient, affordable, and easy to use. With 10 lines covering most tourist spots, it’s the best way to avoid traffic. Download the MetroMan app for real-time updates and route planning.

Pro Tip: Avoid rush hours (7:30–9:30 AM and 5–7 PM) if you want a more comfortable ride.

Taxis & Ride-Hailing

Taxis are plentiful, but always insist on the meter. For convenience, use Didi (China’s Uber equivalent). If you don’t speak Mandarin, have your destination written in Chinese characters to show the driver.

Biking Like a Local

Nanjing has a great bike-sharing system (HelloBike, Meituan Bike). Rent one to explore the city at your own pace—just scan the QR code and go.

Must-Visit Attractions

The Sun Yat-sen Mausoleum

A tribute to China’s founding father, this mausoleum sits atop Purple Mountain. Climbing the 392 steps is worth it for the panoramic views.

Hack: Visit early (before 9 AM) to avoid crowds and the midday heat.

Confucius Temple (Fuzimiao)

This historic area is perfect for souvenir shopping and trying local snacks. At night, the Qinhuai River lights up beautifully.

Skip the touristy restaurants—head to Laomendong nearby for more authentic food.

Nanjing Massacre Memorial Hall

A sobering but essential visit to understand Nanjing’s WWII history. Allow at least two hours to fully absorb the exhibits.

Note: Photography is restricted in certain areas.

Hidden Gems

Xuanwu Lake

A peaceful escape from the city buzz. Rent a paddleboat or stroll along the lakeside paths.

Best Time to Visit: Sunset, when the sky turns golden over the water.

Nanjing Yangtze River Bridge

An engineering marvel with stunning river views. Walk across for free or take the elevator (¥15) to the viewing platform.

Fun Fact: It was the first bridge in China built without foreign assistance.

Food You Can’t Miss

Duck Dishes

Nanjing is famous for its duck—Nanjing Salted Duck (盐水鸭) is a must-try. For the best, visit Jinling Duck Restaurant.

Soup Dumplings (Xiaolongbao)

Head to Guo Li Xiang for juicy, flavorful dumplings. Pair them with vinegar and ginger for the full experience.

Street Food at Shiziqiao

This night market offers everything from stinky tofu to sweet glutinous rice balls. Go hungry and sample as much as you can.

Money-Saving Hacks

Free Walking Tours

Some hostels and organizations offer free guided walks covering major landmarks. Check online for schedules.

Student Discounts

If you’re a student, bring your ISIC card—many attractions offer discounted or even free entry.

Bargain at Markets

At places like Confucius Temple Market, haggling is expected. Start at 50% of the asking price and negotiate from there.

Seasonal Considerations

Spring (March–May)

Cherry blossoms at Mochou Lake and Purple Mountain make this the prettiest time to visit.

Summer (June–August)

Hot and humid—pack light clothing and stay hydrated. Early mornings are best for outdoor activities.

Autumn (September–November)

Cool weather and golden ginkgo trees at Nanjing University create a magical atmosphere.

Winter (December–February)

Chilly but less crowded. Warm up with a bowl of duck blood soup from a local vendor.

Language & Communication

While younger locals may speak basic English, learning a few Mandarin phrases helps:

  • Xièxie (谢谢) – Thank you
  • Nǎlǐ yǒu cèsuǒ? (哪里有厕所?) – Where is the bathroom?
  • Duōshǎo qián? (多少钱?) – How much?

Google Translate’s offline mode is a lifesaver if you don’t have data.

Staying Connected

SIM Cards & Wi-Fi

Buy a China Unicom SIM at the airport for affordable data. Alternatively, rent a portable Wi-Fi device if traveling in a group.

VPN Necessity

Since many Western sites (Google, Instagram, WhatsApp) are blocked, install a VPN (like ExpressVPN or NordVPN) before arriving.

Safety & Etiquette

General Safety

Nanjing is very safe, but watch out for pickpockets in crowded areas like markets and metro stations.

Cultural Tips

  • Tipping isn’t customary—service charges are usually included.
  • Avoid discussing sensitive historical topics unless the other person brings it up.

Final Pro Tips

  • Carry tissues & hand sanitizer—many public restrooms don’t provide toilet paper.
  • Wear comfortable shoes—you’ll be walking a lot on cobblestone streets and up temple steps.
  • Visit a tea house—Nanjing’s Yuhuatai Tea House offers a serene break with high-quality local tea.

With these hacks, your first trip to Nanjing will be smooth, enjoyable, and unforgettable. Happy travels!
