Nanjing Travel: Transportation Options and Tips

  • Home
  • Travel Blog
  • Blog Details

Nanjing, the capital of Jiangsu Province, is a city steeped in history, culture, and modernity. From the majestic Sun Yat-sen Mausoleum to the bustling Confucius Temple area, this city offers a blend of ancient charm and contemporary vibrancy. To make the most of your trip, understanding the transportation options is key. Here’s a detailed guide to getting around Nanjing efficiently and affordably.

Public Transportation in Nanjing

Nanjing boasts a well-developed public transportation system, including metro lines, buses, and taxis. Whether you're a budget traveler or looking for convenience, there’s an option for everyone.

The Nanjing Metro

The Nanjing Metro is one of the fastest and most efficient ways to explore the city. With multiple lines connecting major attractions, shopping districts, and transportation hubs, it’s a traveler’s best friend.

Key Metro Lines for Tourists

  • Line 1 (Blue Line): Runs from north to south, stopping at Nanjing Railway Station, Xinjiekou (shopping district), and Confucius Temple.
  • Line 2 (Red Line): Connects to the Sun Yat-sen Mausoleum and Nanjing Museum.
  • Line 3 (Green Line): Links to the Presidential Palace and Jiming Temple.

Pro Tip: Purchase a Jinlingtong Card (Nanjing’s transit card) for discounted fares and seamless transfers between metro and buses.

Buses: Affordable but Tricky

Nanjing’s bus network is extensive, covering areas the metro doesn’t reach. However, routes can be confusing for non-Mandarin speakers.

Useful Bus Routes

  • Bus 20: Connects Nanjing Railway Station to Xuanwu Lake.
  • Bus 202: Runs from Confucius Temple to Zhongshan Scenic Area.

Note: Use apps like Gaode Maps (Amap) or Baidu Maps for real-time bus tracking.

Taxis and Ride-Hailing

Taxis are plentiful but can get stuck in traffic during peak hours. Ride-hailing apps like Didi Chuxing are reliable alternatives.

Fare Tip: Always ensure the meter is running to avoid overcharging.

Exploring Nanjing’s Top Attractions

Once you’ve figured out transportation, it’s time to visit Nanjing’s must-see spots.

Zhongshan Scenic Area

Home to the Sun Yat-sen Mausoleum, this area is a tribute to China’s revolutionary leader. The metro (Line 2) drops you close, but be prepared for a short hike uphill.

Best Time to Visit: Early morning to avoid crowds.

Confucius Temple (Fuzimiao)

A lively area blending history, shopping, and street food. Take Metro Line 3 and walk along the Qinhuai River for stunning night views.

Must-Try: Nanjing’s famous duck blood soup (鸭血粉丝汤, yā xuè fěnsī tāng).

Xuanwu Lake

A serene escape in the city center. Rent a paddleboat or stroll along the lakeside paths. Accessible via Metro Line 1 or 4.

Day Trips from Nanjing

If you have extra time, consider these nearby destinations:

Purple Mountain (Zijin Shan)

A short taxi ride from the city center, this mountain offers hiking trails, historical sites, and panoramic views.

Qixia Mountain

Famous for its autumn foliage and ancient Qixia Temple, this spot is perfect for nature lovers. Take Bus 138 from Nanjing Railway Station.

Travel Tips for a Smooth Experience

  • Avoid Rush Hour: Metro and buses get crowded between 7:30–9:00 AM and 5:00–7:00 PM.
  • Cashless Payments: Most places accept Alipay and WeChat Pay, but carry small bills for street vendors.
  • Weather Prep: Summers are hot and humid; pack light clothing and sunscreen. Winters can be chilly, so layer up.

With these tips and transportation insights, your Nanjing adventure will be both seamless and unforgettable. Happy travels!
