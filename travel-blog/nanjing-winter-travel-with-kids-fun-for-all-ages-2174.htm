Nanjing Winter Travel with Kids: Fun for All Ages

  • Home
  • Travel Blog
  • Blog Details

Winter in Nanjing is a magical time, especially when exploring the city with children. From snow-covered parks to cozy indoor attractions, there’s something for every age group. Whether you’re looking for adventure, culture, or just a fun family day out, Nanjing delivers. Here’s your ultimate guide to making the most of a winter trip to this historic city with kids in tow.

Best Outdoor Winter Activities for Families

Xuanwu Lake Ice Skating

One of the highlights of winter in Nanjing is the temporary ice-skating rink set up at Xuanwu Lake. The frozen lake transforms into a winter wonderland, perfect for kids learning to skate or families enjoying a glide together. Rent skates on-site, and don’t worry—there are trainers available for beginners. The surrounding park also offers hot cocoa stalls to warm up afterward.

Purple Mountain Snow Play

Purple Mountain (Zijin Shan) becomes a snowy paradise in winter, ideal for sledding and snowball fights. The Sun Yat-sen Mausoleum area, with its wide-open spaces, is a safe spot for kids to play. Pack a thermos of hot tea and some snacks for a mini picnic amidst the winter scenery.

Qinhuai River Lantern Festival

Though technically an evening activity, the Qinhuai River Lantern Festival is a must-see. Held during the Lunar New Year period, the riverbanks glow with colorful lanterns shaped like animals, mythical creatures, and even cartoon characters. Kids will love the vibrant displays, and street vendors sell warm treats like tanghulu (candied fruit) to keep little hands warm.

Indoor Attractions for Chilly Days

Nanjing Museum’s Interactive Exhibits

When the temperature drops, head to the Nanjing Museum. The museum’s children’s section features hands-on exhibits, including ancient artifact replicas kids can touch and traditional craft workshops. The "Treasure of Nanjing" exhibit, with its glittering gold and jade displays, captivates all ages.

Deji Plaza’s Indoor Play Zones

For a break from the cold, Deji Plaza offers multiple indoor play zones. The “Happy Valley” play area has ball pits, slides, and climbing structures for younger kids, while older children might enjoy the VR gaming arcade. Parents can sneak in some shopping or relax at one of the plaza’s many cafés.

Tangshan Hot Springs

A short drive from the city, Tangshan Hot Springs is a great way to unwind. Many resorts here have family-friendly pools with warm, mineral-rich waters. Some even offer private tubs, so you can soak while the kids splash safely nearby.

Seasonal Treats to Warm Up

Nanjing’s Famous Salted Duck (Yànyā)

Winter is the best time to try Nanjing’s signature dish—salted duck. The rich, savory flavor pairs perfectly with steaming bowls of rice. Many local restaurants, like Jinling Restaurant, offer kid-friendly portions.

Sweet Osmanthus Rice Balls (Guìhuā Tāngyuán)

A popular winter dessert, these glutinous rice balls are filled with sweet sesame or red bean paste and served in a fragrant osmanthus syrup. Street vendors near Confucius Temple sell them piping hot.

Hot Chestnuts and Sweet Potatoes

You’ll find vendors roasting chestnuts and sweet potatoes on street corners throughout the city. These make for perfect on-the-go snacks while exploring.

Festive Events Not to Miss

Nanjing International Christmas Market

Held in the Xinjiekou area, this market features festive stalls selling handmade crafts, German-style sausages, and mulled wine (with kid-friendly apple cider options). Carol singers and a visit from “Santa” add to the cheer.

Chinese New Year at Fuzimiao

Confucius Temple (Fuzimiao) becomes the epicenter of Lunar New Year celebrations. Expect lion dances, calligraphy demonstrations, and red lanterns everywhere. Kids can participate in paper-cutting workshops or watch traditional puppet shows.

Practical Tips for Winter Travel with Kids

  • Layer Up: Nanjing winters can be damp and chilly. Dress kids in thermal layers and waterproof boots.
  • Early Mornings: Popular spots like Xuanwu Lake get crowded by midday. Arrive early for shorter lines.
  • Public Transport: The metro is stroller-friendly and warmer than waiting for taxis.
  • Indoor Breaks: Schedule indoor activities between outdoor adventures to avoid overexposure to the cold.

Nanjing in winter is far from dormant—it’s a season of joy, flavor, and family fun. Whether you’re skating on a frozen lake or warming up with a bowl of tāngyuán, the city offers endless ways to create lasting memories.
