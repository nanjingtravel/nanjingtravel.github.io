Nanjing’s Best Bird-Watching Bridges and Overlooks

  • Home
  • Travel Blog
  • Blog Details

Nanjing, a city where history and nature intertwine, offers some of the most spectacular bird-watching opportunities in eastern China. With its lush wetlands, sprawling lakes, and scenic riverbanks, the city is a haven for both migratory and resident bird species. Whether you're an avid birder or a casual nature lover, Nanjing’s bridges and overlooks provide perfect vantage points to observe feathered wonders. Here’s your guide to the best spots for bird-watching in Nanjing.

The Best Bridges for Bird-Watching

Yangtze River Bridge (Changjiang Daqiao)

One of Nanjing’s most iconic landmarks, the Yangtze River Bridge, isn’t just an engineering marvel—it’s also a fantastic spot for bird enthusiasts. The surrounding wetlands attract a variety of waterfowl, including herons, egrets, and even the occasional black-faced spoonbill. Early mornings and late afternoons are the best times to visit, as birds are most active during these hours.

What to Look For:
- Great Cormorants – Often seen diving for fish near the bridge piers.
- Black-headed Gulls – Common in winter months, swirling above the river.
- Chinese Pond Herons – Camouflaged among reeds but easily spotted with patience.

Qinhuai River’s Historic Bridges

The Qinhuai River, winding through Nanjing’s historic districts, is lined with charming bridges that double as bird-watching hotspots. The Fuzimiao Bridge and Wende Bridge are particularly rewarding, offering views of kingfishers darting over the water and swallows nesting under the arches.

Pro Tip: Visit at dusk to catch the mesmerizing sight of flocks of starlings returning to roost along the riverbanks.

Scenic Overlooks for Bird Enthusiasts

Xuanwu Lake Park’s Lakeside Trails

Xuanwu Lake, a sprawling freshwater lake in central Nanjing, is a bird-watcher’s paradise. The Lingzhou Overlook and Huanzhou Pavilion provide elevated views of the lake’s islands, where you can spot:
- Mandarin Ducks – Vibrantly colored and often seen in pairs.
- Little Grebes – Tiny, energetic divers that vanish and reappear unexpectedly.
- White Wagtails – Darting along the shoreline in quick, bobbing motions.

Best Season: Spring and autumn, when migratory species like the Black-crowned Night Heron pass through.

Purple Mountain (Zijin Shan) Observation Decks

For those who prefer forest birds, Purple Mountain’s elevated trails and observation decks offer sightings of:
- Light-vented Bulbuls – Noisy and social, often in small flocks.
- Azure-winged Magpies – Striking blue-and-white birds common in Nanjing’s woodlands.
- Hoopoes – Unmistakable with their crown of feathers and undulating flight.

Hiking Tip: The Ming Xiaoling Scenic Area has several quiet paths where bird activity is high, especially near dawn.

Hidden Gems: Lesser-Known Birding Spots

Jiangxinzhou Ecological Park

This island in the Yangtze River is a hidden treasure for serious birders. The Wetland Boardwalk and Riverside Observation Tower provide unobstructed views of rare species like the Reed Parrotbill and Oriental Stork.

When to Go: Winter is ideal, as the wetlands host large numbers of overwintering ducks and geese.

Mochou Lake’s Quiet Corners

While Mochou Lake is a popular tourist spot, the quieter northern shores near Shengqi Tower are excellent for spotting:
- Common Moorhens – Clumsy yet endearing as they tiptoe across lily pads.
- Chinese Blackbirds – Their melodic calls echo through the willows.

Photography Tip: Early morning fog creates a dreamy backdrop for bird portraits.

Essential Bird-Watching Tips for Nanjing

  • Bring Binoculars – Many birds are shy and stay at a distance.
  • Dress Neutrally – Bright colors can scare birds away.
  • Respect Wildlife – Avoid loud noises and never disturb nests.
  • Join Local Birding Groups – Nanjing has an active bird-watching community that organizes guided walks.

Nanjing’s blend of urban and natural landscapes makes it a unique destination for bird-watching. Whether you’re scanning the Yangtze’s waters from a historic bridge or listening to warblers in Purple Mountain’s forests, the city promises unforgettable encounters with its avian residents.
