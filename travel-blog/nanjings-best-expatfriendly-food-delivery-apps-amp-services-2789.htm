Nanjing’s Best Expat-Friendly Food Delivery Apps & Services

  • Home
  • Travel Blog
  • Blog Details

Nanjing, a city steeped in history and culture, is also a thriving hub for expats and travelers. Whether you're exploring the ancient city walls, strolling along the Qinhuai River, or diving into the vibrant food scene, sometimes you just want the convenience of a great meal delivered straight to your door. Luckily, Nanjing offers a variety of expat-friendly food delivery apps and services that cater to diverse tastes—from local Jiangsu delicacies to international comfort food.

Why Food Delivery is a Must for Expats & Travelers in Nanjing

Navigating a new city’s culinary landscape can be both exciting and overwhelming. For expats and travelers, food delivery apps bridge the gap between convenience and exploration. Whether you're craving a taste of home or eager to try authentic Nanjing dishes like salted duck (盐水鸭) or tangbao (soup dumplings), these platforms make it easy to satisfy your hunger without the language barrier getting in the way.

Key Features to Look For

  • English-language support – Essential for seamless ordering.
  • Diverse cuisine options – From local street food to Western favorites.
  • Reliable delivery times – Because no one likes cold soup dumplings.
  • Secure payment methods – Including international credit cards and digital wallets.

Top Food Delivery Apps in Nanjing for Expats

1. Ele.me (饿了么)

One of China’s most popular food delivery platforms, Ele.me is a go-to for expats in Nanjing. While the app is primarily in Chinese, its user-friendly interface and photo-heavy layout make it navigable even for non-Mandarin speakers. Many restaurants include English translations for their dishes, and payment options like Alipay and WeChat Pay simplify transactions.

Best for: Local Chinese cuisine, quick snacks, and late-night cravings.
Pro tip: Use the camera scan feature to translate menus if needed.

2. Meituan (美团外卖)

Meituan is another giant in China’s food delivery scene, offering not just meals but also grocery and convenience store deliveries. The app occasionally features English-language promotions, and its search function allows filtering by cuisine type—handy if you’re hunting for pizza, burgers, or vegan options.

Best for: Variety, including international restaurants and healthy meal options.
Pro tip: Check the “Expats’ Favorites” section for curated picks.

3. Sherpa’s

A lifesaver for English-speaking expats, Sherpa’s specializes in foreigner-friendly food delivery. The app is fully in English, supports international credit cards, and even delivers alcohol. From Mexican burritos to Japanese ramen, Sherpa’s covers a broad spectrum of cravings.

Best for: Western food, comfort meals, and hassle-free ordering.
Pro tip: Their customer service is responsive—great for special requests.

4. HungryPanda

Focused on Asian cuisine but with a strong selection of international options, HungryPanda is another English-first app catering to expats. It’s particularly strong for Chinese regional dishes, making it a great choice if you want to explore Sichuan hotpot or Cantonese dim sum without leaving your apartment.

Best for: Authentic Chinese regional food and Asian fusion.
Pro tip: Look for combo deals to save on larger orders.

Specialty Services for Expats & Travelers

Grocery Delivery: MissFresh (每日优鲜) & Hema (盒马)

Sometimes you’d rather cook at home—or at least stock up on snacks. MissFresh and Hema (Alibaba’s fresh grocery chain) offer speedy deliveries of fresh produce, imported goods, and even ready-to-eat meals. Hema’s app has an English toggle, making it a favorite among expats.

Coffee & Bakery: Luckin Coffee (瑞幸咖啡) & Baker & Spice

Need a caffeine fix or a croissant? Luckin Coffee delivers affordable, high-quality coffee, while Baker & Spice (available on Sherpa’s) brings artisanal pastries and sandwiches straight to you.

Late-Night Eats: McDonald’s & KFC Delivery

When the midnight munchies strike, international chains like McDonald’s and KFC are reliable options. Both are available on Ele.me and Meituan, with menus that are easy to recognize even if you don’t read Chinese.

Navigating Food Delivery Like a Pro

Language Hacks

  • Use translation apps like Google Lens or Pleco to decipher menus.
  • Save screenshots of your favorite dishes for reordering.

Payment Tips

  • Link your international card to Alipay or WeChat Pay for smoother transactions.
  • Some apps accept PayPal (like Sherpa’s), so check before ordering.

Delivery Etiquette

  • Tipping isn’t customary, but a thank-you message is always appreciated.
  • Track your order in real-time to avoid missed deliveries.

Hidden Gems: Must-Try Nanjing Dishes via Delivery

While you can’t go wrong with classics like duck blood vermicelli soup (鸭血粉丝汤) or lion’s head meatballs (狮子头), here are a few lesser-known delights to order:

  • Xiaolongbao (小笼包) – Juicy soup dumplings best enjoyed fresh.
  • Jianbing (煎饼) – A savory Chinese crepe, perfect for breakfast.
  • Chou Doufu (臭豆腐) – Stinky tofu for the adventurous eater.

Final Bite

Nanjing’s food delivery scene is as dynamic as the city itself. Whether you’re an expat settling in or a traveler passing through, these apps and services ensure you never have to compromise on taste or convenience. So go ahead—order that spicy hotpot, indulge in a burger, or discover a new local favorite, all from the comfort of your temporary home.
