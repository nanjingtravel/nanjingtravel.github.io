Nanjing’s Best Family-Friendly Walking Routes

  • Home
  • Travel Blog
  • Blog Details

Nanjing, a city steeped in history and natural beauty, offers countless opportunities for families to explore on foot. Whether you’re looking for scenic lakeside paths, cultural landmarks, or lush green spaces, Nanjing has something for everyone. Below, we’ve curated the best family-friendly walking routes that combine fun, education, and relaxation.

1. Xuanwu Lake Loop: A Scenic Lakeside Stroll

Why It’s Perfect for Families

Xuanwu Lake, one of Nanjing’s most iconic spots, provides a serene escape from the city’s hustle. The 5-kilometer loop around the lake is flat, stroller-friendly, and dotted with playgrounds, picnic spots, and even boat rentals.

Highlights Along the Way

  • Lotus Flowers in Summer: The lake’s famous lotus blossoms create a stunning backdrop for family photos.
  • Children’s Play Areas: Small playgrounds near the entrance keep younger kids entertained.
  • Boat Rentals: Paddle boats shaped like swans add a fun twist to the walk.

Best Time to Visit

Early mornings or late afternoons are ideal to avoid crowds. Spring and autumn offer the most pleasant weather.

2. Zhongshan Mountain (Purple Mountain) Trails

Why It’s Perfect for Families

Zhongshan Mountain, or Purple Mountain, is a treasure trove of nature and history. While some trails are steep, the flatter routes around the base are great for families.

Must-See Stops

  • Ming Xiaoling Mausoleum: A UNESCO World Heritage Site with wide pathways and ancient stone statues.
  • Zijin Mountain Observatory: A short detour leads to this historic site with telescopes for kids to peek through.
  • Botanical Garden: A colorful, educational stop with themed plant sections.

Tips for the Walk

  • Bring snacks and water, as amenities are sparse in some areas.
  • Consider taking the scenic cable car for a break from walking.

3. Qinhuai River Walk: A Cultural Adventure

Why It’s Perfect for Families

The Qinhuai River area blends history, shopping, and street food into one lively walking route. Kids will love the lantern-lit streets and occasional boat performances.

Key Attractions

  • Confucius Temple (Fuzimiao): A great spot to teach kids about ancient Chinese philosophy.
  • Snack Street: Try local treats like tanghulu (candied fruit) and salted duck.
  • Lantern Displays: Evening walks here feel magical with traditional Chinese lanterns lighting up the river.

Best Time to Go

Late afternoon into the evening, when the area comes alive with lights and performances.

4. Nanjing City Wall Walk: A Historical Journey

Why It’s Perfect for Families

Walking along Nanjing’s ancient city wall offers a mix of history and panoramic views. The best section for families is the Zhonghua Gate to Taicheng Ping section, which is well-preserved and not too strenuous.

What to Look For

  • Ancient Gate Towers: Kids can imagine themselves as medieval guards.
  • Calligraphy Engravings: Some bricks feature inscriptions from the Ming Dynasty.
  • City Views: The elevated path provides great photo ops of modern Nanjing.

Walking Tips

  • Wear comfortable shoes—some sections have uneven stones.
  • Bring a hat and sunscreen, as shade is limited.

5. Mochou Lake Park: A Relaxing Nature Walk

Why It’s Perfect for Families

Mochou Lake Park is a peaceful retreat with gentle paths, pavilions, and even a small amusement area. The name "Mochou" means "free from sorrow," and the tranquil setting lives up to it.

Fun Activities

  • Paddle Boating: Rent a boat and glide across the calm lake.
  • Lotus Pond: Another great spot for seasonal flowers.
  • Children’s Rides: A small fairground section adds excitement for little ones.

When to Visit

Weekdays are quieter, but weekends often feature cultural performances.

6. Presidential Palace & 1912 District: A Blend of Old and New

Why It’s Perfect for Families

This route combines history with modern charm. Start at the Presidential Palace, then walk through the 1912 District, a trendy area with cafes and art installations.

Highlights

  • Presidential Palace Gardens: Open spaces where kids can run around.
  • Street Art in 1912: Colorful murals make for great photo backdrops.
  • Kid-Friendly Cafés: Many spots offer desserts and drinks that appeal to younger visitors.

Best Time for the Walk

Late morning or early evening, when the 1912 District is lively but not too crowded.

7. Jiangxinzhou Island: A Rural Escape

Why It’s Perfect for Families

For families craving a quieter, nature-filled walk, Jiangxinzhou Island is a hidden gem. The island features farmland, orchards, and riverside paths.

What to Do

  • Fruit Picking: Seasonal activities like strawberry picking (winter/spring).
  • Bike Rentals: Extend the adventure by cycling along the river.
  • Riverside Picnics: Pack a lunch and enjoy the peaceful scenery.

Getting There

Take a short ferry ride from the mainland—kids will love the mini boat trip!

Final Tips for Family Walks in Nanjing

  • Stay Hydrated: Carry water bottles, especially in summer.
  • Dress in Layers: Weather can change quickly.
  • Use Public Transport: Many routes are near subway stations, making logistics easy.

Nanjing’s walking routes offer endless ways to bond as a family while discovering the city’s rich culture and stunning landscapes. Whether you prefer lakeside serenity, historical exploration, or vibrant street life, there’s a perfect path waiting for you.
