Nanjing’s Best International Happy Hour Spots

  • Home
  • Travel Blog
  • Blog Details

Nanjing, a city steeped in history and modernity, offers a vibrant nightlife scene that caters to both locals and expats. Whether you’re a traveler looking to unwind after a day of exploring or a resident seeking the perfect after-work drink, Nanjing’s international happy hour spots won’t disappoint. From rooftop bars with stunning skyline views to cozy speakeasies with craft cocktails, here’s your ultimate guide to the best places for happy hour in Nanjing.

The Top Rooftop Bars for Sunset Sips

1. Flair Rooftop Bar

Perched atop The Ritz-Carlton, Flair Rooftop Bar is the epitome of luxury. With panoramic views of the Yangtze River and the city skyline, this spot is perfect for sipping cocktails as the sun dips below the horizon. Their happy hour (5–7 PM) features discounted signature drinks like the Nanjing Mule—a local twist on the Moscow Mule, infused with ginger and lychee.

2. Cloud 9 at InterContinental

If you’re craving high-altitude vibes, Cloud 9 delivers. Located on the 78th floor of the InterContinental, this bar offers breathtaking views of Nanjing’s sprawling urban landscape. Happy hour runs from 4–6 PM, with half-priced wines and beers. Don’t miss their "Purple Mountain Sour," a cocktail inspired by Nanjing’s iconic Purple Mountain.

Trendy Expat Hangouts

3. The Alley Cocktail Bar

Tucked away in the bustling Xinjiekou district, The Alley is a hidden gem known for its creative mixology. The bartenders here craft bespoke cocktails using local ingredients like osmanthus syrup and baijiu-infused bitters. Happy hour (6–8 PM) includes 2-for-1 classic cocktails—perfect for trying something new.

4. Blue Sky Brewery

For beer enthusiasts, Blue Sky Brewery is a must-visit. This microbrewery offers a rotating selection of craft beers, from hoppy IPAs to smooth stouts. Their happy hour (3–6 PM) features ¥20 off all pints, making it a great spot to sample Nanjing’s burgeoning craft beer scene.

Cozy Wine Bars for a Relaxed Vibe

5. Vineyard Café

Nestled in the historic Gulou area, Vineyard Café is a charming spot for wine lovers. With an extensive list of international and Chinese wines, this café offers a relaxed atmosphere perfect for unwinding. Happy hour (4–7 PM) includes 30% off all glasses of wine, paired with delicious tapas.

6. Le Petit Paris

For a touch of European flair, head to Le Petit Paris. This intimate wine bar specializes in French vintages and offers a happy hour (5–8 PM) with discounted wine flights. Pair your drink with their cheese platter for the ultimate indulgence.

Lively Sports Bars for Game Night

7. The Spot Sports Bar

If you’re looking to catch a game while enjoying a cold one, The Spot is the place to be. With multiple screens broadcasting international sports, this bar is a favorite among expats. Happy hour (4–7 PM) features ¥10 draft beers and half-priced wings.

8. O’Reilly’s Irish Pub

A staple in Nanjing’s expat community, O’Reilly’s offers a taste of Ireland in the heart of the city. Their happy hour (5–8 PM) includes discounts on Guinness and whiskey flights. The pub also hosts weekly trivia nights, making it a great spot for socializing.

Unique Themed Bars for a Memorable Night

9. 1928 Secret Bar

Step back in time at 1928 Secret Bar, a speakeasy-style venue inspired by Nanjing’s 1920s glamour. The entrance is disguised as a vintage bookstore, adding to the allure. Happy hour (6–8 PM) offers classic cocktails at half price, including their famous "Jinling Old Fashioned."

10. Mystic Garden

For a whimsical experience, Mystic Garden blends mixology with fantasy. The bar’s enchanted forest theme and smoke-infused cocktails make it a standout. Happy hour (5–7 PM) includes discounted "magic potions" like the "Dragon’s Breath," a fiery mezcal concoction.

Late-Night Eats to Pair with Your Drinks

No happy hour is complete without some delicious bites. Many of Nanjing’s bars offer tasty snacks, but if you’re craving more, head to these nearby spots:
- Laomendong Night Market: Just a short walk from The Alley, this market serves up local favorites like duck blood vermicelli soup and stinky tofu.
- Shanghai Road Food Street: Near Blue Sky Brewery, this street is lined with late-night eateries offering everything from dumplings to skewers.

Nanjing’s happy hour scene is as diverse as the city itself. Whether you’re in the mood for a sophisticated cocktail, a craft beer, or a lively sports bar, there’s something for everyone. So grab your friends, raise a glass, and toast to Nanjing’s vibrant nightlife!
