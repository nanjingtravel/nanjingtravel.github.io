Nanjing’s Best Lakes and Riverside Attractions

  • Home
  • Travel Blog
  • Blog Details

Nanjing, a city steeped in history and natural beauty, offers some of China’s most breathtaking lakeside and riverside landscapes. Whether you’re a history buff, a nature lover, or simply seeking a peaceful retreat, Nanjing’s waterways provide the perfect backdrop for exploration. Here’s a curated list of the city’s must-visit lakes and riverside spots that promise unforgettable experiences.

Xuanwu Lake: The Heart of Nanjing

A Historical Oasis

Xuanwu Lake, located in the heart of Nanjing, is a sprawling freshwater lake surrounded by lush greenery and historic sites. Dating back over 1,500 years, it was once a royal garden during the Six Dynasties period. Today, it’s a favorite among locals and tourists alike for its serene ambiance and scenic walking paths.

What to Do Here

  • Boat Rides: Rent a paddleboat or take a guided cruise to admire the lake’s five interconnected islands.
  • Cherry Blossom Festival: Visit in spring to witness the breathtaking cherry blossoms around the lake.
  • Nearby Attractions: Don’t miss the Ming City Wall and Jiming Temple, both within walking distance.

Mochou Lake: A Poetic Retreat

Legend and Landscape

Named after the tragic legend of Mochou, a virtuous woman from ancient times, this lake is as poetic as its backstory. The surrounding Mochou Lake Park blends classical Chinese garden design with modern recreational spaces.

Highlights

  • Mochou Pavilion: A historic structure offering panoramic lake views.
  • Lotus Blooms: Summer brings vibrant lotus flowers covering the lake’s surface.
  • Local Culture: Watch elderly locals practicing tai chi or playing chess in the park’s shaded corners.

Qinhuai River: The Soul of Old Nanjing

A Riverside Stroll Through History

The Qinhuai River, often called Nanjing’s "mother river," is the lifeblood of the city’s cultural heritage. The riverbanks are lined with well-preserved Ming and Qing dynasty architecture, creating a nostalgic atmosphere.

Must-Experience Activities

  • Night Cruise: Glide past illuminated traditional buildings and under ancient bridges.
  • Confucius Temple Area: Sample local snacks like duck blood vermicelli soup (鸭血粉丝汤) and sesame cakes.
  • Fuzimiao Market: Hunt for souvenirs, from calligraphy brushes to handmade silk fans.

Purple Mountain’s Lakes: Nature Meets History

Zixia Lake and Baima Lake

Nestled at the foot of Purple Mountain (Zijin Shan), these lesser-known lakes offer tranquility away from the crowds. Zixia Lake is famous for its autumn foliage, while Baima Lake is a hidden gem surrounded by dense forests.

Why Visit?

  • Hiking Trails: Explore trails leading to Sun Yat-sen Mausoleum and Linggu Temple.
  • Photography Spots: Capture reflections of Purple Mountain in the still lake waters.
  • Picnic Perfect: Pack a lunch and enjoy a quiet afternoon by the shore.

Yangtze River: Nanjing’s Mighty Waterway

The Power and Beauty of the Yangtze

As the longest river in Asia, the Yangtze shapes Nanjing’s identity. The city’s Yangtze River Bridge, an engineering marvel built in the 1960s, offers stunning views, especially at sunset.

Top Riverside Attractions

  • Nanjing Eye: A futuristic pedestrian bridge with LED light displays at night.
  • Yanziji Scenic Area: Cliffs along the Yangtze where you can spot rare birds.
  • Cruise Options: Take a short ferry ride or a multi-day Yangtze River cruise.

Qianhu Wetland Park: An Eco-Adventure

A Paradise for Birdwatchers

This expansive wetland park is a haven for migratory birds and a fantastic spot for eco-tourism. Wooden walkways let visitors explore without disturbing the wildlife.

Activities to Enjoy

  • Biking: Rent a bike to traverse the park’s extensive trails.
  • Fishing: Join locals fishing in the designated areas.
  • Seasonal Beauty: Visit in autumn for golden reeds and winter for misty landscapes.

Tips for Visiting Nanjing’s Waterfront Attractions

Best Times to Visit

  • Spring (March-May): Mild weather and blooming flowers.
  • Autumn (September-November): Cool air and vibrant foliage.

Getting Around

  • Public Transport: Subway lines 1, 2, and 3 serve major lakes and riverside areas.
  • Bike Rentals: Nanjing’s bike-sharing system is convenient for short trips.

Local Etiquette

  • Respect quiet zones in parks, especially where people meditate or practice tai chi.
  • Always dispose of trash properly to help maintain Nanjing’s pristine waterways.

Nanjing’s lakes and rivers are more than just scenic spots—they’re gateways to the city’s soul. From the poetic charm of Mochou Lake to the grandeur of the Yangtze, each destination tells a story waiting to be discovered.
