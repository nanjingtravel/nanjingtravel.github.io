Nanjing’s Best Mixology Bars for Creative Cocktails

  • Home
  • Travel Blog
  • Blog Details

Nanjing, a city steeped in history and culture, is also home to a thriving nightlife scene. While traditional tea houses and historic sites often steal the spotlight, the city’s mixology bars are quietly crafting some of the most innovative cocktails in China. Whether you’re a traveler looking for a unique evening experience or a local seeking a fresh twist on classic drinks, these bars blend creativity, ambiance, and top-tier mixology. Here’s a curated list of Nanjing’s best spots for creative cocktails.

The Rise of Craft Cocktails in Nanjing

Over the past decade, Nanjing’s bar scene has evolved from simple beer halls and karaoke joints to sophisticated lounges where mixology is treated as an art form. Inspired by global trends and local flavors, bartenders here are pushing boundaries with house-made infusions, rare spirits, and unexpected ingredients like osmanthus syrup or fermented rice wine.

Why Nanjing’s Cocktail Scene Stands Out

Unlike Shanghai or Beijing, where international influences dominate, Nanjing’s mixology bars often incorporate regional elements. Think cocktails infused with longjing tea, yancheng smoked duck, or even Nanjing salted duck—a playful nod to the city’s culinary heritage. This fusion of old and new makes the drinking experience here uniquely memorable.

Top Mixology Bars to Visit

1. The Secret Garden

Location: Near Confucius Temple

Tucked away in a restored Qing-dynasty courtyard, The Secret Garden feels like stepping into a hidden oasis. The bar’s signature drink, "The Scholar’s Dream," combines gin with chrysanthemum honey and a dash of Sichuan peppercorn for a floral yet spicy kick. The ambiance—dim lanterns, antique furniture, and a whisper-quiet vibe—makes it perfect for a romantic night out.

Must-Try Cocktail: "Nanjing Mule"

A twist on the Moscow Mule, this version uses jiangnan rice wine instead of vodka, topped with ginger beer and a slice of kumquat.

2. Neon Tiger

Location: Xinjiekou District

For those who prefer a modern, high-energy vibe, Neon Tiger delivers with its cyberpunk aesthetic and inventive drink menu. The bartenders here are known for their molecular mixology techniques, creating cocktails that smoke, bubble, or change color.

Must-Try Cocktail: "Digital Rain"

A futuristic blend of butterfly pea-infused tequila, activated charcoal, and lime foam, served in a glowing glass.

3. The Ink House

Location: Gulou Area

This literary-themed bar pays homage to Nanjing’s history as a hub for scholars and poets. Cocktails are named after classic Chinese literature, and the menu is designed like an ancient scroll. The "Dream of the Red Chamber" cocktail, for instance, mixes rose-infused bourbon with pomegranate molasses.

Must-Try Cocktail: "Ink Wash"

A striking black-and-white cocktail made with sesame-washed rum, coconut milk, and a drizzle of squid ink for dramatic effect.

4. Bamboo & Bourbon

Location: Zhongshan Road

A fusion of Southern American and Jiangnan influences, this bar specializes in smoked cocktails. The "Nanjing Old Fashioned" is a standout, featuring bourbon infused with Lapsang Souchong tea and a touch of brown sugar syrup.

Must-Try Cocktail: "Smoked Plum Sour"

A smoky, tangy concoction of Japanese whisky, umeboshi (pickled plum), and egg white foam.

5. The Rooftop Lab

Location: Top of a high-rise in Hexi District

As the name suggests, this bar offers panoramic views of Nanjing’s skyline. The menu is experimental, with seasonal ingredients driving the creations. Try the "Skyline Spritz," a refreshing mix of Aperol, prosecco, and a housemade osmanthus liqueur.

Must-Try Cocktail: "Cloud Chaser"

A light, frothy drink with gin, elderflower, and a mist of lavender spray served in a chilled coupe.

Tips for Enjoying Nanjing’s Cocktail Scene

  • Timing Matters: Most bars get busy after 9 PM, especially on weekends. Arrive early to snag a good seat.
  • Ask for Recommendations: Bartenders often have off-menu creations or can tailor drinks to your taste.
  • Pair with Local Snacks: Many bars serve small bites like xiao long bao or spicy crayfish—perfect for balancing strong flavors.

Nanjing’s mixology bars are more than just places to drink; they’re immersive experiences that reflect the city’s blend of tradition and innovation. Whether you’re sipping a tea-infused cocktail in a hidden courtyard or watching the sunset with a smoked creation in hand, these spots promise unforgettable nights.
