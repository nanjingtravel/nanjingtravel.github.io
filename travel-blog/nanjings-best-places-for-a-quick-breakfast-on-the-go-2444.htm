Nanjing’s Best Places for a Quick Breakfast on the Go

  • Home
  • Travel Blog
  • Blog Details

Nanjing, a city steeped in history and culture, is also a paradise for food lovers. Whether you're a busy local rushing to work or a traveler eager to explore, grabbing a quick yet delicious breakfast is a must. From savory jianbing to steaming xiaolongbao, Nanjing offers a variety of on-the-go breakfast options that will fuel your day. Here’s a curated list of the best spots to grab a bite while soaking in the city’s vibrant morning energy.

1. The Iconic Jianbing Stands

Why Jianbing is a Must-Try

Jianbing, a crispy crepe-like dish filled with eggs, scallions, and savory sauces, is Nanjing’s ultimate street breakfast. Portable, flavorful, and packed with energy, it’s the perfect grab-and-go meal.

Best Spots for Jianbing

  • Confucius Temple Area: Near the bustling tourist hub, you’ll find vendors whipping up fresh jianbing with extra crunch.
  • Xinjiekou Metro Station: A hotspot for office workers, these stands serve jianbing with a spicy kick.
  • Nanjing University Campus: Budget-friendly and student-approved, these stalls offer generous portions.

2. Steamed Delights: Xiaolongbao & Soup Dumplings

The Art of Xiaolongbao

Xiaolongbao, or soup dumplings, are a Nanjing favorite. These delicate pockets of broth and meat are perfect for a quick yet satisfying breakfast.

Top Xiaolongbao Stops

  • Guo’s Xiaolongbao (Near Zhonghua Gate): Famous for their thin-skinned, juicy dumplings.
  • Xiao Pan Sheng Jian (Xuanwu District): Known for their crispy-bottomed shengjianbao, a pan-fried variant.
  • Nanjing Da Pai Dong (Qinhuai District): A no-frills spot serving piping-hot dumplings since the 1980s.

3. Warm & Comforting: Congee & Youtiao

The Classic Combo

Congee (rice porridge) paired with youtiao (fried dough sticks) is a timeless Nanjing breakfast. Light yet filling, it’s ideal for those who prefer a simple start.

Where to Find the Best Congee

  • Lao Wang Congee Shop (Gulou District): A local legend serving silky-smooth congee with pickled veggies.
  • Sifangfang (Near Xinjiekou): Modern twist with toppings like preserved eggs and minced pork.
  • Morning Markets (Nanjing’s Residential Areas): Authentic, home-style congee sold by street vendors.

4. Sweet Treats: Sesame Pancakes & Doujiang

Nanjing’s Sweet Tooth Fix

For those with a sweet tooth, sesame pancakes (shaobing) and doujiang (soy milk) are a match made in heaven. Crispy, flaky, and slightly sweet, these treats are perfect for a quick bite.

Best Sweet Breakfast Spots

  • Aijiawu Shaobing (Qinhuai Riverside): Famous for their honey-glazed sesame pancakes.
  • Yonghe Doujiang (Multiple Locations): A chain offering fresh soy milk and warm pastries.
  • Nanjing Train Station Stalls: Grab a shaobing before hopping on a high-speed train.

5. Fast & Flavorful: Noodle Soups

Nanjing’s Noodle Culture

Piping-hot noodle soups, like yangchun noodles or beef brisket noodles, are a hearty way to kickstart your day.

Top Noodle Breakfast Joints

  • Liu Changshou Noodles (Fuzimiao Area): A historic shop serving savory yangchun noodles.
  • Laomendong Beef Noodles (Near Confucius Temple): Rich broth and tender beef slices.
  • Xiaopan Mian (Xuanwu District): Quick-service noodles with customizable toppings.

6. Modern Twists: Cafés & Bakeries

Western-Inspired Breakfasts

For travelers craving familiar flavors, Nanjing’s café scene offers excellent coffee and pastries.

Best Cafés for a Quick Bite

  • Uniuni (Multiple Locations): Specialty coffee with avocado toast.
  • The Fish Tank Café (Gulou Area): Cozy spot with fresh croissants.
  • Bread Society (Xinjiekou): Artisanal bread and sandwiches.

7. Hidden Gems: Local Markets & Food Streets

Explore Like a Local

Nanjing’s morning markets are treasure troves of breakfast delights. From stuffed pancakes to glutinous rice balls, these spots offer authenticity at its best.

Must-Visit Markets

  • Nanjing Tech University Morning Market: Affordable and diverse options.
  • Shiziqiao Food Street: A mix of traditional and modern bites.
  • Mochou Road Market: Lesser-known but packed with flavor.

Nanjing’s breakfast scene is as dynamic as the city itself. Whether you’re indulging in street food or sipping coffee in a chic café, these spots ensure you start your day right—quick, delicious, and full of local charm.
