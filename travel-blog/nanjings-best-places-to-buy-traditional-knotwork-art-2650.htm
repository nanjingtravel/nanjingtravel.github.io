Nanjing’s Best Places to Buy Traditional Knotwork Art

  • Home
  • Travel Blog
  • Blog Details

Nanjing, a city steeped in history and culture, is a treasure trove for travelers seeking authentic Chinese craftsmanship. Among its many artistic traditions, traditional knotwork art (中国结, Zhōngguó jié) stands out as a beautiful and symbolic handicraft. These intricate knots, often made from silk or cotton, carry deep cultural meanings and are popular souvenirs. If you’re visiting Nanjing and want to bring home a piece of this heritage, here’s where to find the best traditional knotwork art in the city.

Why Traditional Knotwork Art is a Must-Buy in Nanjing

Before diving into the best shopping spots, let’s explore why knotwork is such a cherished art form.

The History and Symbolism of Chinese Knots

Chinese knotwork dates back thousands of years, originally used for recording events before the invention of writing. Over time, these knots evolved into decorative art, often symbolizing luck, prosperity, and unity. Each knot pattern has a unique meaning:

  • Double Coin Knot (双钱结): Represents wealth and good fortune.
  • Mystic Knot (盘长结): Symbolizes endless love and harmony.
  • Button Knot (纽扣结): Used in clothing and signifies strong bonds.

Why Nanjing is a Great Place to Buy Knotwork

Nanjing, as a former capital of China, has preserved many traditional crafts. Local artisans here take pride in handcrafting high-quality knotwork, often incorporating modern designs while keeping the ancient techniques alive.

Top Places to Buy Traditional Knotwork in Nanjing

1. Confucius Temple (Fuzimiao) Market

One of Nanjing’s most famous tourist spots, Fuzimiao, isn’t just about history—it’s also a fantastic place to shop for traditional crafts.

What to Expect:

  • Numerous stalls selling handmade knotwork, from simple bracelets to elaborate wall hangings.
  • Affordable prices, with small pieces starting at ¥20 and larger artworks going up to ¥200.
  • A lively atmosphere with street food and cultural performances nearby.

Pro Tip:

Visit in the evening when the area is beautifully lit, and vendors are more open to bargaining.

2. Nanjing Folk Custom Museum (南京民俗博物馆)

For those who appreciate authenticity, this museum doubles as a workshop where artisans demonstrate knot-making.

Highlights:

  • Watch skilled craftsmen create knots in real time.
  • Purchase directly from the artists, ensuring high-quality, unique pieces.
  • Learn about the cultural significance of different knot patterns.

3. Laomendong (老门东) Cultural District

A restored historical area blending old-world charm with trendy shops, Laomendong is perfect for travelers who want a mix of tradition and modernity.

Best Shops for Knotwork:

  • Yunxiang Knot Art Studio (云祥结艺坊): Specializes in custom-made knot jewelry.
  • Jinling Handicrafts (金陵手工艺): Offers a wide range of knotwork, from keychains to home decor.

Why Visit?

Besides shopping, the area has photogenic alleyways and teahouses where you can relax.

4. Xinjiekou Shopping District

If you prefer a more commercial setting, Xinjiekou—Nanjing’s bustling downtown—has several stores selling knotwork alongside other souvenirs.

Recommended Stores:

  • Nanjing Arts & Crafts Emporium (南京工艺美术大楼): A trusted spot for premium knotwork.
  • Deji Plaza (德基广场): High-end department stores with curated craft sections.

5. Qinhuai River Night Market

For a more casual shopping experience, head to the Qinhuai River area at night.

What Makes It Special:

  • Street vendors sell budget-friendly knotwork.
  • Great for last-minute gifts or small trinkets.
  • The scenic riverside adds to the experience.

How to Choose the Best Knotwork

With so many options, how do you pick the perfect piece?

Quality Checkpoints:

  • Material: Silk knots are more durable and luxurious than synthetic ones.
  • Symmetry: Well-made knots are evenly tied without loose threads.
  • Color: Traditional red symbolizes luck, but modern designs come in various hues.

Popular Knotwork Items to Buy:

  • Bracelets & Necklaces: Easy to wear and carry.
  • Home Decor: Wall hangings or car ornaments make great gifts.
  • Wedding Knots: Often bought as symbols of love and commitment.

Supporting Local Artisans

When buying knotwork, consider purchasing from small workshops or cultural centers where artisans directly benefit. Not only do you get a genuine product, but you also help preserve this ancient craft.

Final Shopping Tips

  • Bargaining: Acceptable in markets but not in museums or high-end stores.
  • Authenticity: Ask about the maker—some shops mass-produce, while others handcraft.
  • Packaging: Many stores offer gift boxes, ideal for presents.

Nanjing’s knotwork art is more than just a souvenir—it’s a piece of cultural heritage. Whether you’re strolling through Fuzimiao or exploring Laomendong, you’re sure to find a knot that speaks to you. Happy shopping!
