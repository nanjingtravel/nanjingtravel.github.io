Nanjing’s Best Transport for History Lovers

  • Home
  • Travel Blog
  • Blog Details

Nanjing, one of China’s most historically rich cities, offers a treasure trove of ancient sites, museums, and cultural landmarks. For history enthusiasts, navigating the city efficiently is key to uncovering its storied past. From imperial dynasties to modern revolutions, Nanjing’s transport options not only get you from point A to point B but also enhance your journey through time. Here’s a guide to the best ways to explore Nanjing’s history while on the move.

The Metro: A Modern Gateway to Ancient Stories

Nanjing’s metro system is a marvel of modern engineering, but its routes are carefully designed to connect travelers with the city’s historical heart.

Key Historical Stops on the Metro

  1. Xuanwumen Station (Line 1 & 4)

    • Just steps away from the Ming Xiaoling Mausoleum, the final resting place of the Hongwu Emperor, founder of the Ming Dynasty.
    • A short walk leads to the Purple Mountain, where history and nature intertwine.

  2. Confucius Temple Station (Line 3)

    • The bustling Qinhuai River area, once a hub for scholars and poets during the Ming and Qing dynasties.
    • Nearby, the Imperial Examination Museum offers a glimpse into China’s ancient civil service system.

  3. Daxinggong Station (Line 3)

    • Home to the Presidential Palace, where Sun Yat-sen’s provisional government once stood.
    • A stone’s throw from the 1912 Historical District, a lively blend of early 20th-century architecture and modern nightlife.

Why the Metro Works for History Buffs

  • Affordable and efficient: Fares start at just 2 RMB, and trains run frequently.
  • English signage: Makes navigation easy for international travelers.
  • Air-conditioned comfort: A relief during Nanjing’s hot summers.

Vintage Trams: Riding Through Time

For a slower, more nostalgic journey, Nanjing’s vintage trams offer a charming way to see the city’s historical layers.

The Qinhuai Tram Line

  • Route: Runs along the Qinhuai River, passing landmarks like the Zhonghua Gate, one of the best-preserved ancient city gates in China.
  • Atmosphere: The tram’s retro design complements the traditional architecture of the area.

Why Choose the Tram?

  • Scenic views: Perfect for photographers and leisurely explorers.
  • Less crowded: A peaceful alternative to buses and the metro.

Bicycle Tours: Pedal Through the Past

Nanjing’s bike-sharing programs (like HelloBike and Mobike) make it easy to explore at your own pace.

Top Historical Routes for Cyclists

  1. City Wall Circuit

    • Ride along sections of the Ming Dynasty City Wall, the longest surviving city wall in the world.
    • Stop at Taicheng Ruins, where the wall meets Xuanwu Lake.

  2. Sun Yat-sen Memorial Route

    • Start at the Sun Yat-sen Mausoleum, a monumental tribute to the "Father of Modern China."
    • Cycle through Zijin Mountain, dotted with historical tombs and temples.

Bike-Sharing Tips

  • Download the apps: HelloBike and Mobike are user-friendly for foreigners.
  • Early mornings are best: Beat the heat and crowds.

Taxis and Ride-Hailing: Comfort with Context

For those short on time or energy, taxis and Didi (China’s Uber) are convenient options.

Must-Visit Spots Accessible by Taxi

  • Nanjing Massacre Memorial Hall: A sobering but essential stop to understand WWII history.
  • Porcelain Tower of Bao’en Temple: A reconstructed Ming Dynasty marvel.

Pro Tips

  • Use Didi for English-friendly service: The app allows cashless payments and route tracking.
  • Have your destination written in Chinese: Helps avoid language barriers.

Walking: The Slowest (But Most Rewarding) Option

Some of Nanjing’s best historical gems are best discovered on foot.

Best Walking Routes

  1. Confucius Temple to Zhonghua Gate

    • Wander through ancient alleyways and along the Qinhuai River.
    • Stop at Dacheng Hall, a tribute to Confucius.

  2. Xuanwu Lake Perimeter

    • A serene walk with views of the city wall and skyline.

Walking Advice

  • Wear comfortable shoes: Cobblestone streets are charming but uneven.
  • Carry cash: Small vendors near historical sites may not accept cards.

Final Transport Tips for History Lovers

  • Combine methods: Use the metro for long distances and bikes/walking for exploration.
  • Buy a transportation card: Saves time and offers small discounts.
  • Check opening hours: Some historical sites close early.

Nanjing’s transport network is more than just a way to get around—it’s a bridge between the past and present. Whether you’re gliding through underground tunnels or pedaling along ancient walls, every journey adds another layer to your understanding of this incredible city.
