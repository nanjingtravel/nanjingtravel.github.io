Nanjing’s Best Vegan Restaurants

  • Home
  • Travel Blog
  • Blog Details

Nanjing, a city steeped in history and culture, is also a hidden gem for vegan travelers. With its vibrant food scene and growing emphasis on sustainability, the city offers an impressive array of plant-based dining options. Whether you're a dedicated vegan, a curious flexitarian, or just looking to explore healthier eats, Nanjing’s vegan restaurants will surprise and delight you. Here’s a curated guide to the best spots for plant-based cuisine in this dynamic city.

Why Nanjing is a Vegan-Friendly Destination

Nanjing’s culinary landscape is evolving, and veganism is no longer a niche trend. The city’s rich Buddhist heritage has long embraced vegetarianism, and modern chefs are now reinventing traditional dishes with innovative, plant-based twists. From cozy cafés to upscale dining, Nanjing proves that vegan food can be both flavorful and deeply satisfying.

The Rise of Veganism in Nanjing

In recent years, Nanjing has seen a surge in vegan-friendly establishments. Younger generations are driving the demand for sustainable, ethical dining, and restaurants are responding with creative menus that highlight local ingredients. Whether it’s a hearty bowl of vegan "roujiamo" or a delicate tofu-based "tangbao," Nanjing’s chefs are redefining what plant-based eating can be.

Top Vegan Restaurants in Nanjing

1. Loving Hut (Nanjing Branch)

Location: Near Confucius Temple

A global vegan chain with a Nanjing outpost, Loving Hut is a must-visit for its affordable and delicious plant-based comfort food. The menu features everything from vegan dumplings to mock-meat stir-fries, all served in a casual, welcoming atmosphere. Don’t miss their signature "vegan Peking duck," made with soy protein and served with homemade hoisin sauce.

Must-Try Dishes:

  • Vegan Xiaolongbao – Juicy, soup-filled dumplings that rival the traditional pork version.
  • Spicy Tofu Hot Pot – A fiery, flavorful dish packed with fresh vegetables and silken tofu.

2. Vegetarian Lifestyle (Su Shi Shenghuo)

Location: Xinjiekou District

This elegant restaurant offers a refined take on Buddhist vegetarian cuisine. The dishes are artfully presented, and the ambiance is serene, making it perfect for a leisurely meal. Their menu avoids artificial mock meats, focusing instead on whole foods like mushrooms, lotus root, and seasonal greens.

Must-Try Dishes:

  • Braised Lion’s Head Mushroom – A meatless version of the classic Jiangsu dish, slow-cooked in a rich broth.
  • Stir-Fried Bamboo Shoots with Snow Peas – A light yet flavorful dish that highlights Nanjing’s fresh produce.

3. Green Option (Lü Se Xuanze)

Location: Gulou District

A favorite among health-conscious locals, Green Option specializes in organic, plant-based bowls and salads. Their ingredients are sourced from local farms, ensuring freshness and sustainability. The café also offers cold-pressed juices and vegan desserts, making it a great spot for a light lunch or afternoon pick-me-up.

Must-Try Dishes:

  • Quinoa Buddha Bowl – Packed with roasted veggies, avocado, and a creamy tahini dressing.
  • Matcha Chia Pudding – A guilt-free dessert that’s as Instagram-worthy as it is delicious.

4. Veggie Table (Cai Zhi Yuan)

Location: Near Zhongshan Scenic Area

Nestled close to one of Nanjing’s most famous tourist spots, Veggie Table is ideal for travelers exploring the city’s historical sites. The restaurant offers a mix of Chinese and Western vegan dishes, including burgers, pasta, and traditional Nanjing-style noodles. Their outdoor seating area is perfect for enjoying a meal after a day of sightseeing.

Must-Try Dishes:

  • Vegan Dan Dan Noodles – A Sichuan-inspired dish with a nutty, spicy sauce.
  • Mushroom "Beef" Burger – A hearty, umami-packed alternative to the classic beef patty.

5. Pure & Whole (Chun Su)

Location: Hexi District

For those seeking a more upscale vegan experience, Pure & Whole delivers with its gourmet tasting menus. The restaurant emphasizes seasonal ingredients and minimalist plating, creating dishes that are as visually stunning as they are tasty. Pair your meal with one of their organic teas or house-made kombucha.

Must-Try Dishes:

  • Truffle-infused Pumpkin Soup – A velvety, aromatic starter that sets the tone for the meal.
  • Charred Eggplant with Miso Glaze – A smoky, savory dish that showcases the versatility of vegetables.

Vegan Street Food & Snacks

Nanjing’s street food scene isn’t just about meaty treats—vegan options abound if you know where to look. Here are a few plant-based street eats to try:

Vegan Roujiamo

A meatless take on the famous Shaanxi sandwich, stuffed with braised tofu or mushrooms.

Sweet Osmanthus Rice Cakes

A traditional Nanjing dessert made with glutinous rice and fragrant osmanthus flowers—naturally vegan and utterly delicious.

Fried Glutinous Rice Balls (Jian Dui)

Crispy on the outside, chewy on the inside, these sesame-coated treats are a must-try.

Tips for Vegan Travelers in Nanjing

  • Learn Key Phrases – While many restaurants have English menus, knowing how to say "wú ròu" (no meat) or "sù shí" (vegetarian) can be helpful.
  • Explore Temple Cuisine – Buddhist temples often serve vegetarian meals, offering a unique cultural and culinary experience.
  • Use HappyCow – This app is a lifesaver for finding vegan-friendly spots in Nanjing and beyond.

Nanjing’s vegan dining scene is thriving, blending tradition with innovation. Whether you’re indulging in a gourmet plant-based feast or grabbing a quick vegan snack from a street vendor, the city offers endless opportunities to savor cruelty-free cuisine. So pack your appetite—and your curiosity—and dive into Nanjing’s best vegan eats!
