Nanjing’s Coziest European Cafés

  • Home
  • Travel Blog
  • Blog Details

Nanjing, a city steeped in history and culture, is also home to a thriving café scene that blends Eastern charm with European elegance. Whether you're a traveler seeking a quiet retreat or a local looking for a cozy spot to unwind, these European-inspired cafés offer the perfect ambiance. From rustic French patisseries to minimalist Scandinavian coffee houses, here’s your guide to Nanjing’s most inviting European-style cafés.

The French Touch: Le Petit Paris

A Slice of Paris in Nanjing

Tucked away in the tree-lined streets of Gulou District, Le Petit Paris transports you straight to the heart of Montmartre. With its vintage chandeliers, wrought-iron furniture, and walls adorned with French art, this café is a love letter to Parisian culture.

Must-Try Delights

Their pain au chocolat is flaky perfection, and the crème brûlée is a creamy dream. Pair these with their signature lavender latte for a truly indulgent experience. Don’t forget to snap a photo of their Instagram-worthy macaron tower!

Nordic Minimalism: Fika Café

Scandinavian Serenity

For those who appreciate clean lines and a calming atmosphere, Fika Café in Xuanwu District is a haven of Nordic simplicity. The name Fika comes from the Swedish tradition of taking a coffee break, and this café embodies that philosophy perfectly.

Coffee with a Conscience

Their ethically sourced beans are roasted in-house, and their pour-over coffee is a must-try. The kanelbullar (Swedish cinnamon buns) are freshly baked daily, offering a sweet contrast to their robust coffee blends. The minimalist décor, with its pale wood and soft lighting, makes it an ideal spot for remote work or quiet reflection.

Italian Romance: Bella Napoli

A Taste of Tuscany

Nestled near Confucius Temple, Bella Napoli brings the warmth of Italy to Nanjing. The café’s terracotta walls, checkered tablecloths, and the scent of freshly baked focaccia create an unmistakably Italian vibe.

Authentic Flavors

Their tiramisu is legendary—layers of espresso-soaked ladyfingers and mascarpone cream that melt in your mouth. For something savory, try their panini al prosciutto, stuffed with imported Italian cured ham. The outdoor seating area, draped in ivy, is perfect for people-watching on a sunny afternoon.

German Gem: Café Berlin

Hearty and Homely

If you’re craving robust flavors and a cozy, pub-like atmosphere, Café Berlin in Jianye District is the place to be. Dark wood furnishings, vintage beer steins, and the occasional live accordion music make this spot feel like a little piece of Bavaria.

Beyond Coffee

While their kaffee und kuchen (coffee and cake) combo is delightful, don’t miss their apfelstrudel—a buttery, cinnamon-spiced apple pastry that pairs perfectly with a strong espresso. On weekends, they serve glühwein (mulled wine), making it a winter favorite.

Belgian Bliss: The Chocolate House

A Chocolate Lover’s Paradise

Located near Xinjiekou, The Chocolate House is a decadent escape for anyone with a sweet tooth. The café’s rich, velvety hot chocolate, made from Belgian couverture chocolate, is a highlight. The interior, with its dark wood and gold accents, exudes old-world charm.

Indulgent Treats

Their waffles are crispy on the outside, fluffy on the inside, and topped with everything from fresh berries to molten chocolate sauce. For a truly luxurious experience, order the chocolate fondue with a side of fresh fruit and marshmallows.

British Charm: The Rose Tea Room

Afternoon Tea, Nanjing Style

For a taste of British elegance, head to The Rose Tea Room in Qinhuai District. With its floral wallpaper, delicate china, and tiered cake stands, this café offers a refined take on the classic English afternoon tea.

Tea Time Traditions

Their Earl Grey is brewed to perfection, and the scones are served with clotted cream and homemade strawberry jam. The finger sandwiches, filled with cucumber and smoked salmon, are as dainty as they are delicious. It’s the perfect spot for a leisurely afternoon with friends.

Spanish Vibes: Café Sol

Sunny and Lively

Bright, colorful, and full of energy, Café Sol in Jiangning District brings the spirit of Spain to Nanjing. The café’s mosaic tiles, hanging lanterns, and flamenco music create a festive atmosphere.

Tapas and More

Their churros con chocolate are a crowd-pleaser—crispy fried dough dipped in thick, rich chocolate. For something savory, try the tortilla española, a hearty potato omelet that’s a staple of Spanish cuisine. Their sangria, available by the glass or pitcher, adds a refreshing touch.

Dutch Delight: The Windmill Café

Quaint and Quirky

Inspired by the Netherlands, The Windmill Café in Qixia District is a charming spot with wooden clogs, Delft blue ceramics, and a miniature windmill at the entrance. The café’s laid-back vibe makes it a favorite among students and artists.

Sweet and Savory

Their stroopwafels (caramel-filled waffle cookies) are a must-try, especially when paired with a cup of Dutch coffee. For a heartier option, the bitterballen (deep-fried meatballs) are crispy on the outside and creamy on the inside.

Austrian Elegance: Café Mozart

Classical and Refined

Named after the legendary composer, Café Mozart in Gulou District offers a taste of Vienna’s coffeehouse culture. The café’s grand piano, crystal chandeliers, and marble tabletops exude old-world sophistication.

Musical Treats

Their sachertorte, a dense chocolate cake with apricot jam, is a masterpiece. Pair it with a wiener melange, a Viennese coffee similar to a cappuccino, for the full experience. Live piano performances on weekends add to the enchanting ambiance.

Portuguese Hideaway: Pastéis de Nata

A Bite of Lisbon

Specializing in Portugal’s famous custard tarts, Pastéis de Nata in Hexi District is a small but mighty café. The buttery, flaky pastry and creamy custard filling are baked fresh throughout the day.

Simple Perfection

While the pastéis de nata are the star, their galão (Portuguese latte) is the perfect accompaniment. The café’s azulejo-tiled walls and ocean-blue décor make it feel like a seaside escape.

Russian Warmth: Babushka’s Café

Cozy and Nostalgic

With its plush armchairs, samovars, and shelves lined with Russian literature, Babushka’s Café in Xianlin District feels like a grandmother’s living room. The café’s warm, inviting atmosphere is perfect for chilly Nanjing evenings.

Comfort Food

Their medovik (honey cake) is layers of delicate pastry and sweet cream, while the blini (Russian pancakes) served with sour cream and caviar offer a savory option. A pot of chai (Russian tea) completes the experience.

Whether you’re in the mood for French pastries, Italian espresso, or Belgian chocolate, Nanjing’s European cafés offer a delightful escape. Each spot brings a unique flavor and ambiance, making them perfect for travelers and locals alike. So grab a book, order a coffee, and savor the European charm right here in Nanjing.
