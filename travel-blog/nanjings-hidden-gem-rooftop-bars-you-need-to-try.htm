Nanjing’s Hidden Gem Rooftop Bars You Need to Try

  • Home
  • Travel Blog
  • Blog Details

When travelers think of Nanjing, they often picture its rich history, ancient temples, and bustling food streets. But beyond the traditional attractions lies a vibrant nightlife scene, and nothing captures the city’s modern charm better than its rooftop bars. These hidden gems offer breathtaking views, creative cocktails, and an ambiance that blends Nanjing’s past and present. Here’s your guide to the best rooftop bars in Nanjing that deserve a spot on your must-visit list.

The Skyline Sippers: Where to Drink with a View

1. Flair Rooftop Bar

Perched atop The Ritz-Carlton, Flair is the epitome of luxury. With a 360-degree view of the Qinhuai River and the city skyline, this bar is perfect for those who want to sip cocktails while watching the sunset. Try their signature Nanjing Mule, a twist on the Moscow Mule infused with local ingredients like osmanthus syrup. The sleek, modern design contrasts beautifully with the historic scenery below, making it a favorite among both locals and tourists.

2. Cloud Nine at InterContinental

Located on the 78th floor of the InterContinental, Cloud Nine holds the title of one of the highest bars in Nanjing. The floor-to-ceiling windows offer an unobstructed view of the Yangtze River, especially stunning at night when the city lights shimmer. Their Jinling Sunset cocktail, featuring baijiu and tropical fruits, is a must-try. The live jazz performances on weekends add an extra layer of sophistication.

3. The Roof at Mandarin Oriental

Tucked away in the heart of Xinjiekou, The Roof is a secluded escape from the urban hustle. The bar’s lush greenery and minimalist decor create a serene atmosphere. Their mixologists excel at crafting bespoke drinks—ask for the Xuanwu Spritz, a refreshing blend of prosecco, elderflower, and a hint of Sichuan pepper. The view of the Xuanwu Lake at dusk is nothing short of magical.

Hidden Oases: Rooftop Bars with a Twist

1. Lost Horizon

Inspired by the mythical Shangri-La, Lost Horizon is a bohemian rooftop bar hidden in a nondescript building near Confucius Temple. The vibe is eclectic, with mismatched furniture, fairy lights, and a small herb garden where they grow ingredients for their cocktails. Don’t miss their Dragon’s Breath, a smoky mezcal concoction served with a dramatic dry ice presentation.

2. The Secret Garden

True to its name, this bar is a well-kept secret. Accessible only through a discreet elevator in a shopping mall, The Secret Garden feels like a private party. The menu changes seasonally, but their Purple Mountain Sour (made with purple sweet potato vodka) is a year-round hit. The cozy seating and intimate setting make it ideal for date nights.

3. Bamboo Heights

For a taste of Nanjing’s artistic side, head to Bamboo Heights. This rooftop bar doubles as an art gallery, showcasing works by local artists. The cocktails are just as creative—try the Ink Wash, a black sesame-infused gin cocktail that pays homage to traditional Chinese painting. The open-air terrace is perfect for warm summer nights.

Local Favorites: Where Nanjing’s Cool Crowd Hangs Out

1. Nanjing 1927

Named after the year the city became China’s capital, this bar is a nostalgic nod to Nanjing’s golden era. The decor features vintage posters and antique phonographs, while the drink menu includes classics with a local twist. Their Republic Old Fashioned, made with Chinese whiskey and osmanthus bitters, is a crowd-pleaser.

2. The Drunken Poet

This literary-themed bar is a haven for book lovers. The shelves are stocked with poetry collections, and the cocktails are named after famous verses. The Li Bai’s Dream (a potent mix of baijiu and lychee liqueur) is a tribute to the Tang Dynasty poet. The rooftop terrace overlooks a quiet hutong, offering a glimpse of old Nanjing.

3. Hibiscus Lounge

A favorite among Nanjing’s creative crowd, Hibiscus Lounge is known for its vibrant atmosphere and innovative drinks. The Jiangnan Fizz, a blend of sake, yuzu, and sparkling tea, is a refreshing choice. The bar often hosts pop-up events, from live DJ sets to poetry slams.

Pro Tips for Rooftop Bar Hopping in Nanjing

  • Timing is everything: Arrive just before sunset to snag the best seats and watch the city transition from day to night.
  • Dress code: While most rooftop bars are casual, upscale spots like Flair and Cloud Nine may require smart casual attire.
  • Reservations: Popular bars fill up quickly, especially on weekends. Book a table in advance if possible.
  • Seasonal specialties: Many bars offer limited-edition cocktails based on local seasonal ingredients—ask the bartender for recommendations.

Nanjing’s rooftop bars are more than just places to drink; they’re experiences that elevate your visit to this dynamic city. Whether you’re a cocktail connoisseur, a photography enthusiast, or simply someone who loves a good view, these hidden gems promise unforgettable nights under the stars.
