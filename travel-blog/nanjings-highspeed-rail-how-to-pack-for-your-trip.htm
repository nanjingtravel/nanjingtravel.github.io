Nanjing’s High-Speed Rail: How to Pack for Your Trip

  • Home
  • Travel Blog
  • Blog Details

Nanjing, the ancient capital of China, is a city where history and modernity collide. With its efficient high-speed rail network, traveling to and from Nanjing has never been easier. Whether you’re heading to the bustling streets of Shanghai, the scenic beauty of Hangzhou, or the cultural wonders of Beijing, packing smartly for your high-speed rail journey can make all the difference. Here’s your ultimate guide to packing for a seamless trip on Nanjing’s high-speed rail.

Why Packing Smart Matters for High-Speed Rail Travel

Unlike air travel, high-speed rail offers more flexibility in terms of luggage allowances, but that doesn’t mean you should overpack. The key is to bring essentials that maximize comfort, convenience, and efficiency. Since high-speed rail stations are often located in city centers, you’ll want to avoid dragging heavy suitcases through crowded terminals.

The Golden Rule: Less is More

High-speed rail cabins have overhead storage and space near your seat, but bulky luggage can be a hassle. Stick to a carry-on-sized suitcase or a spacious backpack to keep things manageable.

Essential Packing List for Your Nanjing High-Speed Rail Trip

1. Travel Documents & Tech Gear

  • Ticket & ID: Keep your high-speed rail ticket (electronic or printed) and passport/ID easily accessible.
  • Power Bank: Trains have outlets, but a backup power bank ensures your devices stay charged.
  • Noise-Canceling Headphones: Perfect for blocking out cabin noise during the journey.
  • Portable Wi-Fi or SIM Card: While some trains offer Wi-Fi, having your own connection ensures uninterrupted browsing.

2. Comfort Essentials

  • Neck Pillow & Light Blanket: Trains are climate-controlled, but a small blanket adds extra coziness.
  • Reusable Water Bottle: Stay hydrated—most trains have hot and cold water dispensers.
  • Snacks: Pack light, non-messy snacks like nuts, granola bars, or fruit.
  • Hand Sanitizer & Wet Wipes: Keep germs at bay, especially during peak travel seasons.

3. Clothing & Footwear

  • Layered Outfits: Trains can be chilly, so bring a light jacket or cardigan.
  • Slip-On Shoes: Easy to remove during long rides and comfortable for walking in stations.
  • Scarf or Shawl: Doubles as a blanket or pillow in a pinch.

4. Entertainment & Leisure

  • E-Reader or Book: Long rides are perfect for catching up on reading.
  • Travel Journal: Jot down memories or plan your Nanjing itinerary.
  • Offline Games or Movies: Download content beforehand in case of spotty connectivity.

Pro Tips for a Stress-Free Journey

Arrive Early, But Not Too Early

High-speed rail stations in Nanjing, like Nanjing South Railway Station, are well-organized, but security checks can take time. Arrive 30-45 minutes before departure to avoid last-minute stress.

Use Luggage Storage Wisely

If you’re making a quick stop in Nanjing, consider using station lockers to store bulky bags while you explore nearby attractions like Confucius Temple or Xuanwu Lake.

Download Useful Apps

Apps like 12306 (China’s official rail booking platform) or Didi (ride-hailing) can help with last-minute ticket changes or transport needs.

What Not to Pack

  • Excessive liquids: While restrictions are looser than flights, spills are still a nuisance.
  • Strong-smelling food: Be considerate of fellow passengers—skip the durian or stinky tofu.
  • Valuables in checked luggage: Keep important items in your personal bag.

Making the Most of Your Nanjing Stopover

If you have time before your next train, drop your bags and explore:
- Nanjing South Railway Station’s shopping area for last-minute souvenirs.
- A quick metro ride to Fuzimiao (Confucius Temple) for street food and culture.
- A stroll along Qinhuai River to soak in the city’s historic charm.

Packing for a high-speed rail trip is all about balancing practicality and comfort. With these tips, you’ll glide through your journey effortlessly, leaving more time to enjoy Nanjing’s vibrant sights and sounds. Happy travels!
