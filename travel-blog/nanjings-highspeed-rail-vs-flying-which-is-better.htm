Nanjing’s High-Speed Rail vs. Flying: Which is Better?

  • Home
  • Travel Blog
  • Blog Details

Traveling between cities in China has never been easier, thanks to the country’s extensive high-speed rail network and well-developed aviation system. Nanjing, a bustling metropolis with rich history and modern attractions, is a major transportation hub. But when it comes to getting in or out of the city, should you choose the high-speed rail or a flight? Let’s break down the pros and cons to help you decide.

Speed and Travel Time

High-Speed Rail: Fast and Efficient

China’s high-speed trains, or gaotie, are renowned for their punctuality and speed. From Nanjing, you can reach cities like Shanghai in just over an hour, Beijing in about 3.5 hours, and Hangzhou in under 2 hours. The trains are incredibly reliable, with minimal delays compared to flights.

One major advantage is that high-speed rail stations are usually located closer to city centers than airports. Nanjing South Railway Station, for example, is well-connected to the metro system, making it easy to reach downtown quickly.

Flying: Quick but with Hidden Delays

For longer distances, flying might seem like the obvious choice. A flight from Nanjing to Guangzhou takes around 2 hours, compared to roughly 7 hours by train. However, this doesn’t account for the time spent traveling to the airport (often far from the city), security checks, boarding, and potential delays.

Nanjing Lukou International Airport is about 35 kilometers from the city center, meaning you’ll need at least an hour to get there by taxi or metro. Add in the recommended 2-hour pre-flight arrival, and the total travel time can easily surpass that of a high-speed rail journey for mid-range distances.

Cost Comparison

High-Speed Rail: Affordable and Transparent

Train tickets are generally cheaper than flights, especially for shorter routes. A second-class ticket from Nanjing to Shanghai costs around ¥140 (about $20), while a flight might be double that, not including baggage fees.

First-class and business-class train seats offer extra comfort at a fraction of the price of a flight’s premium cabin. There are no hidden fees—what you see is what you pay.

Flying: Variable Pricing and Extra Fees

Airfare prices fluctuate based on demand, and budget airlines often charge extra for checked luggage, seat selection, and even snacks. If you’re booking last-minute, flights can become significantly more expensive than high-speed rail.

That said, for long-distance travel (e.g., Nanjing to Chengdu), flights might still be the more economical choice when considering time saved.

Comfort and Experience

High-Speed Rail: Spacious and Scenic

Train travel in China is remarkably comfortable. Seats are wider than airplane seats, legroom is generous, and you can freely walk around. Many trains have dining cars, power outlets, and free Wi-Fi (though speeds vary).

The biggest perk? The view. Unlike flying, where you’re above the clouds, high-speed rail lets you see China’s changing landscapes—lush countryside, sprawling cities, and misty mountains.

Flying: Convenience at High Altitudes

If you prioritize speed and don’t mind cramped spaces, flying might suit you. Modern planes offer in-flight entertainment, and business class provides lie-flat seats on longer routes. However, turbulence, limited movement, and dry cabin air can make flights less enjoyable for some.

Luggage and Flexibility

High-Speed Rail: No Weight Limits

Train travel is a dream for heavy packers. There are no strict baggage weight restrictions, and overhead racks are spacious. You can even bring your own food and drinks without restrictions.

Missed your train? Tickets can often be exchanged for a small fee, and frequent departures mean you won’t wait long for the next one.

Flying: Strict Policies and Fees

Airlines enforce strict baggage allowances, and exceeding them can lead to hefty fees. Security checks mean liquids are limited, and last-minute changes can be costly. However, for international travel, flying is the only viable option.

Environmental Impact

High-Speed Rail: The Greener Choice

Trains produce significantly fewer carbon emissions per passenger than planes. If sustainability is a priority, high-speed rail is the clear winner.

Flying: Faster but Less Eco-Friendly

Air travel has a much larger carbon footprint. While some airlines are investing in biofuels and carbon offset programs, flying remains less environmentally friendly.

Final Thoughts for Travelers

For trips under 1,000 kilometers (e.g., Nanjing to Xian), high-speed rail is often the better choice—faster door-to-door, cheaper, and more comfortable. For longer distances or international travel, flying may still be necessary.

Your decision ultimately depends on priorities: time, cost, comfort, or eco-friendliness. Either way, Nanjing’s excellent transport links ensure a smooth journey.
