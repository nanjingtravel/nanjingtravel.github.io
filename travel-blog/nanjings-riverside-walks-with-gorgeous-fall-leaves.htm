Nanjing’s Riverside Walks with Gorgeous Fall Leaves

  • Home
  • Travel Blog
  • Blog Details

Few cities blend history and natural beauty as seamlessly as Nanjing. When autumn arrives, the city transforms into a golden paradise, especially along its scenic riverside walks. The crisp air, the rustling leaves, and the tranquil waters create an unforgettable experience for travelers and locals alike. If you’re looking for the perfect fall getaway, Nanjing’s riverside paths should be at the top of your list.

The Best Riverside Walks for Fall Foliage

Xuanwu Lake Perimeter

Xuanwu Lake is a crown jewel of Nanjing, and its perimeter walk is a must-visit in autumn. The lake is surrounded by ginkgo and maple trees, which turn vibrant shades of yellow and red as the season progresses. Strolling along the lakeside, you’ll encounter picturesque bridges, traditional pavilions, and even small boats gliding across the water. The reflection of the foliage on the lake’s surface doubles the beauty, making it a photographer’s dream.

Pro Tip: Visit early in the morning to avoid crowds and catch the soft golden light filtering through the leaves.

Qinhuai River Scenic Belt

The Qinhuai River is the lifeblood of Nanjing’s cultural heritage, and its riverside walk is especially enchanting in fall. The mix of historic architecture and autumn colors creates a nostalgic atmosphere. As you walk, you’ll pass by ancient temples, teahouses, and stone-carved bridges draped in ivy and climbing vines that turn fiery red.

Don’t Miss: The night cruise along the Qinhuai River, where the illuminated trees and lantern-lit boats create a magical scene.

Yangtze River Promenade

For a more expansive view of autumn’s grandeur, head to the Yangtze River Promenade. The wide-open spaces and towering trees make this a fantastic spot for a leisurely walk or bike ride. The contrast between the golden leaves and the mighty Yangtze River is breathtaking.

Bonus: Nearby, you’ll find the Nanjing Eye Footbridge, a modern architectural marvel that offers panoramic views of the river and the city’s skyline.

Hidden Gems for Leaf Peeping

Mochou Lake Park

Tucked away in the southwestern part of the city, Mochou Lake Park is a serene escape from the urban hustle. The weeping willows and ginkgo trees here create a delicate golden canopy, perfect for a quiet afternoon stroll. The park’s namesake lake adds to the tranquility, with fallen leaves floating gently on its surface.

Local Secret: Visit the park’s teahouse for a warm cup of tea while enjoying the view.

Purple Mountain’s Forest Trails

While not strictly a riverside walk, Purple Mountain’s forested slopes are a must for fall foliage lovers. The winding paths lead you through dense groves of maple, oak, and pine trees, all ablaze with autumn hues. The Ming Xiaoling Mausoleum, nestled within the mountain, adds a historical touch to the natural beauty.

Hiking Tip: Wear comfortable shoes—some trails are steep but worth the effort for the sweeping views.

Fall-Themed Activities Along the Riverside

Photography Walks

Autumn in Nanjing is a photographer’s paradise. Many local photography clubs organize guided walks along the riverside, offering tips on capturing the best angles of the foliage. Whether you’re using a smartphone or a professional camera, the vibrant colors and reflections provide endless creative opportunities.

Picnics with a View

Pack a basket with local treats like salted duck, sesame cakes, and hot osmanthus tea, then find a quiet spot along the riverbank. The combination of good food and stunning scenery makes for a perfect autumn afternoon.

Bike Tours

Renting a bike is a fantastic way to cover more ground while enjoying the crisp fall air. Many rental shops near Xuanwu Lake and Qinhuai River offer hourly or daily rates, with routes that take you past the best foliage spots.

When to Visit for Peak Fall Colors

The best time to experience Nanjing’s autumn splendor is from mid-October to late November. The exact timing varies each year depending on weather conditions, but this window typically offers the most vibrant displays. Early mornings and weekdays are ideal for avoiding crowds.

Final Thoughts on Packing

  • Layered clothing – Mornings and evenings can be chilly, while afternoons are pleasantly cool.
  • Comfortable walking shoes – You’ll be covering a lot of ground.
  • A good camera or smartphone – You’ll want to capture every moment.
  • A reusable water bottle – Stay hydrated while exploring.

Nanjing’s riverside walks in autumn are more than just a visual treat—they’re an immersive experience that blends nature, history, and culture. Whether you’re a solo traveler, a couple, or a family, these golden pathways offer something unforgettable for everyone.
