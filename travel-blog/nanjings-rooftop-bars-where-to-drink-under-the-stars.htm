Nanjing’s Rooftop Bars: Where to Drink Under the Stars

  • Home
  • Travel Blog
  • Blog Details

Nanjing, a city steeped in history and modern charm, offers a vibrant nightlife scene that’s best experienced from above. Rooftop bars here combine stunning skyline views, creative cocktails, and a relaxed ambiance, making them perfect for travelers and locals alike. Whether you’re looking for a romantic evening, a lively night out with friends, or a quiet spot to unwind, Nanjing’s rooftop bars deliver unforgettable moments under the stars.

The Allure of Nanjing’s Rooftop Bars

There’s something magical about sipping a drink while gazing at a cityscape bathed in twilight. Nanjing’s rooftop bars capture this essence, blending the city’s rich cultural backdrop with contemporary flair. From sleek, modern lounges to cozy, intimate terraces, each venue offers a unique perspective of the city.

Why Rooftop Bars?

  • Breathtaking Views: Overlook the Qinhuai River, the neon-lit skyline, or even the distant Purple Mountain.
  • Atmosphere: Open-air settings with stylish décor, live music, and a cosmopolitan crowd.
  • Culinary Delights: Many spots serve gourmet bites alongside expertly crafted cocktails.

Top Rooftop Bars in Nanjing

1. Flair Rooftop Bar at The Ritz-Carlton

Location: Xinjiekou District

Perched on the 62nd floor of The Ritz-Carlton, Flair is Nanjing’s highest rooftop bar. The panoramic views are unmatched, stretching across the Yangtze River and the city’s glittering skyline.

Highlights:
- Signature cocktails like the Jinling Mule (a local twist on the Moscow Mule).
- Luxe, modern interiors with a spacious outdoor terrace.
- Live DJ sets on weekends.

Pro Tip: Arrive before sunset to secure a prime spot—the golden hour here is legendary.

2. The Roof at Mandarin Oriental

Location: Hexi District

Elegant and understated, The Roof offers a refined rooftop experience. The bar’s minimalist design lets the views take center stage, with the Qinhuai River shimmering below.

Highlights:
- An extensive wine list featuring local Jiangsu vintages.
- Seasonal mixology—try their Osmanthus Sour in autumn.
- Private cabanas for intimate gatherings.

3. Cloud 9 at InterContinental

Location: Gulou District

A favorite among young professionals, Cloud 9 blends trendy vibes with laid-back comfort. The bar’s infinity pool (for guests only) adds a resort-like touch.

Highlights:
- Creative shareable platters, like spicy Sichuan-style tapas.
- Themed nights, from jazz evenings to cocktail masterclasses.
- Instagram-worthy neon lighting.

4. Sky Lounge 1912

Location: 1912 Nightlife District

For those who want to be in the heart of the action, Sky Lounge sits above Nanjing’s bustling 1912 district. The vibe here is energetic, with a mix of locals and expats.

Highlights:
- Affordable happy hour deals (5–7 PM).
- A retro-chic interior with vintage Nanjing posters.
- Great for group outings—large tables available.

5. The Secret Garden at En Space

Location: Lao Men Dong

Tucked away in the historic Lao Men Dong area, this hidden gem feels like an escape from the city. The bar is surrounded by lush greenery and fairy lights, creating a whimsical atmosphere.

Highlights:
- Botanical-inspired cocktails (try the Purple Mountain Spritz).
- Cozy, bohemian décor with hammocks and wooden swings.
- Live acoustic performances on Fridays.

What to Drink: Nanjing’s Rooftop Bar Specialties

Nanjing’s mixologists love infusing local flavors into their creations. Here are some must-try drinks:

1. Jinling Fizz

A refreshing blend of baijiu, lychee liqueur, soda, and a dash of osmanthus syrup.

2. Qinhuai Sunset

Tequila-based with dragon fruit puree and lime—vibrant in color and taste.

3. Nanjing Old Fashioned

A twist on the classic, using aged rice wine instead of whiskey.

Tips for Visiting Nanjing’s Rooftop Bars

  • Dress Code: Most upscale bars enforce a smart-casual dress code (no flip-flops or tank tops).
  • Reservations: Popular spots like Flair and The Roof often require advance booking, especially on weekends.
  • Weather Check: Rooftop bars are best enjoyed in spring or autumn—summer can be humid, and winter may close outdoor seating.
  • Transport: Use Didi (China’s Uber) or the metro—many bars are near Xinjiekou or Gulou stations.

Beyond the Bars: Nearby Attractions

Make it a full evening by exploring these nearby hotspots:

  • Confucius Temple (Fuzimiao): Stroll along the Qinhuai River after drinks.
  • Xinjiekou Shopping District: High-end retail therapy by day, vibrant nightlife by night.
  • Purple Mountain: If you’re an early riser, hike up for sunrise after a late-night rooftop session.

Nanjing’s rooftop bars are more than just places to drink—they’re gateways to experiencing the city’s dynamic spirit. Whether you’re toasting to a special occasion or simply soaking in the views, these elevated escapes promise memories as sparkling as the skyline itself.
