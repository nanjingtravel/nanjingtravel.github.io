Nanjing’s Street Food Hotspots: Where Locals Love to Eat

  • Home
  • Travel Blog
  • Blog Details

Nanjing, a city steeped in history and culture, is also a paradise for food lovers. While its ancient walls and scenic spots draw tourists, the real magic happens on the streets—where sizzling woks, aromatic spices, and the chatter of locals create an unforgettable culinary experience. If you’re looking to dive into Nanjing’s authentic flavors, here’s your guide to the street food hotspots locals swear by.

The Heart of Nanjing’s Street Food Scene

Nanjing’s street food culture is a vibrant mix of tradition and innovation. From time-honored snacks passed down through generations to modern twists on classics, the city’s alleys and night markets are a treasure trove of delicious discoveries.

Confucius Temple (Fuzimiao) Area

No visit to Nanjing is complete without exploring the Confucius Temple area. While it’s a major tourist attraction, it’s also a goldmine for street food. Locals and visitors alike flock here for:

  • Duck Blood Vermicelli Soup (鸭血粉丝汤, Yāxiě Fěnsī Tāng): A Nanjing staple, this hearty soup features silky vermicelli, tender duck blood, and a rich, savory broth.
  • Pan-Fried Dumplings (锅贴, Guōtiē): Crispy on the outside, juicy on the inside, these dumplings are often stuffed with pork or beef.
  • Sweet Osmanthus Cake (桂花糕, Guìhuā Gāo): A fragrant, chewy dessert made with osmanthus flowers, perfect for satisfying your sweet tooth.

Pro tip: Venture into the smaller alleys around Fuzimiao for hidden gems where locals queue up.

Shiziqiao (Lion Bridge) Night Market

For a more local experience, head to Shiziqiao Night Market. This bustling spot comes alive after sunset, offering everything from skewers to seafood. Must-tries include:

  • Grilled Squid (烤鱿鱼, Kǎo Yóuyú): Freshly grilled and slathered in a spicy-sweet sauce, this is a crowd favorite.
  • Stinky Tofu (臭豆腐, Chòu Dòufu): Love it or hate it, this fermented tofu dish is a Nanjing classic. The crispy exterior and pungent aroma are part of the charm.
  • Jianbing (煎饼): A savory crepe filled with eggs, crispy wonton skins, and sauces, perfect for a quick bite.

Hidden Gems: Where the Locals Go

While tourist spots are great, the real magic lies in the neighborhoods where locals dine daily. Here are a few under-the-radar spots:

Laomendong (Old Gate East)

This historic area is a foodie’s dream, blending traditional architecture with mouthwatering eats. Don’t miss:

  • Saltwater Duck (盐水鸭, Yánshuǐ Yā): Nanjing’s signature dish, featuring tender, brine-cured duck with a subtle salty flavor.
  • Xiaolongbao (小笼包): These soup-filled dumplings are a must, especially at family-run stalls.

Hunan Road Food Street

A favorite among locals, Hunan Road offers a mix of street food and sit-down eateries. Highlights include:

  • Nanjing Fried Noodles (南京炒面, Nánjīng Chǎomiàn): Chewy noodles stir-fried with veggies and your choice of meat.
  • Pearl Balls (珍珠丸子, Zhēnzhū Wánzi): Glutinous rice-coated pork balls, steamed to perfection.

Seasonal Delights: Street Food by the Season

Nanjing’s street food scene changes with the seasons, offering unique treats year-round.

Spring

  • Qingtuan (青团): Glutinous rice balls filled with sweet red bean paste, tinted green with mugwort.

Summer

  • Cold Noodles (凉面, Liángmiàn): Refreshing noodles tossed with sesame sauce and chili oil.

Autumn

  • Chestnut Cake (栗子糕, Lìzi Gāo): A warm, nutty dessert made with fresh chestnuts.

Winter

  • Hot Pot Skewers (串串香, Chuànchuàn Xiāng): Spicy broths for dipping skewers of meat and veggies, perfect for chilly nights.

Tips for Exploring Nanjing’s Street Food

  1. Follow the Crowds: If a stall has a long line, it’s usually worth the wait.
  2. Cash is King: Many small vendors don’t accept digital payments.
  3. Embrace the Chaos: Street food is about the experience—savor the flavors and the lively atmosphere.

Nanjing’s street food is more than just sustenance; it’s a window into the city’s soul. Whether you’re slurping noodles at a bustling night market or biting into a crispy jianbing on a quiet alley, every bite tells a story. So grab your chopsticks and dive in—the best of Nanjing awaits!
