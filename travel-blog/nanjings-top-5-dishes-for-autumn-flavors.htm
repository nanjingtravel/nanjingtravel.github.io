Nanjing’s Top 5 Dishes for Autumn Flavors

  • Home
  • Travel Blog
  • Blog Details

As the crisp autumn breeze sweeps through Nanjing, the city transforms into a culinary paradise. Known for its rich history and vibrant food culture, Nanjing offers a unique blend of seasonal dishes that capture the essence of fall. Whether you're a foodie exploring street markets or a traveler seeking authentic local flavors, these five dishes will warm your soul and tantalize your taste buds.

1. Salted Duck (Yánshuǐ Yā)

A Nanjing Classic with Autumn Twists

Nanjing’s salted duck is a year-round favorite, but autumn brings a special depth to this dish. The cooler weather enhances the curing process, resulting in tender, flavorful meat with a perfect balance of saltiness and umami. Traditionally marinated in a blend of spices and salt, then air-dried, the duck is often served cold as an appetizer or paired with warm congee.

Where to Try It

Head to Zhāng Hóngxīng Salted Duck (a legendary local spot) or explore the bustling Fuzimiao (Confucius Temple) area, where vendors serve it fresh alongside other street snacks.

2. Osmanthus Cake (Guìhuā Gāo)

Sweetness of the Season

Autumn in Nanjing is synonymous with osmanthus blossoms, and this fragrant flower takes center stage in the city’s desserts. Osmanthus cake, a delicate steamed treat, combines glutinous rice flour with sweet osmanthus syrup, creating a floral, slightly sticky texture. It’s often enjoyed with a cup of local tea, making it a perfect afternoon pick-me-up.

Pro Tip

Visit Lao Men Dong (a historic food street) in October when osmanthus blooms are at their peak. Many bakeries offer limited-edition versions infused with autumn ingredients like chestnuts.

3. Crab Roe Dumplings (Xièhuáng Tāngbāo)

A Luxurious Autumn Bite

As crab season peaks in fall, Nanjing’s chefs craft these indulgent soup dumplings filled with rich crab roe and minced pork. The broth inside bursts with savory-sweet flavors, while the thin, delicate wrapper holds everything together. Each bite is a celebration of seasonal seafood.

Best Places to Savor Them

  • Jinling Restaurant: A high-end spot known for its refined take on the dish.
  • Nanjing Da Pai Dang: A casual eatery where locals flock for affordable, authentic versions.

4. Duck Blood and Vermicelli Soup (Yāxuè Fěnsī Tāng)

Comfort in a Bowl

This hearty soup is a Nanjing staple, but autumn’s chill makes it even more satisfying. Silky duck blood curds, chewy vermicelli, and a savory broth infused with spices create a warming dish. Locals swear by its restorative properties, especially during seasonal transitions.

Where to Find the Best Bowls

  • Ye’s Duck Blood Soup: A no-frills joint with a cult following.
  • Night markets near Xinjiekou: Perfect for late-night cravings.

5. Chestnut and Sweet Potato Roast (Lìzi Hóngshǔ Kǎo)

Street Food Heaven

Autumn wouldn’t be complete without roasted chestnuts and sweet potatoes, and Nanjing’s street vendors master this simple yet irresistible combo. The smoky aroma of charred sweet potatoes and crackling chestnuts fills the air, offering a nostalgic snack for chilly evenings.

Insider’s Pick

Look for vendors around Xuanwu Lake Park, where you can enjoy your treats while strolling along the scenic lakeside.

Bonus: Autumn Food Tours & Experiences

For the Ultimate Foodie Adventure

  • Qinhuai River Night Cruise with Snacks: Pair sightseeing with seasonal bites.
  • Nanjing Agricultural University Market: A hidden gem for fresh, local produce.

Whether you’re savoring salted duck by the Qinhuai River or biting into a steaming crab roe dumpling, Nanjing’s autumn flavors are a feast for the senses. Pack your appetite and explore—the city’s culinary treasures await!
