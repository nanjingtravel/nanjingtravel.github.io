Nanjing’s Top Shopping Streets for Bargain Hunters

  • Home
  • Travel Blog
  • Blog Details

Nanjing, a city steeped in history and culture, is also a paradise for shoppers looking for great deals. Whether you're hunting for trendy fashion, unique souvenirs, or local snacks, the city’s bustling shopping streets offer something for every budget. Here’s a guide to the best spots where bargain hunters can score amazing finds while soaking in Nanjing’s vibrant atmosphere.

The Must-Visit Shopping Streets

Confucius Temple (Fuzimiao) Pedestrian Street

One of Nanjing’s most iconic destinations, Fuzimiao isn’t just about history—it’s a shopper’s dream. The pedestrian street surrounding the Confucius Temple is packed with stalls selling everything from traditional handicrafts to modern accessories.

  • What to Buy: Silk products, calligraphy sets, and Nanjing-style souvenirs like Yuhua stones.
  • Bargaining Tip: Vendors here expect haggling, so start at 50% of the asking price and negotiate from there.
  • Bonus: Don’t miss the food stalls offering local delicacies like salted duck and tanghulu (candied fruit skewers).

Hunan Road Shopping District

A favorite among locals and tourists alike, Hunan Road is lined with shops offering mid-range to budget-friendly fashion, electronics, and cosmetics.

  • Best Stores: Look for underground markets and small boutiques where prices are flexible.
  • Evening Deals: Visit after sunset when some vendors drop prices to clear daily stock.

Hidden Gems for Thrifty Shoppers

Nanjing Yunjin Road Fabric Market

If you love custom-made clothing or high-quality fabrics at unbeatable prices, this is the place to go.

  • Tailor Services: Bring a design or choose from catalogs to get a custom qipao or suit made in days.
  • Fabric Selection: From luxurious silks to affordable cottons, the variety is astounding.

Shiziqiao Flea Market

For those who enjoy treasure hunting, Shiziqiao offers vintage items, second-hand goods, and quirky collectibles.

  • Best Finds: Antique jewelry, old books, and retro electronics.
  • Timing: Arrive early on weekends for the best selection.

Modern Shopping with Discounts

Deji Plaza and Xinjiekou Area

While Deji Plaza is known for luxury brands, the surrounding Xinjiekou area has smaller shops and seasonal sales.

  • Discount Seasons: Major holidays like Chinese New Year and Singles’ Day (November 11) bring huge markdowns.
  • Underground Malls: Explore the connected subway shopping zones for budget-friendly fashion.

Hexi Wanda Plaza

A newer shopping hub with a mix of international brands and local retailers, often offering promotions.

  • Food Court Perks: Refuel with affordable local dishes after a long shopping spree.

Tips for Bargain Shopping in Nanjing

  • Cash is King: Some smaller vendors prefer cash and may offer discounts for it.
  • Learn Basic Mandarin Phrases: Simple phrases like "Tai gui le!" (Too expensive!) can help in negotiations.
  • Compare Prices: Check multiple stalls before committing to a purchase.

Nanjing’s shopping streets blend tradition with modernity, making every bargain hunt an adventure. Whether you’re exploring ancient markets or sleek malls, the thrill of finding a great deal is always around the corner.
