Nanjing’s Transport for Photography Hotspots

  • Home
  • Travel Blog
  • Blog Details

Nanjing, a city steeped in history and modern charm, offers countless opportunities for photographers. From ancient temples to bustling urban scenes, the city is a visual feast. But to capture its essence, you need to know how to get around efficiently. This guide covers the best transport options to reach Nanjing’s top photography hotspots, ensuring you don’t miss a single shot.

Navigating Nanjing’s Public Transport

Metro: The Fastest Way to Iconic Spots

Nanjing’s metro system is clean, efficient, and photographer-friendly. With over 10 lines covering major attractions, it’s the best way to avoid traffic while carrying heavy gear.

  • Line 1: Stops at Xinjiekou (Nanjing’s bustling shopping district) and Confucius Temple (Fuzimiao), perfect for street photography and night scenes.
  • Line 2: Connects to Zhongshan Ling (Sun Yat-sen Mausoleum), where grand architecture meets lush greenery.
  • Line 3: Takes you to Presidential Palace, a blend of Chinese and Western architectural styles.

Pro tip: Avoid rush hours (7:30–9:00 AM and 5:00–7:00 PM) to travel comfortably with your equipment.

Buses: Scenic Routes for Casual Shoots

While slower, buses offer unique vantage points. Route Y1 (Tourist Bus 1) loops around Xuanwu Lake and Jiming Temple, providing glimpses of lakeside reflections and golden temple roofs. For a nostalgic vibe, hop on the 33 Bus, which passes through Lao Men Dong, Nanjing’s historic quarter.

Taxis & Ride-Hailing: Flexibility for Offbeat Locations

Sometimes, the best shots are in less accessible areas. Apps like Didi (China’s Uber) are reliable for reaching spots like Qixia Mountain (fall foliage) or Yangtze River Bridge (dramatic sunsets).

  • Early morning tip: Book a taxi for sunrise shoots at Zifeng Tower—drivers know the quickest routes.
  • Negotiate flat rates for remote locations like Niushou Mountain to avoid meter surprises.

Cycling: Capture the City’s Pulse

Nanjing’s bike-sharing system (Hellobike, Meituan Bike) is ideal for exploring photogenic alleys and riverside paths.

  • Qinhuai River Trail: Pedal along the water, stopping for shots of traditional boats and lantern-lit bridges.
  • Ming Xiaoling Mausoleum: Rent a bike to cover the vast Sacred Way, lined with stone statues.

Warning: Avoid cycling in downtown traffic during peak hours.

Photography Hotspots & How to Reach Them

1. Confucius Temple (Fuzimiao)

  • Transport: Metro Line 3 (Fuzimiao Station) or a short walk from Laomendong.
  • Best time: Evening for neon-lit streets and river reflections.

2. Sun Yat-sen Mausoleum

  • Transport: Metro Line 2 to Muxuyuan Station, then walk or take shuttle bus No. 34.
  • Pro shot: The 392-step staircase creates a striking symmetrical composition.

3. Xuanwu Lake

  • Transport: Metro Line 1 (Xuanwu Gate Station) or bus Y1.
  • Golden hour: Frame the lake with the Nanjing skyline at dusk.

4. Nanjing Yangtze River Bridge

  • Transport: Didi/taxi (no nearby metro).
  • Safety note: Tripods are allowed but avoid obstructing pedestrian pathways.

5. Qixia Temple (Autumn Foliage)

  • Transport: Take metro Line 2 to Xianlin Center, then Didi (15-minute ride).
  • Seasonal tip: Mid-November for fiery red maple leaves.

Gear-Friendly Transport Tips

  • Backpacks over rollers: Metro escalators and temple stairs aren’t suitcase-friendly.
  • Peak-hour avoidance: Crowded transport = higher risk of gear damage.
  • Taxi hacks: Learn the phrase “Qǐng màn yīdiǎn” (“Please drive slower”) for smooth long-exposure rides.

Hidden Gems: Off-the-Grid Transport

  • Ferry rides: The Zhongshan Wharf–Pukou ferry (¥2) offers unspoiled river views.
  • Electric tuk-tuks: Bargain for a ride to Shitou Cheng, a lesser-known ancient fortress.

Nanjing’s transport network is a gateway to visual storytelling. Whether you’re chasing the perfect sunrise or a fleeting street moment, the right route makes all the difference.
