Nanjing’s Transport for Short Layovers

  • Home
  • Travel Blog
  • Blog Details

Nanjing, a city steeped in history and modernity, is a fantastic destination for travelers with short layovers. Whether you’re stopping by for a few hours or a full day, the city’s efficient transport system makes it easy to explore key attractions without the stress of getting lost or wasting time. Here’s how to navigate Nanjing’s transport options and squeeze in some unforgettable experiences during your brief stay.

Getting from Nanjing Lukou International Airport to the City

Nanjing Lukou International Airport (NKG) is approximately 35 kilometers from the city center, but thanks to multiple transport options, you can reach downtown in no time.

By Metro (Subway)

The airport is connected to Line S1 of the Nanjing Metro, which links directly to Nanjing South Railway Station. From there, you can transfer to Line 1 or Line 3 to reach popular areas like Xinjiekou (the city’s bustling commercial hub) or Confucius Temple (Fuzimiao). The metro is fast, affordable (around ¥7-10), and runs from 6:00 AM to 11:00 PM.

By Taxi or Ride-Hailing Apps

If you’re short on time or traveling with luggage, a taxi or ride-hailing service (like Didi) is a convenient choice. The ride to downtown takes about 40-60 minutes, depending on traffic, and costs around ¥100-150.

Airport Shuttle Buses

For budget-conscious travelers, airport shuttle buses operate on several routes, dropping passengers at major transport hubs like Nanjing Railway Station. Tickets cost around ¥20-30, and the journey takes roughly an hour.

Must-Visit Spots for Short Layovers

With limited time, focus on attractions close to transport hubs. Here are three top picks:

1. Confucius Temple (Fuzimiao)

A cultural and historical gem, Fuzimiao is easily accessible via Metro Line 3. Stroll along the Qinhuai River, sample local snacks like salted duck (Nanjing’s famous delicacy), and browse traditional handicrafts. The area is especially magical at night when lanterns illuminate the waterways.

2. Xinjiekou Shopping District

Nanjing’s answer to Times Square, Xinjiekou is a shopper’s paradise. Reachable via Metro Line 1 or 2, this area boasts luxury malls, street food vendors, and vibrant nightlife. Don’t miss the iconic Sun Yat-sen Mausoleum if you have extra time—it’s a short taxi ride away.

3. Xuanwu Lake Park

For a breath of fresh air, head to Xuanwu Lake Park, a short walk from Metro Line 1 or 4. Rent a paddleboat, cycle around the scenic paths, or simply relax by the water. The nearby Nanjing Museum (free entry!) is also worth a quick visit if you’re into history.

Pro Tips for Smooth Layovers

  • Luggage Storage: If you’re burdened with bags, use the luggage storage services at Nanjing South Railway Station or the airport (¥10-20 per item).
  • Mobile Payments: Have Alipay or WeChat Pay set up—cash is rarely used in Nanjing’s metros and taxis.
  • Timing: Avoid rush hours (7:30-9:30 AM and 5:00-7:00 PM) to dodge metro crowds.

Nanjing’s blend of ancient charm and modern efficiency makes it ideal for layover adventures. With smart planning, even a short stop can turn into a memorable mini-vacation.
