Parks for a Perfect Picnic in Nanjing

  • Home
  • Travel Blog
  • Blog Details

Nanjing, a city where ancient history and modern life intertwine, offers some of the most picturesque and serene parks perfect for a delightful picnic. As the capital of Jiangsu Province, this city is not only rich in cultural heritage but also boasts an array of green spaces that cater to both locals and tourists seeking a peaceful escape. With the rising trend of outdoor leisure activities, especially post-pandemic, picnicking has become a popular way to enjoy Nanjing's natural beauty. From the iconic Xuanwu Lake to the lush greenery of Zhongshan Mountain Scenic Area, each park provides a unique backdrop for a perfect picnic. In this guide, we'll explore the top parks in Nanjing that are ideal for spreading out a blanket, unpacking a basket of local delicacies, and soaking in the vibrant atmosphere. Whether you're a history buff, a nature lover, or simply looking for a relaxing day out, these spots promise an unforgettable experience that blends relaxation with exploration.

Xuanwu Lake Park: A Lakeside Paradise

Xuanwu Lake Park is one of Nanjing's most famous destinations, renowned for its expansive lake and stunning views of the city skyline. Located in the heart of Nanjing, this park covers an area of over 500 hectares and is divided into five islets connected by bridges, making it a perfect spot for a scenic picnic. The park's name, Xuanwu, refers to the Black Tortoise of Chinese mythology, adding a touch of ancient charm to your outing.

Why It's Perfect for a Picnic

The park offers numerous grassy areas and pavilions where you can set up your picnic spread. Imagine lounging by the water's edge, enjoying a meal while watching boats glide across the lake. The gentle breeze and the sound of rustling leaves create a tranquil ambiance that's hard to beat. Plus, the park is easily accessible by public transport, so you can bring along a basket filled with local treats like salted duck or sesame cakes from nearby markets.

Highlights and Activities

Aside from picnicking, Xuanwu Lake Park is a hub for outdoor activities. You can rent a paddleboat to explore the lake, visit the nearby Ming City Wall, or simply stroll along the pathways lined with willow trees. The park also hosts seasonal events, such as cherry blossom viewings in spring, which add to its appeal as a picnic hotspot. Don't forget to capture the sunset over the lake—it's a photographer's dream and a perfect way to end your day.

Zhongshan Mountain Scenic Area: A Natural Retreat

For those who prefer a more rugged and historical setting, the Zhongshan Mountain Scenic Area is an excellent choice. This vast area includes the Purple Mountain, which is dotted with ancient tombs, temples, and lush forests. It's a UNESCO World Heritage site candidate and offers a blend of natural beauty and cultural significance, making it a top pick for a picnic with an educational twist.

Picnic Spots Amidst History

Within the scenic area, you'll find several quiet clearings and designated picnic areas near landmarks like the Sun Yat-sen Mausoleum and the Linggu Temple. These spots provide a peaceful environment where you can enjoy your food surrounded by towering trees and the echoes of history. Pack a basket with some Jiangsu-style snacks, such as tangyuan (sweet rice balls) or a thermos of tea, to enhance the experience. The cooler mountain air makes it especially pleasant during the summer months.

Exploring the Surroundings

After your picnic, take advantage of the many hiking trails that wind through the mountain. You can visit the Ming Xiaoling Mausoleum, a majestic tomb from the Ming Dynasty, or explore the Nanjing Botanical Garden. The area is also popular for bird-watching and photography, so bring your camera to capture the diverse flora and fauna. This park is a must-visit for anyone interested in Nanjing's imperial past and natural wonders.

Mochou Lake Park: A Poetic Escape

Mochou Lake Park, named after the legendary figure Mochou (meaning "free from sorrow"), is a charming park that combines water features, gardens, and cultural elements. Located in the southwestern part of Nanjing, it's smaller than Xuanwu Lake but equally enchanting, with a rich history dating back to the Six Dynasties period.

Ideal Picnic Settings

The park features serene lakeside lawns and shaded areas under ancient trees, perfect for a intimate picnic. You can spread out a blanket near the lake and enjoy views of lotus flowers in bloom during the summer. The peaceful atmosphere is ideal for a relaxing day with family or friends, and the park's well-maintained facilities include benches and restrooms for convenience. Consider bringing some local fruits, like peaches from Nanjing's orchards, to complement your meal.

Cultural and Recreational Offerings

Mochou Lake Park is home to the Mochou Pavilion, which offers insights into local legends and poetry. You can also enjoy a leisurely walk along the winding paths or rent a small boat for a romantic ride on the lake. The park often hosts traditional performances and festivals, adding a cultural dimension to your picnic outing. It's a hidden gem that provides a quiet respite from the city's hustle and bustle.

Nanjing Green Expo Park: A Modern Oasis

For a more contemporary picnic experience, head to the Nanjing Green Expo Park, a large-scale park that was developed for the 2011 International Horticultural Exposition. Situated along the Yangtze River, this park showcases innovative garden designs, international pavilions, and sustainable landscapes, making it a favorite among younger crowds and families.

Picnic-Friendly Areas

The park boasts vast open lawns, themed gardens, and riverfront spots that are ideal for picnics. You can choose a sunny patch near one of the many sculptures or find a shady corner in the bamboo forest. The modern amenities, including food stalls and rental services, mean you can easily supplement your picnic with local street food or drinks. Try packing a mix of international and local flavors, such as Nanjing's famous duck blood soup or some fresh pastries, to match the park's global vibe.

Activities and Attractions

Beyond picnicking, the Green Expo Park offers a range of activities, from cycling along the river to exploring the various international gardens. The park also features playgrounds for children and art installations that change seasonally. It's a great place to spend a full day, especially if you're interested in eco-tourism and modern design. The panoramic views of the Yangtze River make for a stunning backdrop as you unwind with your picnic spread.

Qinhuai River Scenic Area: A Riverside Delight

The Qinhuai River Scenic Area is the heart of Nanjing's ancient culture, known for its historic canals, traditional architecture, and vibrant nightlife. While it might be busier than other parks, it offers unique picnic opportunities along its picturesque banks, blending urban energy with natural charm.

Picnic by the Water

Find a spot along the riverbank, perhaps near the Confucius Temple, to enjoy a picnic with a view of traditional boats and illuminated bridges. The area comes alive in the evening, so an afternoon picnic can be a great way to avoid the crowds while still soaking in the atmosphere. Pack some local snacks from the nearby food street, such as stinky tofu or sugar-coated haws, for an authentic taste of Nanjing.

Cultural Immersion and Entertainment

After your picnic, explore the surrounding historic sites, including the Zhonghua Gate and the ancient streets filled with shops and teahouses. You can also take a boat tour to learn about the river's history or enjoy a performance at the local theater. This park is perfect for those who want to combine a picnic with a cultural adventure, making it a highlight of any trip to Nanjing.

Overall, Nanjing's parks offer a diverse range of picnic experiences, from tranquil lakeside retreats to historically rich mountain escapes. Each one provides a unique opportunity to connect with nature, history, and local culture. So grab your picnic basket, gather your loved ones, and discover why these green spaces are the perfect setting for a memorable day out in this dynamic city.
