Private Nanjing Tours: How to Leave a Great Review for Your Guide

  • Home
  • Travel Blog
  • Blog Details

Exploring Nanjing with a private tour guide can transform your travel experience from ordinary to extraordinary. Whether you’re marveling at the Sun Yat-sen Mausoleum, strolling through the Confucius Temple, or uncovering the history of the Nanjing Massacre Memorial, a knowledgeable guide brings the city’s rich culture and heritage to life. After such an unforgettable journey, leaving a thoughtful review for your guide is a meaningful way to show appreciation and help future travelers make informed decisions. Here’s how to craft a standout review that highlights your experience.

Why Your Review Matters

Tour guides rely heavily on word-of-mouth and online feedback to attract new clients. A well-written review not only boosts their credibility but also assists other travelers in choosing the right guide for their Nanjing adventure. Platforms like TripAdvisor, Viator, and Google Reviews are often the first places potential visitors check before booking a tour.

The Impact of a Great Review

  • Helps guides stand out in a competitive market.
  • Provides valuable insights about the tour’s strengths.
  • Encourages guides to maintain high standards.

Key Elements of an Outstanding Review

1. Start with a Catchy Opening

Your first sentence should grab attention. Instead of a generic "Great tour!" try something like:
"Our private Nanjing tour with [Guide’s Name] was the highlight of our China trip—filled with fascinating stories, hidden gems, and personalized attention!"

2. Highlight What Made the Tour Special

Did your guide go above and beyond? Mention specific moments:
- Historical expertise: "Our guide’s deep knowledge of Ming Dynasty history made the Nanjing City Wall come alive."
- Flexibility: "When we mentioned an interest in local food, they adjusted the itinerary to include a stop at a famous xiaolongbao spot."
- Personal touches: "They even helped us bargain at the Qinhuai Night Market—saved us a fortune!"

3. Describe the Itinerary

Future travelers want to know what to expect. Briefly outline the key stops:
- Sun Yat-sen Mausoleum: "The guide’s storytelling made the climb feel effortless."
- Presidential Palace: "We loved the behind-the-scenes anecdotes about modern Chinese history."
- Zhonghua Gate: "Seeing the ancient fortifications at sunset was magical."

4. Mention Logistics

Was the guide punctual? Was communication smooth? Details like these matter:
- "Our guide arrived early with a comfortable, air-conditioned van."
- "They responded to emails within hours during the booking process."

5. Keep It Balanced

If there were minor hiccups, frame them constructively:
- "The only downside was the midday heat at the Ming Xiaoling Mausoleum, but our guide brought cold towels—genius!"

What to Avoid in Your Review

1. Vague Praise

Phrases like "Nice tour" don’t help much. Be specific!

2. Overly Critical Remarks

Unless there was a serious issue, avoid harsh language. Instead of "The guide was terrible," try "We wished there had been more time at the Nanjing Museum."

3. Personal Information

Never share private details like your guide’s phone number or last name.

Sample Review Template

Title: "An Unforgettable Journey Through Nanjing’s History!"

Rating: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ (5/5)

Review:
"From the moment we met [Guide’s Name], we knew we were in good hands. Their passion for Nanjing’s history was contagious—whether explaining the symbolism at the Sun Yat-sen Mausoleum or sharing little-known facts about the Qinhuai River. The tour was perfectly paced, blending major landmarks with off-the-beaten-path surprises. We especially loved the stop at a tucked-away tea house, where we sampled authentic Biluochun. Communication was flawless, and they even helped us arrange a last-minute river cruise. If you’re visiting Nanjing, don’t miss this experience!"

Where to Post Your Review

  • TripAdvisor: The go-to platform for tour reviews.
  • Google Reviews: Helps with local SEO for guides.
  • Viator/GetYourGuide: If you booked through these sites.
  • Social Media: Tag the tour company or guide for extra visibility.

Bonus: Tips for Future Travelers

  • Book early: Top guides fill up fast, especially during peak seasons like cherry blossom time.
  • Ask questions: A good guide will tailor the tour to your interests.
  • Bring cash: Some smaller vendors at stops like Lao Men Dong don’t accept cards.

By taking a few minutes to write a detailed review, you’re not just thanking your guide—you’re helping others discover the magic of Nanjing through their eyes. Happy travels!
