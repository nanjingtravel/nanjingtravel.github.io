The Best Calligraphy Workshops for Expats in Nanjing

  • Home
  • Travel Blog
  • Blog Details

Nanjing, a city steeped in history and culture, offers expats a unique opportunity to dive into the ancient art of Chinese calligraphy. Whether you're a curious beginner or a seasoned artist, participating in a calligraphy workshop can be a transformative experience. Here’s a curated list of the best calligraphy workshops in Nanjing, designed to help expats connect with this timeless tradition while exploring the city’s vibrant cultural scene.

Why Learn Calligraphy in Nanjing?

Nanjing, once the capital of China, is a treasure trove of historical landmarks and artistic heritage. Calligraphy, or shūfǎ (书法), is more than just writing—it’s a meditative practice that reflects the soul of Chinese culture. For expats, learning calligraphy is a gateway to understanding Nanjing’s rich history, from the Ming Dynasty to modern-day artistic innovations.

The Cultural Significance of Calligraphy

Calligraphy is one of China’s "Four Arts" (alongside painting, music, and chess). In Nanjing, you’ll find calligraphy etched into stone steles at the Confucius Temple or displayed in museums like the Nanjing Museum. By joining a workshop, you’re not just learning strokes—you’re engaging with a 3,000-year-old tradition.

Top Calligraphy Workshops for Expats

1. Nanjing University’s Cultural Exchange Program

Location: Gulou District
Duration: 4-week course (weekend sessions)

Nanjing University offers a structured calligraphy program tailored for expats. Taught by professors from the School of Arts, this workshop covers:
- Basic brush techniques (bǐfǎ)
- The five major script styles (kǎishū, xíngshū, cǎoshū, lìshū, zhuànshū)
- Historical context of calligraphy in Nanjing

Why expats love it: The course includes field trips to local calligraphy exhibitions and the chance to practice in scenic spots like Xuanwu Lake.

2. The Confucius Temple Calligraphy Studio

Location: Qinhuai District
Duration: Drop-in or 3-day intensive

Nestled near the iconic Confucius Temple, this studio is perfect for expats who want a flexible schedule. Highlights include:
- Hands-on sessions with master calligraphers
- Customized chops (name seals) carved with your Chinese name
- Night markets nearby to practice your skills on red paper (hóngzhǐ)

Pro tip: Pair your workshop with a Qinhuai River cruise to see calligraphy-inspired lantern art.

3. Ink Art Nanjing

Location: Jianye District
Duration: 2-hour private or group workshops

This modern studio blends traditional calligraphy with contemporary art. Expats rave about:
- "Calligraphy & Tea" sessions, pairing lessons with local bìluóchūn tea
- Workshops on writing poetic couplets (duìlián) for Lunar New Year
- Instagram-worthy ink-wash painting add-ons

Bonus: The studio hosts monthly exhibitions featuring local and expat artists.

4. Nanjing Calligraphers’ Association Open Classes

Location: Various cultural centers
Duration: Seasonal (spring/autumn)

For expats seeking authenticity, these classes are taught by award-winning calligraphers. You’ll:
- Learn rare techniques like "flying white" (fēibái)
- Visit the Nanjing Calligraphy Museum as part of the curriculum
- Network with local calligraphy enthusiasts

Note: Mandarin proficiency helps, but translators are often available.

How to Choose the Right Workshop

For Beginners

  • Look for workshops with English-speaking instructors (e.g., Ink Art Nanjing).
  • Opt for shorter sessions (2–3 hours) to test your interest.

For Advanced Learners

  • Seek masterclasses (like those at Nanjing University) focusing on specific scripts.
  • Ask about cultural immersion add-ons, such as museum visits.

Budget-Friendly Options

  • Confucius Temple Studio offers pay-per-session rates.
  • Check community centers for subsidized programs.

Beyond the Workshop: Calligraphy Experiences in Nanjing

1. Calligraphy Souvenirs

Visit Zhongshan Ling’s gift shops for hand-painted scrolls or commission a piece at the Nanjing Art Factory.

2. Festivals & Events

  • Qinhuai Lantern Festival: Try writing wishes on lanterns.
  • Nanjing Cultural Week: Free calligraphy demonstrations.

3. DIY Exploration

  • Practice at Xu Garden’s tranquil pavilions.
  • Buy supplies at Deji Plaza’s art stores (ask for mò, zhǐ, yàn—ink, paper, inkstone).

Final Tips for Expats

  • Respect the art: Calligraphy is deeply revered—avoid tossing practice sheets casually.
  • Dress comfortably: Ink stains are common (and a badge of honor!).
  • Combine with tourism: Schedule workshops near attractions like the Presidential Palace or Porcelain Tower.

From university courses to temple studios, Nanjing’s calligraphy scene welcomes expats with open arms—and brushes. Whether you’re leaving with a framed masterpiece or just a newfound appreciation for shūfǎ, these workshops promise a brushstroke of cultural magic.
