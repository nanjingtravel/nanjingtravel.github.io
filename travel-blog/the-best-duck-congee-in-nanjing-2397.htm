The Best Duck Congee in Nanjing

  • Home
  • Travel Blog
  • Blog Details

Nanjing, the ancient capital of China, is a city steeped in history, culture, and most importantly, incredible food. Among its many culinary treasures, one dish stands out as a must-try for every traveler: duck congee. This humble yet flavorful dish captures the essence of Nanjing’s food scene—simple, comforting, and deeply rooted in tradition.

Why Duck Congee?

Nanjing is famous for its saltwater duck (盐水鸭, yánshuǐ yā), a dish so iconic that it’s often called the city’s "unofficial mascot." But while the duck itself is a star, its byproducts—like rich, savory duck broth—are the secret behind one of the best breakfasts you’ll ever have: duck congee (鸭粥, yā zhōu).

This dish is more than just rice porridge with duck. It’s a slow-cooked masterpiece where the duck’s essence infuses every grain of rice, creating a creamy, umami-packed bowl of comfort. Locals swear by it as a hangover cure, a winter warmer, or just a hearty way to start the day.

The Top Spots for Duck Congee in Nanjing

1. Laomendong’s Hidden Gem: Chen’s Duck Congee

Tucked away in the historic Laomendong district, Chen’s is a no-frills eatery that’s been serving duck congee for over three decades. The recipe? Simmered duck bones, fragrant jasmine rice, and a dash of secret spices.

What to order:
- Classic Duck Congee – Silky smooth with tender shredded duck.
- Spicy Duck Congee – A kick of chili oil for those who like heat.
- Side of Pickled Vegetables – The perfect tangy contrast.

2. Qinhuai River’s Breakfast Staple: Lotus Duck Porridge

Along the scenic Qinhuai River, where tourists flock for boat rides, there’s a tiny stall that locals line up for at dawn. Their duck congee is cooked with lotus root, adding a subtle sweetness and crunch.

Pro tip: Go early—this place sells out by 9 AM.

3. Modern Twist: Duck Congee at Nanjing 1912

For travelers who prefer a contemporary setting, Nanjing 1912—the city’s trendy dining and nightlife hub—has upscale restaurants reinventing duck congee. Think truffle oil, edible gold flakes, or even a foie gras topping.

Best for: Instagram-worthy foodies who still want authenticity.

The Cultural Significance of Duck Congee

Nanjing’s love affair with duck dates back centuries. During the Ming Dynasty, the city’s imperial kitchens perfected duck dishes, and the tradition trickled down to street vendors. Duck congee became a staple for laborers needing a cheap, filling meal.

Today, it’s a symbol of Nanjing’s resilience. After the devastation of WWII, the city rebuilt itself, and duck congee remained a comforting constant. Locals say a bowl tastes like "home."

How to Enjoy Duck Congee Like a Local

  1. Timing is everything – Most places serve it only for breakfast or late-night supper.
  2. Add-ins matter – Try crispy duck skin, scallions, or a drizzle of sesame oil.
  3. Pair with youtiao – Fried dough sticks for dipping.

Beyond the Bowl: Duck-Themed Souvenirs

Love the dish? Take a piece of Nanjing home:
- Vacuum-packed saltwater duck (sold at Zhonghua Gate Market).
- Duck-shaped pastries – Sweet treats from Fuzimiao.
- DIY duck congee kits – Available at specialty shops.

Final Thoughts

Whether you’re a history buff, a food lover, or just someone who appreciates a damn good bowl of porridge, Nanjing’s duck congee is a culinary experience not to be missed. It’s a dish that tells a story—one of tradition, survival, and the simple joy of a well-made meal.
