The Best Duck Tacos in Nanjing’s Luxury Hotels

  • Home
  • Travel Blog
  • Blog Details

Nanjing, a city steeped in history and culinary tradition, is famous for its succulent duck dishes. But what happens when you take the city’s iconic flavors and fuse them with a modern twist? Enter duck tacos—a surprising yet irresistible creation that’s taking Nanjing’s luxury hotel dining scene by storm.

For travelers seeking a gourmet experience that blends local heritage with global flair, these duck tacos are a must-try. From crispy skin to tender meat, paired with bold sauces and fresh toppings, here’s where to find the best duck tacos in Nanjing’s most luxurious hotels.

Why Duck Tacos? A Fusion Worth Trying

Duck has long been a staple in Nanjing cuisine, with dishes like Nanjing salted duck (盐水鸭) and duck blood soup (鸭血粉丝汤) dominating local menus. But chefs in the city’s high-end hotels are reimagining these flavors in unexpected ways.

Tacos, with their versatility and global appeal, provide the perfect canvas for innovation. Imagine: slow-roasted duck confit tucked into a warm tortilla, topped with hoisin-infused crema, pickled vegetables, and a sprinkle of crushed peanuts. It’s a marriage of Mexican street food and Jiangsu province’s finest ingredients—and it works.

Top Luxury Hotels Serving Duck Tacos

1. The Ritz-Carlton, Nanjing – “Jinling Tacos” at Li Xuan

At Li Xuan, the Ritz-Carlton’s signature Chinese restaurant, the chefs have crafted a Jinling-style duck taco that pays homage to Nanjing’s ancient name, Jinling.

  • The Duck: Roasted with a blend of five-spice and honey glaze, then shredded for maximum tenderness.
  • The Tortilla: A delicate, house-made scallion pancake hybrid for a crisp yet pliable bite.
  • The Toppings: A tangy slaw of cabbage and radish, drizzled with a chili-citrus sauce.

Pair it with a Jasmine Margarita—a cocktail that blends tequila with local tea flavors—for the ultimate indulgence.

2. Fairmont Nanjing – “Duck Carnitas” at The Cube

Fairmont’s The Cube offers a playful take on duck tacos, drawing inspiration from Mexican carnitas but using Nanjing’s famed duck instead of pork.

  • The Duck: Braised in a rich stock with star anise and soy, then crisped to perfection.
  • The Tortilla: Blue corn tortillas imported from Mexico, adding an earthy depth.
  • The Toppings: Guacamole with a hint of Sichuan peppercorn, plus a fiery salsa verde.

The dish is part of their “East Meets West” tasting menu, which has become a hit among foodie travelers.

3. InterContinental Nanjing – “Peking Duck Tacos” at Horizon

For a luxurious twist on Peking duck, head to Horizon, the InterContinental’s rooftop lounge. Their version features:

  • The Duck: Thinly sliced, skin-on Peking duck with that signature crackle.
  • The Tortilla: Mini flour tortillas, steamed to softness.
  • The Toppings: Classic hoisin sauce, cucumber, and scallions—but with a sprinkle of crushed candied walnuts for extra crunch.

Enjoy these with a Yuzu Paloma while taking in panoramic views of the Yangtze River.

The Secret Behind the Trend

Why are luxury hotels in Nanjing jumping on the duck taco bandwagon?

  1. Tourist Appeal – International visitors love familiar formats (like tacos) with local twists.
  2. Social Media Buzz – These photogenic dishes are Instagram gold, drawing food influencers.
  3. Chef Creativity – High-end hotel kitchens have the freedom to experiment beyond traditional menus.

Where Else to Find Duck Tacos in Nanjing

While luxury hotels lead the trend, a few standalone restaurants are also catching on:

  • Xinjiekou’s Hidden Gem: A speakeasy-style bar serving duck tacos with black garlic aioli.
  • Qinhuai Night Market: A street vendor’s “Nanjing Duck Burrito”—messy but delicious.

Final Bite

Whether you’re a luxury traveler or a curious food explorer, Nanjing’s duck tacos offer a delicious way to experience the city’s culinary evolution. From high-end hotel dining rooms to trendy rooftop lounges, this fusion dish proves that tradition and innovation can coexist—one bite at a time.

So next time you’re in Nanjing, skip the usual duck dishes (just this once) and let these tacos surprise you. Your taste buds will thank you.
