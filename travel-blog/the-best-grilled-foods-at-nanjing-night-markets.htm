The Best Grilled Foods at Nanjing Night Markets

  • Home
  • Travel Blog
  • Blog Details

Nanjing’s night markets are a sensory explosion—where the sizzle of skewers on hot grills mingles with the chatter of locals and travelers alike. The city’s street food scene is legendary, and grilled foods take center stage. From smoky lamb kebabs to crispy squid tentacles, here’s your ultimate guide to the best-grilled delights you can’t miss.

Must-Try Grilled Skewers

Yang Rou Chuan (Lamb Skewers)

No visit to a Nanjing night market is complete without trying Yang Rou Chuan. These cumin-spiced lamb skewers are a staple, marinated in a mix of chili, cumin, and garlic before hitting the flames. The result? Juicy, charred perfection. Head to Fuzimiao (Confucius Temple) area for some of the best stalls.

Ji Chi (Grilled Chicken Wings)

Crispy on the outside, tender on the inside—Ji Chi are a crowd favorite. Vendors often baste them in a sweet-and-spicy glaze while grilling, creating a sticky, finger-licking experience. Look for stalls with long lines; that’s where the magic happens.

You Mian Jin (Grilled Gluten Skewers)

A vegetarian standout, You Mian Jin is chewy wheat gluten brushed with a savory soy-based sauce and grilled until slightly crispy. It’s a textural wonder and a great option for non-meat eaters.

Seafood on the Grill

Kao You Yu (Grilled Squid)

Massive squid tentacles, scored and grilled to smoky perfection, are a Nanjing night market icon. Served with a drizzle of spicy mayo or a dusting of Sichuan pepper, this is seafood at its most addictive.

Kao Sheng Hao (Grilled Oysters)

Fresh oysters grilled with garlic, chili, and scallions are a luxurious treat. The briny sweetness of the oysters pairs perfectly with the bold toppings. Find them at Shiziqiao night market for the freshest picks.

Unique Grilled Specialties

Kao Niu Rou (Grilled Beef Ribs)

For meat lovers, Kao Niu Rou offers thick, marbled beef ribs slow-grilled over charcoal. The meat falls off the bone, and the marinade—often a secret family recipe—is worth savoring.

Kao Bao Yu (Grilled Stuffed Buns)

A lesser-known gem, these buns are stuffed with minced pork or red bean paste, then grilled until the outside is crispy and the inside molten. Perfect for a quick, handheld snack.

Pro Tips for Night Market Grilling

  • Follow the Smoke: The best stalls are usually the ones billowing the most smoke—it means high turnover and fresh ingredients.
  • Spice Levels: Ask for wei la (mild), zhong la (medium), or te la (extra spicy) to customize your heat preference.
  • Cash is King: While some vendors accept mobile payments, smaller stalls prefer cash for speed.

Nanjing’s night markets are a grill master’s paradise. Whether you’re a carnivore, seafood enthusiast, or veggie lover, the smoky, savory flavors will keep you coming back for more.
