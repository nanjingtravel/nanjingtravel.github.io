The Best Pizza and Pasta in Nanjing

  • Home
  • Travel Blog
  • Blog Details

Nanjing, a city steeped in history and culture, is also home to a thriving food scene. While local delicacies like salted duck and tangbao (soup dumplings) often steal the spotlight, the city’s Italian cuisine has been gaining serious traction. Whether you’re a traveler craving a taste of home or a local looking to explore global flavors, Nanjing’s pizza and pasta offerings won’t disappoint. Here’s a curated list of the best spots to indulge in authentic Italian fare.

Top Pizza Spots in Nanjing

1. La Mia Casa: The Gold Standard

Tucked away in the bustling Gulou district, La Mia Casa is a favorite among expats and locals alike. Their wood-fired pizzas are crispy on the outside, chewy on the inside, and topped with high-quality ingredients imported straight from Italy.

Must-Try: The Diavola—spicy salami, mozzarella, and a hint of chili oil for an extra kick. Pair it with a glass of Chianti for the full experience.

2. Bella Napoli: A Slice of Naples

If you’re after authentic Neapolitan pizza, Bella Napoli is the place to be. The chef, a Naples native, follows traditional techniques, including a 48-hour dough fermentation process. The result? A light, airy crust with a perfect char.

Pro Tip: Try their Margherita DOP, made with San Marzano tomatoes and buffalo mozzarella. It’s simplicity at its finest.

3. Pizza Marzano: Modern Twists

For those who enjoy creative toppings, Pizza Marzano delivers. This international chain has a strong presence in Nanjing, offering gourmet pizzas with unexpected combinations like truffle oil and prosciutto.

Hidden Gem: Their Truffle Mushroom Pizza is a umami bomb that keeps customers coming back.

Unmissable Pasta Dishes

1. Mercato Piccolo: Handmade Perfection

Located near Confucius Temple, Mercato Piccolo is a cozy trattoria specializing in fresh, handmade pasta. Their Tagliatelle al Ragu is slow-cooked for hours, ensuring a rich, meaty sauce that clings perfectly to the noodles.

Bonus: The intimate setting makes it ideal for a romantic dinner.

2. Va Bene: Classic & Contemporary

Va Bene strikes a balance between traditional recipes and modern flair. Their Cacio e Pepe is a masterclass in simplicity—just pecorino cheese, black pepper, and al dente spaghetti.

For Adventurous Eaters: The Squid Ink Linguine is a bold choice, packed with seafood flavors and a striking black hue.

3. La Dolce Vita: A Taste of Rome

This upscale eatery in Xinjiekou brings Roman classics to Nanjing. Their Carbonara uses guanciale (cured pork cheek) instead of bacon, staying true to its roots.

Wine Pairing: Ask for a glass of Montepulciano d’Abruzzo to complement the dish’s richness.

Why Italian Food Thrives in Nanjing

Nanjing’s cosmopolitan vibe has paved the way for diverse culinary experiences. The city’s growing expat community and increasing interest in global cuisines have made Italian food a staple. Many restaurants source ingredients directly from Italy, ensuring authenticity, while others put a local spin on classics—think mapo tofu pasta (yes, it exists!).

Final Bites: Where to Go Next

If you’re planning a food tour, pair your pizza and pasta crawl with visits to nearby attractions like Xuanwu Lake or the Nanjing Museum. Many of these restaurants are just a short walk from major landmarks, making them perfect pit stops during sightseeing.

Whether you’re a die-hard foodie or just looking for a comforting meal, Nanjing’s Italian scene has something for everyone. Buon appetito!
