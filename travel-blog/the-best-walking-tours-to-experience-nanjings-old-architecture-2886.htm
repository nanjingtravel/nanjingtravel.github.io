The Best Walking Tours to Experience Nanjing’s Old Architecture

  • Home
  • Travel Blog
  • Blog Details

Nanjing, one of China’s ancient capitals, is a treasure trove of historical architecture. From Ming Dynasty relics to Republican-era buildings, the city offers a fascinating blend of old and new. Walking tours are the perfect way to immerse yourself in Nanjing’s rich heritage, allowing you to explore hidden alleys, grand palaces, and charming European-style villas at a leisurely pace. Here are some of the best walking routes to experience Nanjing’s old architecture.

The Ming Dynasty Legacy: A Stroll Through Time

Start at the Ming Xiaoling Mausoleum

Begin your journey at the Ming Xiaoling Mausoleum, the final resting place of the Hongwu Emperor, founder of the Ming Dynasty. This UNESCO World Heritage Site is a masterpiece of imperial tomb architecture, featuring the famous Sacred Way lined with stone statues of mythical creatures and officials. The grandeur of the mausoleum sets the tone for the rest of your walk through Nanjing’s Ming-era landmarks.

Explore the City Wall and Zhonghua Gate

From the mausoleum, head toward the Nanjing City Wall, one of the longest ancient city walls in the world. A walk along its ramparts offers stunning views of the city and the Qinhuai River. Make sure to stop at Zhonghua Gate (Zhonghua Men), the largest and best-preserved gate in the wall. Its intricate defensive structures, including a triple-arched entrance and hidden chambers, showcase Ming military engineering at its finest.

End at the Confucius Temple (Fuzimiao)

Finish your Ming-themed walk at the Confucius Temple area, a lively district along the Qinhuai River. While the temple itself has been rebuilt multiple times, the surrounding architecture retains a traditional charm. Stroll through the lantern-lit streets, admire the classical Chinese pavilions, and perhaps take a boat ride to see the riverbanks lined with historic buildings.

The Republican Era: Nanjing’s European-Inspired Gems

Begin at Presidential Palace

Nanjing served as the capital during the Republican era (1912–1949), leaving behind a wealth of Western-influenced architecture. Start at the Presidential Palace, a sprawling complex blending Chinese and European styles. Once the office of Sun Yat-sen and Chiang Kai-shek, the palace features grand halls, lush gardens, and a mix of Baroque and traditional Chinese elements.

Walk Along Yihe Road’s Historic Villas

Next, head to Yihe Road, often called "Nanjing’s Architectural Museum." This tree-lined avenue is dotted with early 20th-century villas built by foreign diplomats and wealthy Chinese. The eclectic mix of Gothic, Renaissance, and Art Deco styles makes it a photographer’s paradise. Don’t miss No. 15 Yihe Road, a stunning French-style mansion that once housed high-ranking officials.

Visit the Nanjing Museum’s Old Buildings

Wrap up your Republican-era tour at the Nanjing Museum, where the main hall is a striking example of Sino-Western architecture. Designed by renowned architect Liang Sicheng, the building combines traditional Chinese roof lines with modern materials. The museum’s extensive collection also offers deeper insights into Nanjing’s historical transformation.

Hidden Alleys and Local Life: A Walk Through Laomendong

Start at the Zhonghua Gate Area

For a more local experience, explore Laomendong, a revitalized historic district near Zhonghua Gate. This area retains the charm of old Nanjing with its narrow alleys, gray-brick houses, and traditional courtyard homes. Start your walk at the gate and meander through the labyrinth of lanes, where artisans sell handmade crafts and street vendors offer local snacks like salted duck and tanghulu.

Discover the Ningbo Guild Hall

One of Laomendong’s hidden gems is the Ningbo Guild Hall, a beautifully restored Qing Dynasty building. Once a meeting place for merchants from Ningbo, the hall now hosts cultural exhibitions and tea ceremonies. Its intricate wood carvings and tranquil courtyard provide a peaceful escape from the bustling streets.

End with a Riverside Walk Along the Qinhuai

Conclude your tour with a stroll along the Qinhuai River, where old teahouses and opera stages evoke the city’s literary and artistic past. As the sun sets, the area comes alive with neon lights, blending history with modern energy.

Tips for Your Walking Tour

  • Wear comfortable shoes: Nanjing’s historic sites involve a lot of walking, often on uneven surfaces.
  • Visit early or late: Avoid crowds by exploring popular spots like the Ming Xiaoling Mausoleum in the early morning or late afternoon.
  • Try local snacks: Fuel your walk with Nanjing specialties like duck blood soup and sesame pancakes.
  • Hire a guide: Consider a local guide for deeper insights into the architecture and history.

Nanjing’s old architecture tells stories of emperors, revolutionaries, and everyday life across centuries. Whether you’re a history buff or just love beautiful buildings, these walking tours offer unforgettable glimpses into the city’s past.
