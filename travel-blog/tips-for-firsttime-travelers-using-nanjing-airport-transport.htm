Tips for First-Time Travelers Using Nanjing Airport Transport

  • Home
  • Travel Blog
  • Blog Details

Nanjing Lukou International Airport (NKG) is the primary gateway to Jiangsu Province and a bustling hub for both domestic and international travelers. Whether you're visiting for business or leisure, navigating airport transport can be overwhelming, especially if it's your first time. Here’s a comprehensive guide to help you move seamlessly from the airport to your destination.

Getting to Know Nanjing Lukou International Airport

Nanjing Airport is located about 35 kilometers south of the city center, making it a relatively quick trip if you choose the right transport option. The airport has two terminals: Terminal 1 for domestic flights and Terminal 2 for international and some domestic routes. Free shuttle buses run between the terminals, so don’t worry if you land in the wrong one.

Terminal Layout and Amenities

Before diving into transport options, take a moment to familiarize yourself with the airport’s layout. Both terminals offer:
- Currency exchange and ATMs
- Free Wi-Fi (register with your passport)
- Luggage storage for short layovers
- Dining options ranging from local Nanjing cuisine to international fast food

Best Transport Options from Nanjing Airport

1. Airport Metro (S1 Line)

The most efficient and budget-friendly way to reach downtown Nanjing is via the S1 Metro Line. Here’s what you need to know:
- Frequency: Trains run every 8–10 minutes from 6:00 AM to 10:00 PM.
- Travel Time: Approximately 35–40 minutes to Nanjing South Railway Station.
- Cost: Around ¥7–10 (less than $2).
- Pro Tip: If your hotel is near Xinjiekou or Gulou, transfer to Metro Line 1 or 3 at Nanjing South Station.

2. Airport Shuttle Buses

For those with heavy luggage or traveling in groups, shuttle buses are a comfortable alternative. Key routes include:
- Line 1: To Nanjing Railway Station (¥20, ~1 hour).
- Line 2: To Zhonghuamen (¥20, ~50 minutes).
- Night Buses: Available for late arrivals (¥25, limited routes).

3. Taxis and Ride-Hailing Apps

Taxis are readily available outside both terminals. Key details:
- Fare: Around ¥120–150 ($17–22) to the city center, depending on traffic.
- Ride-Hailing: Didi (China’s Uber) is cheaper but requires a local SIM card or roaming data.
- Scam Alert: Always insist on using the meter and avoid unlicensed cabs.

4. Private Transfers and Car Rentals

For a stress-free arrival, pre-book a private transfer through your hotel or platforms like Klook. Prices start at ¥200 ($30) for a sedan. Car rentals are also available at the airport, but driving in Nanjing’s traffic isn’t for the faint-hearted.

Navigating Public Transport in Nanjing

Once you’re in the city, public transport is your best friend. Here’s a quick breakdown:

Metro System

Nanjing’s metro is clean, efficient, and covers most tourist spots:
- Line 1: Connects Nanjing Station to Confucius Temple.
- Line 2: Links to Zhongshan Ling (Sun Yat-sen Mausoleum).
- Line 3: Stops at Presidential Palace and Nanjing Museum.

Buses and Trams

Buses are cheaper but slower due to traffic. Trams (e.g., S8 Line) are great for exploring suburban areas like Gaochun.

Must-Visit Attractions Near Transport Hubs

1. Confucius Temple (Fuzimiao)

Accessible via Metro Line 3, this historic area is perfect for souvenir shopping and trying Nanjing’s famous salted duck.

2. Xuanwu Lake

A short walk from Nanjing Station, this scenic lake offers paddle boating and cherry blossoms in spring.

3. Zhongshan Mountain Scenic Area

Take Metro Line 2 to Muxuyuan Station, then hop on a shuttle bus to explore Sun Yat-sen’s Mausoleum and the Ming Xiaoling Tomb.

Money-Saving Tips for Airport Transfers

  • Buy a Transportation Card: The Jinlingtong Card offers discounts on metro and bus fares.
  • Shared Rides: Split a Didi with fellow travelers to cut costs.
  • Avoid Peak Hours: Metro and roads get crowded during rush hours (7:30–9:30 AM and 5:00–7:00 PM).

Language and Cultural Tips

While many signs are in English, learning a few Mandarin phrases can help:
- “Qù nǎlǐ?” (Where are you going?) – Useful for taxis.
- “Duōshǎo qián?” (How much?) – Handy for negotiating fares.

Final Safety Reminders

  • Keep Valuables Secure: Pickpocketing is rare but possible in crowded areas.
  • Have Your Hotel’s Address in Chinese: Show it to taxi drivers to avoid confusion.
  • Check Flight Updates: NKG is modern, but delays happen during holidays like Golden Week.

With these tips, your first trip through Nanjing Airport will be smooth and stress-free. Enjoy your adventure in this ancient capital!
