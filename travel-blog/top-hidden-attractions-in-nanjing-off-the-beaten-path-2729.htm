Top Hidden Attractions in Nanjing Off the Beaten Path

  • Home
  • Travel Blog
  • Blog Details

Nanjing, the ancient capital of China, is often overshadowed by its flashier siblings like Beijing and Shanghai. Yet, this city is a treasure trove of history, culture, and natural beauty—especially if you know where to look. While the Sun Yat-sen Mausoleum and Confucius Temple are must-sees, the real magic lies in the lesser-known spots. Here’s your guide to Nanjing’s hidden gems that most tourists miss.

The Forgotten Ruins: Ming Dynasty’s Secret Corners

1. The Abandoned Ming Palace (Ming Gugong)

Most visitors flock to the well-preserved Ming Xiaoling Mausoleum, but few venture to the ruins of the Ming Palace, the original seat of power before the Forbidden City in Beijing was built. Overgrown with weeds and scattered with broken stone carvings, this site offers a hauntingly beautiful glimpse into the past. Wander through the remnants of grand halls and imagine the dynasty’s former glory.

2. Dongshuiguan Water Park

Tucked away near the city center, this park was once part of Nanjing’s ancient water defense system. The crumbling stone gates and quiet canals make it a perfect spot for photographers and history buffs. Visit at sunrise for an ethereal experience.

Hidden Temples & Spiritual Retreats

3. Jinghai Temple (Jinghai Si)

Unlike the crowded Jiming Temple, Jinghai Temple remains a serene escape. Built during the Ming Dynasty, it’s one of the few temples that survived the Taiping Rebellion. The intricate wood carvings and peaceful courtyard are ideal for meditation.

4. Wuchaomen Christian Church

A bizarre yet fascinating blend of Chinese and Gothic architecture, this abandoned church near Zhonghua Gate is a relic of Nanjing’s colonial past. Its decaying walls and stained-glass fragments tell stories of a bygone era.

Nature’s Best-Kept Secrets

5. Linggu Temple Bamboo Forest

While Purple Mountain draws crowds, the Linggu Temple Bamboo Forest nearby is a tranquil alternative. The towering bamboo groves create a natural tunnel, perfect for a quiet stroll or a photoshoot.

6. Qixia Mountain’s Hidden Waterfall

Most hikers stop at Qixia Temple, but if you trek further up the mountain, you’ll find a secluded waterfall—especially stunning after rainfall. Locals call it "Xianren Pu" (Fairy Falls), and it’s worth the extra climb.

Quirky & Offbeat Spots

7. Nanjing’s Underground City (Dixia Cheng)

A Cold War-era bomb shelter turned tourist oddity, this labyrinth of tunnels stretches beneath the city. Only a small section is open to the public, but its damp corridors and propaganda posters are eerily fascinating.

8. The "Ghost Street" of Shiziqiao

By day, Shiziqiao is a normal alley. By night, it transforms into a hub for midnight noodles and street food. Locals swear by the late-night dumpling stalls—just don’t ask about the alley’s ghost stories unless you want chills with your chili oil.

Local Hangouts & Creative Spaces

9. 1865 Creative Industrial Park

A repurposed 19th-century weapons factory, this artsy complex now houses indie galleries, cafés, and design studios. The mix of industrial grit and modern creativity makes it a hotspot for Nanjing’s hipsters.

10. Xiaguan Railway Park

An abandoned railway line turned public park, this spot is a favorite for urban explorers. The rusted tracks and vintage train cars are Instagram gold, especially during golden hour.

Foodie Secrets: Where the Locals Eat

11. Sanshan Street’s Stinky Tofu Master

Forget fancy restaurants—Nanjing’s best chou doufu (stinky tofu) comes from a tiny stall near Sanshan Street. The owner, "Lao Zhang," has been frying tofu for 30 years, and his secret sauce is legendary.

12. Hidden Hotpot in a Back Alley

Ask a taxi driver for "lao Ma’s hotpot," and they’ll drop you at an unmarked door in a residential alley. The spicy broth and fresh ingredients are worth the hunt.

Final Tips for Exploring Nanjing’s Hidden Side

  • Rent a bike: Many of these spots are easier to reach on two wheels.
  • Talk to locals: Nanjing’s elderly residents love sharing stories about the city’s secrets.
  • Visit in autumn: The crowds thin, and the fall foliage makes hidden spots even more magical.

Now, ditch the guidebook and uncover the Nanjing that few travelers ever see.
