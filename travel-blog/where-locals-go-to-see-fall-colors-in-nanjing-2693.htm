Where Locals Go to See Fall Colors in Nanjing

  • Home
  • Travel Blog
  • Blog Details

When autumn arrives in Nanjing, the city transforms into a canvas of fiery reds, golden yellows, and deep oranges. While tourists flock to well-known spots like the Sun Yat-sen Mausoleum or Xuanwu Lake, locals have their own secret havens for soaking in the season’s beauty. If you’re looking to escape the crowds and experience fall like a true Nanjing native, here’s where you should go.

Hidden Gems for Autumn Foliage

Qixia Mountain (栖霞山)

No list of Nanjing’s fall destinations is complete without mentioning Qixia Mountain. While it’s not exactly a “hidden” spot, locals know the best times and trails to avoid the weekend rush. The mountain is famous for its maple trees, which turn a brilliant crimson by late November.

Pro Tip: Visit on a weekday morning and take the less-traveled eastern path. You’ll find quieter spots with equally stunning views.

Purple Mountain’s Lesser-Known Trails

Purple Mountain (Zijin Shan) is a year-round favorite, but in autumn, its lesser-known trails become magical. Locals often hike the paths near Linggu Temple or the secluded areas around the Ming Xiaoling Mausoleum. The ginkgo trees here create a golden carpet, perfect for photos.

Best Time to Go: Mid-to-late November, when the ginkgo leaves are at their peak.

Mochou Lake Park

Mochou Lake Park is a local favorite for its serene atmosphere and vibrant fall foliage. The park’s willow trees, maples, and sweet osmanthus create a fragrant, colorful escape right in the city center.

Don’t Miss: The lakeside teahouses, where you can enjoy a warm cup of tea while watching the leaves drift onto the water.

Off-the-Beaten-Path Spots

Jiangning Tangshan Hot Springs Area

For a unique fall experience, locals head to the Tangshan Hot Springs area in Jiangning. The surrounding hills are dotted with persimmon trees and maples, and after a hike, you can relax in one of the area’s famed hot springs.

Why Locals Love It: The combination of warm springs and cool autumn air is unbeatable.

Lao Shan National Forest Park

If you’re willing to venture a bit farther, Lao Shan National Forest Park offers some of the most spectacular fall scenery near Nanjing. The park’s dense forests turn into a mosaic of red, orange, and yellow, with few tourists in sight.

Local Secret: Pack a picnic and hike to one of the park’s quieter ridges for an uninterrupted view of the foliage.

Urban Oases for Fall Colors

Nanjing University’s Gulou Campus

Even in the heart of the city, you can find stunning fall colors. Nanjing University’s Gulou Campus is a local favorite for its historic buildings framed by golden ginkgo trees. Students and photographers alike flock here in November.

Photography Tip: Early mornings on weekends are surprisingly peaceful.

Confucius Temple Qinhuai Scenic Area

While the Confucius Temple area is busy year-round, the tree-lined Qinhuai River takes on a special charm in autumn. Locals love strolling along the riverbanks as the leaves change, especially in the evening when the lights reflect off the water.

Bonus: Stop by a street vendor for roasted sweet potatoes—a classic Nanjing fall snack.

Seasonal Delights to Pair with Your Foliage Tour

No autumn trip in Nanjing is complete without indulging in seasonal treats. Locals often combine leaf-peeping with food adventures.

Must-Try Autumn Foods

  • Hairy Crab (大闸蟹): A Nanjing delicacy in fall, best enjoyed with ginger and vinegar.
  • Osmanthus Cake (桂花糕): Sweet and fragrant, perfect with tea.
  • Persimmons: Plucked fresh from local trees in Tangshan.

Where to Eat: Head to a local nongjia le (farmhouse restaurant) near Qixia Mountain for a rustic meal with a view.

Practical Tips for Your Fall Adventure

  • Timing Matters: Peak foliage varies yearly but usually hits its stride from late October to late November.
  • Dress in Layers: Mornings and evenings can be chilly, while afternoons are warm.
  • Public Transport Wins: Traffic around popular spots can be heavy; opt for the metro or buses.

Whether you’re hiking a quiet trail or sipping tea by a lake, Nanjing’s autumn beauty is best enjoyed the way locals do—slowly and savoringly. So grab your camera, pack some snacks, and get ready to fall in love with the city’s seasonal splendor.
