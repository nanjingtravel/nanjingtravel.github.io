Where to Drink with a View in Nanjing

  • Home
  • Travel Blog
  • Blog Details

Nanjing, a city steeped in history and modern charm, offers some of the most breathtaking backdrops for sipping your favorite drink. Whether you're a traveler seeking a rooftop cocktail or a local looking for a serene spot by the water, this guide will take you through the best places to enjoy a beverage with a view.

The Best Rooftop Bars in Nanjing

1. Flair Rooftop Bar

Perched atop The Ritz-Carlton, Flair offers panoramic views of the Yangtze River and the city skyline. The modern, sleek design pairs perfectly with their signature cocktails like the "Nanjing Mule," a twist on the classic Moscow Mule infused with local flavors. Sunset here is magical—grab a seat by the edge and watch the city lights flicker to life.

2. Cloud 9 at The InterContinental

Located on the 78th floor, Cloud 9 is one of the highest bars in Nanjing. The floor-to-ceiling windows provide an unobstructed view of the sprawling urban landscape. Try their "Purple Mountain Sour," a nod to Nanjing’s iconic Purple Mountain, made with gin, lavender, and citrus.

3. Sky Lounge at The Shangri-La

For a more intimate setting, Sky Lounge offers cozy seating and a curated wine list. The terrace overlooks Xuanwu Lake, making it a prime spot for a romantic evening. Their "Xuanwu Breeze" cocktail, featuring lychee and prosecco, is a crowd favorite.

Lakeside Lounges and Waterfront Cafés

1. The Xuanwu Lake Teahouse

If you prefer tea over cocktails, this traditional teahouse by Xuanwu Lake is a must-visit. Sip on locally sourced Biluochun while watching boats glide across the water. The wooden pavilions and lotus ponds add to the tranquil ambiance.

2. Qinhuai River Bar Street

Along the historic Qinhuai River, you’ll find a string of bars and cafés with outdoor seating. "The Riverside" is a standout, offering craft beers and live music. The view of the illuminated ancient architecture reflecting on the water is unforgettable.

3. Mochou Lake Café

Tucked away near Mochou Lake, this café is perfect for a quiet afternoon. Their honey lemon tea pairs beautifully with the view of willow trees swaying by the lake. It’s a favorite among photographers and writers seeking inspiration.

Hidden Gems with Unique Views

1. The Secret Garden Rooftop

A lesser-known spot in the Xinjiekou district, this rooftop bar is adorned with fairy lights and lush greenery. The "Nanjing Night" cocktail, made with baijiu and elderflower, is a bold choice for adventurous drinkers.

2. Zijin Mountain View Café

Nestled halfway up Zijin Mountain, this café offers sweeping views of the city below. Hike up in the afternoon and reward yourself with their famous "Mountain Fog" latte—a creamy, spiced coffee that warms you up on cooler days.

3. The Old Wall Bar

Built into a section of the ancient city wall, this bar combines history with modern mixology. Try their "Dynasty Old Fashioned," which uses aged whiskey and a hint of osmanthus syrup. The view of the wall lit up at night is awe-inspiring.

Trendy Spots for the Instagram Crowd

1. The Neon Lounge

This futuristic bar in the Hexi district is all about vibrant lights and creative cocktails. The "Electric Nanjing" drink, served in a glowing cube, is as photogenic as the skyline view from their floor-to-ceiling windows.

2. Bubble Tea with a View at Deji Plaza

For something casual, head to the top floor of Deji Plaza. The bubble tea shop here offers a stunning vantage point of the city. Their matcha milk tea with pearls is a must-try.

3. The Bookworm Café

Part café, part bookstore, this spot in Gulou district has a charming rooftop where you can enjoy a latte while flipping through a novel. The view of the bustling streets below adds to the bohemian vibe.

Seasonal Highlights

1. Cherry Blossom Season at Jiming Temple

During spring, the area around Jiming Temple is awash in pink. Nearby cafés like "Sakura Sips" offer cherry blossom-themed drinks, best enjoyed under the falling petals.

2. Autumn at Linggu Temple

The golden ginkgo trees make Linggu Temple a picturesque spot for a warm cup of spiced cider. The temple’s peaceful atmosphere enhances the experience.

3. Winter at Confucius Temple

The Qinhuai River area transforms into a winter wonderland during the Lantern Festival. Hot mulled wine from the street vendors pairs perfectly with the festive lights.

Practical Tips for Drinking with a View in Nanjing

  • Timing is key: Sunset spots like Flair and Cloud 9 get crowded, so arrive early.
  • Dress for the weather: Rooftop bars can be windy, and lakeside cafés chilly in the evenings.
  • Try local flavors: Many bars incorporate Nanjing specialties like salted duck or osmanthus into their drinks.
  • Reservations: High-end lounges often require bookings, especially on weekends.

From sky-high cocktails to serene tea by the lake, Nanjing’s drinking spots are as diverse as its history. Whether you’re a visitor or a local, these venues promise unforgettable sips with even better views.
