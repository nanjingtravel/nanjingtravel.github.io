Where to Find Vegetarian-Friendly Bubble Tea in Nanjing

  • Home
  • Travel Blog
  • Blog Details

Nanjing, a city rich in history and culture, is also a paradise for food lovers. Among its many culinary delights, bubble tea stands out as a favorite among locals and tourists alike. For vegetarians, finding the perfect cup of bubble tea can be a bit tricky, as many shops use non-vegetarian ingredients like gelatin or dairy-based products. But fear not—Nanjing has plenty of vegetarian-friendly bubble tea options that cater to your dietary preferences. Here’s your ultimate guide to the best spots in town.

Why Bubble Tea is a Must-Try in Nanjing

Bubble tea, or boba, has taken the world by storm, and Nanjing is no exception. The city’s vibrant street food scene and youthful energy make it the perfect place to enjoy this Taiwanese import. Whether you’re strolling along Confucius Temple or exploring the trendy Xinjiekou district, you’ll find bubble tea shops at every corner.

For vegetarians, the key is to look for shops that offer:
- Plant-based milk alternatives (soy, almond, oat)
- Tapioca pearls made without gelatin
- Fruit-based teas without honey or other animal-derived sweeteners

Top Vegetarian-Friendly Bubble Tea Shops in Nanjing

1. Heytea (喜茶)

Heytea is a popular chain known for its high-quality ingredients and innovative flavors. While not exclusively vegetarian, they offer several vegan-friendly options.

Must-Try Drinks:
- Cheese Tea (with plant-based cheese foam) – A savory-sweet delight.
- Grapefruit Green Tea – Refreshing and dairy-free.

Location: Multiple outlets, including Deji Plaza and Ist Mall.

2. Lelecha (乐乐茶)

Lelecha is another crowd favorite, famous for its fresh fruit teas and creamy textures. They’ve recently expanded their menu to include more plant-based choices.

Must-Try Drinks:
- Mango Coconut Milk Tea – Made with coconut milk instead of dairy.
- Strawberry Oolong Tea – A fruity, caffeine-free option.

Location: Found in major shopping districts like Hexi and Gulou.

3. Nayuki (奈雪の茶)

Nayuki combines bubble tea with artisanal bakery items, making it a great stop for a quick snack. Their tea selections are top-notch, and many are vegetarian by default.

Must-Try Drinks:
- Avocado Matcha Latte – Creamy and packed with antioxidants.
- Lychee Rose Tea – A floral, lightly sweetened choice.

Location: Popular spots include Rainbow Mall and Kingmo.

4. Coco Fresh Tea & Juice (都可)

Coco is a global brand with a strong presence in Nanjing. They’re transparent about their ingredients, making it easier for vegetarians to customize their orders.

Must-Try Drinks:
- Taro Milk Tea (with soy milk) – A classic with a veg twist.
- Passionfruit Green Tea – Tart, tangy, and completely plant-based.

Location: Nearly every commercial area, including Nanjing University’s vicinity.

Hidden Gems for Vegetarian Bubble Tea Lovers

Small Local Shops with Big Flavors

While chains dominate the scene, smaller independent shops often offer unique vegetarian options. Look for places near universities like Nanjing Normal University, where student demand drives innovation.

Pro Tip: Always ask if their pearls are gelatin-free—some smaller shops make them with agar-agar instead!

How to Customize Your Order for a Vegetarian Diet

Even if a shop isn’t explicitly vegetarian-friendly, you can often modify your drink:
- Swap dairy milk for soy, almond, or oat milk.
- Skip honey and opt for sugar or syrup.
- Avoid toppings like pudding or jelly unless confirmed vegetarian.

Pairing Your Bubble Tea with Nanjing’s Vegetarian Street Food

No bubble tea experience is complete without a snack! Nanjing’s vegetarian street food scene is thriving. Try:
- Vegetarian Tanghulu – Candied fruit skewers, a perfect sweet companion.
- Tofu Skewers – Savory and satisfying.

Final Thoughts

Nanjing’s bubble tea culture is evolving, with more shops recognizing the demand for vegetarian options. Whether you’re a strict vegan or just looking for healthier alternatives, the city has something for everyone. So next time you’re exploring Nanjing’s historic sites or modern malls, don’t forget to grab a refreshing, cruelty-free bubble tea along the way!
