Xuanwu Lake: Nanjing’s Best Lakeside Walks

  • Home
  • Travel Blog
  • Blog Details

Nanjing, a city steeped in history and natural beauty, offers one of its most serene escapes right in the heart of the urban landscape: Xuanwu Lake. This sprawling freshwater lake, covering approximately 444 hectares, is a haven for locals and tourists alike. Whether you're looking for a leisurely stroll, a scenic bike ride, or a deep dive into the area’s rich cultural heritage, Xuanwu Lake delivers an unforgettable experience.

Why Xuanwu Lake is a Must-Visit

Xuanwu Lake isn’t just another body of water—it’s a cultural and recreational hub. Surrounded by lush greenery, ancient temples, and modern amenities, the lake seamlessly blends tradition with contemporary leisure. Here’s why it stands out:

  • Historical Significance: Named after the Black Tortoise (Xuanwu) of Chinese mythology, the lake has been a focal point of Nanjing for over 1,500 years.
  • Scenic Diversity: Five interconnected islands (Huanzhou, Yingzhou, Liangzhou, Cuizhou, and Lingzhou) offer distinct landscapes, from cherry blossoms in spring to golden ginkgo trees in autumn.
  • Accessibility: Located just outside Nanjing’s city wall, it’s easily reachable by metro (Line 1 or 4) or a short taxi ride from downtown.

The Best Lakeside Walks Around Xuanwu Lake

1. The Classic Lakeside Loop (5.5 km)

The most popular route circles the entire lake, offering uninterrupted views of the water, skyline, and distant Purple Mountain. This flat, paved path is perfect for walking, jogging, or cycling. Highlights include:

  • Ming City Wall Scenic Section: Walk alongside the 600-year-old Ming Dynasty wall, where history and nature collide.
  • Nine Peaks Park: A quieter stretch with shaded benches, ideal for a mid-walk break.
  • Lotus Flower Viewing Areas: In summer, the lake’s northern shores burst with vibrant lotus blooms.

2. Island-Hopping Adventure

For a more immersive experience, explore the lake’s five islands via charming arched bridges:

Huanzhou Island

The largest island, home to the Xuanwu Lake Park, features pavilions, tea houses, and the iconic Lingxiao Pagoda. Climb to the top for panoramic lake views.

Yingzhou Island

A birdwatcher’s paradise, especially in winter when migratory birds like egrets and cormorants visit.

Cuizhou Island

Famous for its cherry blossoms in March and April, this island transforms into a pink wonderland.

3. Sunset Promenade (Liangzhou Island to Lingzhou Island)

For golden-hour magic, follow the western shoreline from Liangzhou to Lingzhou Island. The Golden Sunset Pavilion offers one of Nanjing’s most Instagrammable spots, with the sun dipping behind Purple Mountain.

Seasonal Highlights

Spring (March-May)

  • Cherry blossoms on Cuizhou Island.
  • Mild temperatures perfect for picnics.

Summer (June-August)

  • Lotus flowers in full bloom.
  • Evening boat rentals to escape the heat.

Autumn (September-November)

  • Ginkgo trees lining the paths turn brilliant yellow.
  • Cooler weather ideal for long walks.

Winter (December-February)

  • Frost-kissed landscapes and fewer crowds.
  • Hot tea at lakeside cafés.

Hidden Gems & Local Tips

  • Early Mornings: Arrive at 6 AM to join locals practicing tai chi or flying kites.
  • Bike Rentals: Stations around the lake offer affordable rentals (¥20/hour).
  • Street Food: Try tanghulu (candied fruit) or choudoufu (stinky tofu) from vendors near the south gate.

Nearby Attractions

Extend your day with these nearby spots:
- Jiming Temple: A 1,700-year-old Buddhist temple just north of the lake.
- Nanjing Museum: A short taxi ride away, showcasing China’s imperial past.

Xuanwu Lake is more than a scenic retreat—it’s a living postcard of Nanjing’s soul. Whether you’re a history buff, nature lover, or simply seeking tranquility, every step here tells a story.
